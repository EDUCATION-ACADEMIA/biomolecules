MECHANISM OF ENZYME ACTION

[SOURCES]
White bread contains starch. What is the color change when you drop iodine solution into white bread?

(A) ** brown to blue-black

(B) blue-black to brown

(C) remains brown

(D) remians blue-black

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Using an iodine solution, you can test for the presence of starch. When starch is present, the iodine changes from brown to blue-black or purple.

Concept note-2: -Amylose in starch is responsible for the formation of a deep blue color in the presence of iodine. The iodine molecule slips inside of the amylose coil.

Concept note-3: -On adding the iodine solution to starch, the two react to produce a blue-black color.

Concept note-4: -Iodine is a common indicator to test for the presence of starch. The light orange-brown colour Iodine solution turns blue-black in colour when it reacts with starch. This indicates the presence of carbohydrates in the food item to which iodine solution was added.

Concept note-5: -Iodine-KI Reagent: Iodine does not completely soluble in liquids, so the iodine reagent is formulated in the presence of potassium iodide by breaking down iodine in liquids. This creates the complex of a linear triiodide ion soluble, which slides into the starch coil, creating a vivid blue-black appearance.
