MECHANISM OF ENZYME ACTION

[SOURCES]
Enzymes possess active site, so that

(A) its shape is globular

(B) it can lowering the activation energy

(C) ** substrate can bind

(D) chemical reaction is faster

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The part of the enzyme where the substrate binds is called the active site (since that’s where the catalytic “action” happens). A substrate enters the active site of the enzyme. This forms the enzyme-substrate complex.

Concept note-2: -Enzymes bind substrates at key locations in their structure called active sites. They are typically highly specific and only bind certain substrates for certain reactions.

Concept note-3: -The enzyme’s active site binds to the substrate. Since enzymes are proteins, this site is composed of a unique combination of amino acid residues (side chains or R groups).

Concept note-4: -Many enzymes including serine protease, cysteine protease, protein kinase and phosphatase evolved to form transient covalent bonds between them and their substrates to lower the activation energy and allow the reaction to occur. This process can be divided into 2 steps: formation and breakdown.
