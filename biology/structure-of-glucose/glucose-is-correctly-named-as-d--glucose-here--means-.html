Glucose is correctly named as D-[+]-glucose. Here ‘+’ means ____

(A) ** dextrorotatory

(B) laevorotatory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Glucose is correctly named as D(+)-glucose. ‘ D’ before the name of glucose represents the configuration whereas ‘(+)’ represents dextrorotatory nature of the molecule. Hence, Assertion is correct but Reason is incorrect.

Concept note-3: -Glucose is also named D(+)glucose; D represents the configuration, whereas the sign ‘(+)’ represents the dextrorotatory nature of the glucose molecule.
