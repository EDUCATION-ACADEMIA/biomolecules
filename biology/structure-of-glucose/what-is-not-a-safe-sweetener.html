EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ethylene glycol is highly toxic to humans and is not used in food products. Summary Propylene glycol is a synthetic, colorless, odorless, tasteless liquid that belongs to the same chemical class as alcohol. It should not be confused with the toxic substance ethylene glycol.

Concept note-2: -DESCRIPTION: Ethylene glycol is a useful industrial compound found in many consumer products. Examples include antifreeze, hydraulic brake fluids, some stamp pad inks, ballpoint pens, solvents, paints, plastics, films, and cosmetics. It can also be a pharmaceutical vehicle.

Concept note-3: -Ethylene glycol is normally produced from ethylene which is produced from fossil resources rather than biomass. However, ethylene glycol can be produced from biomass. Ethylene can be produced from sugars via microorganisms like Pseudomonas syringae and Penicillium digitatum.

Concept note-4: -Ethylene glycol has many uses, including as antifreeze in cooling and heating systems, in hydraulic brake fluids, and as a solvent.
