STRUCTURE OF GLUCOSE

[SOURCES]
It belongs to ____ series and is a ____ compound.

(A) 1. L2. Dextrorotatory

(B) ** 1. D2. Laevorotatory

(C) 1. L2. Laevorotatory

(D) 1. D2. Dextrorotatory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fructose is laevorotatory. It rotates the plane of polarized light in anticlockwise direction. Thus it is also called as levulose and is named as D-(-)-fructose.

Concept note-2: -Reason: D-compounds are always dextro and L-compounds are always laevorotatory.

Concept note-3: -Levorotatory (l; (-)): A substance that rotates plane polarized light in a counterclockwise direction. Indicated by including (-) before the compound name, or in the older literature, l, such as (-)-nicotine or (l)-nicotine. (S)-(-)-nicotine solution. (S)-(-)-nicotine solution.

Concept note-4: -or British laevorotatory or laevorotary. : turning toward the left or counterclockwise. especially : rotating the plane of polarization of light to the left compare dextrorotatory.
