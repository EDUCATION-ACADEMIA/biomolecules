STRUCTURE OF GLUCOSE

[SOURCES]
What is the chemical composition of a glucose molecule?

(A) CH3 and COOH

(B) ** C, H and O

(C) C, H, O and N

(D) C, H and OH

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The chemical formula of Glucose is C6H12O6. Glucose is a monosaccharide containing an aldehyde group (-CHO). It is made of 6 carbon atoms, 12 hydrogen atoms and 6 oxygen atoms. Glucose is an aldohexose.

Concept note-2: -C6H12O6 C 6 H 12 O 6-It is the molecular formula of glucose. Here, C6 indicates that the number of carbon atoms present in glucose is 6. H12 refers that the number of hydrogen atoms present in glucose as 12. O6 indicates that the number of oxygen atoms present in glucose is 6.

Concept note-3: -Glucose is a group of carbohydrates which is a simple sugar with a chemical formula C6H12O6. It is made of six carbon atoms and an aldehyde group. Therefore, it is referred to as aldohexose. It exists in two forms viz open-chain (acyclic) form or ring (cyclic) form.

Concept note-4: -glucose, also called dextrose, one of a group of carbohydrates known as simple sugars (monosaccharides). Glucose (from Greek glykys; “sweet”) has the molecular formula C6H12O6. It is found in fruits and honey and is the major free sugar circulating in the blood of higher animals.
