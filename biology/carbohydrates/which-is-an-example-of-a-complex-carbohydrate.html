CARBOHYDRATES

[SOURCES]
Which is an example of a complex carbohydrate?

(A) ** Digested slowly

(B) Digested quickly

(C) I have no idea

(D) Digested normally

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They are high in fibre and are digested slowly by the body. Peas, beans, and whole grains are some examples of complex carbohydrates.

Concept note-2: -Think of whole grains as slow carbs because of this slow digestion. (Other slow carbs include fruits, vegetables, beans and grains.) Whole grains-which include everything from whole wheat to brown rice to steel-cut oats and farro-are also rich in fiber.

Concept note-3: -The slowest-digesting grains include 100 percent stone-ground whole wheat or pumpernickel bread, wheat and corn tortillas, quinoa, brown rice, rolled or steel-cut oatmeal, oat bran, barley and bulgur. Of these, barley and oats have the lowest GI values, according to Grains and Legumes Nutrition Council.

Concept note-4: -Complex carbohydrates are made up of sugar molecules that are strung together in long, complex chains. Complex carbohydrates are found in foods such as peas, beans, whole grains, and vegetables.

Concept note-5: -Some slow digesting vegetables include peas, carrots, eggplant, cauliflower, broccoli, onions, lettuce, tomatoes, green beans, and red peppers. Grain choices to consider include oat bran, rolled oats, brown rice, and wheat tortillas.
