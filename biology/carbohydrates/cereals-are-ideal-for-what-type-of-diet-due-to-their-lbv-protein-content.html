Cereals are ideal for what type of diet due to their LBV protein content?

(A) ** Vegan

(B) High fat

(C) Coeliacs

(D) Babies

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -HBV foods: meat, poultry, cheese, soya beans, milk, quinoa, eggs, fish, yogurt, quark, soya beans, quinoa. LBV foods: peas, beans, nuts, lentils, cereals (rice, oats, barley, rye, millet, sorghum) and cereal products (bread, pasta), seeds and gelatine.

Concept note-2: -The biological value relates to how many amino acids are present in a protein. If a food is missing one or more of the indispensable amino acids, it has a low biological value (LBV). For example, baked beans have an LBV. If a food has all the indispensable amino acids, it has a high biological value (HBV).
