Which of the following polysaccharides is used to measure kidney function by determining the glomerular filtration rate (GFR)?

(A) Chitin

(B) Desxtrin

(C) ** Inulin

(D) Pectin

(E) Galactan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The polysaccharide, inulin, is considered the clinical gold standard for measuring glomerular filtration rate (GFR), an assessment of kidney filtering capacity and renal function, and therefore, is a prognostic indicator of chronic kidney disease (CKD).

Concept note-2: -Inulin and its analog sinistrin are used to help measure kidney function by determining the glomerular filtration rate (GFR), which is the volume of fluid filtered from the renal (kidney) glomerular capillaries into the Bowmanâ€™s capsule per unit time.

Concept note-3: -Urinary inulin clearance is considered the gold standard for measuring GFR because inulin has all the properties of an ideal marker. It is freely filtered by the glomerulus, is not secreted or reabsorbed in the tubules, and is not synthetized or metabolized by the kidney.

Concept note-4: -Inulin (INU) is a flexible, fructan type polysaccharide carbohydrate, mainly obtained from the root of chicory. It is a water-soluble dietary fibre and has been recently approved by the Food and Drug Administration for improving the nutritional values of food products.

Concept note-5: -Creatinine is a chemical waste product of creatine. Creatine is a chemical the body makes to supply energy, mainly to muscles. The lab specialist combines your blood creatinine level with several other factors to estimate your GFR.
