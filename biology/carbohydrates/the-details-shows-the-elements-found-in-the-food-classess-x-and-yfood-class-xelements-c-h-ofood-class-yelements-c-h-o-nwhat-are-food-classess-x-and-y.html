EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Carbon, oxygen, hydrogen, nitrogen, phosphorus, sulfur, calcium, sodium, potassium, magnesium, or chlorine are major elements. Carbon is an important element in food since this element is the main component of all organic compounds present in food.

Concept note-2: -The major nutrients in our food are carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins and minerals. In addition, food also contains dietary fibres and water. Carbohydrates and fats mainly provide energy to our body. Proteins and minerals are needed for the growth and the maintenance of our body.

Concept note-3: -There are six major nutrients – water, carbohydrates, protein, fat, vitamins, and minerals.

Concept note-4: -The six most common elements found in food are Carbon (C), Hydrogen (H), Nitrogen (N) Oxygen (O), Phosphorous (P) and Sulphur (S). The elements found in dissolved salts include Sodium (Na), Magnesium (Mg), Chlorine (Cl), Potassium (K) and Calcium (Ca).
