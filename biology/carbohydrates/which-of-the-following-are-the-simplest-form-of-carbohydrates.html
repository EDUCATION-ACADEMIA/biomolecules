Which of the following are the simplest form of carbohydrates?

(A) ** Monosaccharides

(B) Disaccharides

(C) Oligosaccharides

(D) Polysaccharides

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Simplest carbohydrates are monosaccharides as they cannot break down to form sugars.

Concept note-2: -Monosaccharides are the simplest carbohydrates; they conform to the general chemical formula (CH2O)x and are termed simple sugars. The most commonly occurring monosaccharides contain three to six carbon atoms in an unbranched single-bonded chain. Monosaccharides are signified by the suffix-ose.

Concept note-3: -Glucose is the simplest form of carbohydrate. The chemical formula for glucose is C6H12O6.

Concept note-4: -The simplest form of monosaccharides is triose. Triose contains three carbon atoms in the structure.
