CARBOHYDRATES

[SOURCES]
It is also called fruit sugar.

(A) ** fructose

(B) glucose

(C) sucrose

(D) galactose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fructose is also known as “fruit sugar” because it primarily occurs naturally in many fruits. It also occurs naturally in other plant foods such as honey, sugar beets, sugar cane and vegetables. Fructose is the sweetest naturally occurring carbohydrate and is 1.2–1.8 times sweeter than sucrose (table sugar).

Concept note-2: -Fructose: Found in fruits. Glucose: Found in honey, fruits and vegetables. Lactose and galactose: Found in milk and dairy products. Maltose: Found in barley. Sucrose: Table sugar; a combination of glucose and fructose found in plants.

Concept note-3: -Fructose, or “fruit sugar, ” is a monosaccharide like glucose ( 1 ). It’s naturally found in fruit, honey, agave, and most root vegetables. Moreover, it’s commonly added to processed foods in the form of high fructose corn syrup.

Concept note-4: -Fructose, or “fruit sugar”, is one of the three most common natural monosaccharides. (The other two are glucose and galactose.) As its name implies, fructose is found in almost all fruits; but it also exists in commercial quantities in sugarcane, sugarbeets, and corn.

Concept note-5: -Fructose, or levulose, is the sugar source present in both fruit and honey.
