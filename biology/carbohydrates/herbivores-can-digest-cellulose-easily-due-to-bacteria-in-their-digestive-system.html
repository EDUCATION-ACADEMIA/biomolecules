Herbivores can digest cellulose easily due to bacteria in their digestive system.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -All herbivores eat grasses and other plant material. Plants contain cellulose, which is very hard to digest. So, when a herbivore swallows some grass, the chewed grass first enters the compartment of the stomach called the rumen. The rumen contains a salty solution and bacteria that helps to break down the cellulose.

Concept note-2: -Herbivores are animals that usually feed on plants. The plants contain a lot of cellulose which is hard to be digested by digestive organs. Therefore, herbivores have bacteria and protozoa in the rumen, which helps in the digestion of cellulose.

Concept note-3: -Cellulose is a major building block of plant cell walls, consisting of molecules linked together into solid fibers. For humans, cellulose is indigestible, and the majority of gut bacteria lack the enzymes required to break down cellulose.
