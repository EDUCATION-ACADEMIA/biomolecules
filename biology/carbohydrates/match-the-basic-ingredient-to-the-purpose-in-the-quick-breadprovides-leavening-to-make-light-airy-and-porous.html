EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Modern baking powders are combinations of baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) and dry acids or acid salts, usually with starch added for stability in storage.

Concept note-2: -Quick breads use the chemical leavening agents of baking powder and/or baking soda. Baking powder and baking soda do not require time for rising, so the batter for quick bread is cooked immediately after mixing.

Concept note-3: -Leavening is the process of chemically or biologically raising dough to make baked goods. The substances used to make dough airy and porous are known as leavening agents, or leavens. These include air, steam, and carbon dioxide.

Concept note-4: -Leavening adds volume to your baked goods, whether youâ€™re baking bread, cake or cookies. The trapped air that is formed by the leavening process creates a more tender and open crumb in your breads and cakes and provides a more pleasant texture and mouthfeel.
