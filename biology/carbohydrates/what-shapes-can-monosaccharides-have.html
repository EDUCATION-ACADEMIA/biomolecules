What shapes can monosaccharides have?

(A) ** Straight chains of carbon, and 5-sided and 6-sided rings of carbon

(B) Rings of carbons with up to 9 sides

(C) Straight chains of carbon and triangles of carbon

(D) 3-sided and 4-sided rings of carbon

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The monosaccharides can be divided into groups based on the number of carbon atoms in the molecules, thus: trioses have 3-C atoms, tetroses have 4-C atoms, pentoses have 5-C atoms, and hexoses have 6-C atoms.

Concept note-2: -Cyclic structures of this kind are termed furanose (five-membered) or pyranose (six-membered), reflecting the ring size relationship to the common heterocyclic compounds furan and pyran shown on the right.

Concept note-3: -Glucose is six membered ring, while fructose is a five-membered ring.

Concept note-4: -Furan is a five-membered cyclic compound with one oxygen and four carbon atoms.
