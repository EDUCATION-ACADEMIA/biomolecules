Carbon is different from many other elements in that

(A) it has four electrons in its outermost energy level

(B) it readily bonds with other carbon atoms

(C) it can form single, double and triple bonds with other atoms

(D) ** all of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: Carbon is different from most other elements in that it can form single, double, or triple bonds with other atoms (c is correct).

Concept note-2: -Carbon atoms are unique because they can bond together to form very long, durable chains that can have branches or rings of various sizes and often contain thousands of carbon atoms. Silicon and a few other elements can form similar chains; but they are generally shorter, and much less durable.

Carbon-12 has six neutrons in its nucleus, carbon-13 has seven neutrons and carbon-14 has eight neutrons.

Concept note-4: -carbon group element, any of the six chemical elements that make up Group 14 (IVa) of the periodic table-namely, carbon (C), silicon (Si), germanium (Ge), tin (Sn), lead (Pb), and flerovium (Fl).
