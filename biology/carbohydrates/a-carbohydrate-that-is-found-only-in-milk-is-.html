A carbohydrate that is found only in milk is ____

(A) Sucrose

(B) Glucose

(C) Maltose

(D) ** Lactose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lactose is the major disaccharide found in milk, and is catabolized into glucose and galactose by the enzyme lactase. Lactose is an important energy source and ssometimes it is referred to simply as milk sugar, as it is present in high percentages in dairy products.

Concept note-2: -Lactose, as a unique carbohydrate in most mammalian milks, has been part of the human diet since our very origin. Nowadays, because of its chemical characteristics, lactose is found in many milk-derived products and is an important raw material in pharmaceutical products [1, 2].

Concept note-3: -lactose, carbohydrate containing one molecule of glucose and one of galactose linked together. Composing about 2 to 8 percent of the milk of all mammals, lactose is sometimes called milk sugar. It is the only common sugar of animal origin.

Concept note-4: -Lactose is a sugar found in milk and milk products. Lactose intolerance happens when your small intestine does not make enough of a digestive enzyme called lactase. Lactase breaks down the lactose in food so your body can absorb it.
