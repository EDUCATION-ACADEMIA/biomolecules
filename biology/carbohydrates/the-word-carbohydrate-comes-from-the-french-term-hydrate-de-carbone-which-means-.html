[SOURCES]
The word carbohydrate comes from the French term hydrate de carbone which means ____

(A) hydrates of sugar

(B) hydrates of milk

(C) hydrates

(D) ** hydrates of carbon

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The term carbohydrate is derived from the French word known as ‘Hydrate de carbone’ which means hydrate of carbon so we can say that carbohydrates comprises carbon, hydrogen and oxygen. The general formula of carbohydrates can be represented as Cn(H2O)n.

Concept note-2: -noun. carbohydrate [noun] (chemistry) (any of a group of) substances containing carbon, hydrogen and oxygen, especially the sugars and starches found in food.

Concept note-3: -hydrates de carbone pl m Carbohydrates are a part of a healthy diet. Les hydrates de carbone font partie d’une alimentation saine.

Concept note-4: -The term carbohydrate or hydrates of carbon is derived from its basic elemental formula in which carbon is joined to hydrogen and oxygen present in the same ratio as in water.

Concept note-5: -The word ‘Carbohydrate’ comes from the French term ‘hydrate de carbone’, which simply means ‘hydrate of carbon’. A carbohydrate is a macronutrient element composed of three different elements inside it. They are: Carbon.
