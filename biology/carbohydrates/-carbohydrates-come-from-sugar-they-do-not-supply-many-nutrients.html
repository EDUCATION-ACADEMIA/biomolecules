BIOMOLECULES

CARBOHYDRATES

[SOURCES]
____ carbohydrates come from sugar. They do not supply many nutrients.

(A) complex

(B) optional

(C) ** simple

(D) loaded

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Simply put, simple carbohydrates are sugars. They include the white table sugar we think of when we hear the word “sugar.” But they also include naturally occurring sugars such as those found in fruit and milk, and the refined sugars added to many processed foods.

Concept note-2: -Sugars. They are also called simple carbohydrates because they are in the most basic form. They can be added to foods, such as the sugar in candy, desserts, processed foods, and regular soda. They also include the kinds of sugar that are found naturally in fruits, vegetables, and milk.

Concept note-3: -Simple carbohydrates found in processed, refined or added sugars that do not contain any nutritional value include: Candy. Regular (non-diet) carbonated beverages, such as soda. Syrups.

Concept note-4: -Sugar, or carbohydrates, are the most important and accessible source of energy for your body. Carbohydrates are one of three macronutrients. Macronutrients are the main nutrients your body needs to function properly.
