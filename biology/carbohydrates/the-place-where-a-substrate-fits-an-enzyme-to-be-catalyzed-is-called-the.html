The place where a substrate fits an enzyme to be catalyzed is called the

(A) Substrate

(B) ** Active site

(C) Protein

(D) Carbohydrate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The part of the enzyme where the substrate binds is called the active site (since that’s where the catalytic “action” happens). A substrate enters the active site of the enzyme. This forms the enzyme-substrate complex.

Concept note-2: -The active site is a groove or crevice on an enzyme in which a substrate binds to facilitate the catalyzed chemical reaction. Enzymes are typically specific because the conformation of amino acids in the active site stabilizes the specific binding of the substrate.

Concept note-3: -The active site is the region of the enzyme where substrate molecules bind and undergo chemical reaction.

Concept note-4: -The location within the enzyme where the substrate binds is called the enzyme’s active site . The active site is where the “action” happens, so to speak. Since enzymes are proteins, there is a unique combination of amino acid residues (also called side chains, or R groups) within the active site.
