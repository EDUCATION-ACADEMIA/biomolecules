How are the functions of a carbohydrate and a lipid similar?

(A) ** Both are a source of energy.

(B) Both are replicated during meiosis.

(C) Both lower the activation energy of reactions.

(D) Both dissolve nutrients in the digestive system.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Carbohydrate in the form of glucose is the main source of energy for most metabolic processes in the body. Lipids provide essential fatty acids for brain development and are an important source of energy. Together carbohydrates and lipids provide the non-nitrogen energy in parenteral nutrition (PN).

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: Carbohydrates and lipids are alike in the fact that they are both types of organic molecules found in living organisms. This means that they consist of many carbon atoms with oxygen and hydrogen bonded to them. Both carbohydrates and lipids are energy sources for living things.

Concept note-3: -Because lipids have high proportion of C-H bonds which store the chemical potential energy in a much effective way. Carbs on the other hand, have high ratio of C-O bonds because of glycosidic linkages so they do not store as much energy in their bonds.

Concept note-4: -Similarities:-Complex carbohydrates, for example polysaccharides, and lipids both contain alot of chemical energy and can be used for energy storage.-Complex carbohydrates and lipids are both insoluble in water, they are not easily transported.

Concept note-5: -Carbohydrates, proteins and fats are all composed of carbon and hydrogen. In fact, they fall in the category of organic chemistry. All three of them are polymers that are formed when their monomers link together via dehydration synthesis. The polymers break into the monomers through hydrolysis.
