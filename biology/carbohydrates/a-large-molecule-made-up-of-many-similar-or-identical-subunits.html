A large molecule made up of many similar or identical subunits.

(A) lipids

(B) ** polymer

(C) monomer

(D) monosaccharide

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These are known as macromolecules. Most macromolecules are polymers, which are long chains of subunits called monomers. These subunits are often very similar to each other, and for all the diversity of polymers (and living things in general) there are only about 40-50 common monomers.

Concept note-2: -The name given for a long molecule consisting of many similar or identical monomers that are linked together is a polymer.

Concept note-3: -A large molecule composed of similar smaller subunits join via covalent bonds is called a polymer. The small subunits that form polymers are called monomers.

Concept note-4: -Biomacromolecules are large biological polymers, such as nucleic acids, proteins, and carbohydrates, that are made up of monomers linked together.
