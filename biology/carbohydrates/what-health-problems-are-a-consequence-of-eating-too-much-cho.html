What health problems are a consequence of eating too much CHO?

(A) Weight Loss

(B) Dizziness

(C) ** Type 2 Diabetes

(D) all of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -High blood sugar levels from Type 2 diabetes can lead to serious health problems including heart disease, stroke or death.

Concept note-2: -It can lead to: permanent damage to the nerves in your hands and feet (peripheral neuropathy) permanent damage to your eyes and problems with your sight (diabetic retinopathy) life-threatening conditions such as diabetic ketoacidosis.

Concept note-3: -Heart disease and stroke: People with diabetes are two times more likely to have heart disease or a stroke as people without diabetes. Blindness and other eye problems: Damage to blood vessels in the retina (diabetic retinopathy) Clouding of the lens (cataract)

Concept note-4: -Potential complications of diabetes and frequent comorbidities include: Heart and blood vessel disease. Diabetes is associated with an increased risk of heart disease, stroke, high blood pressure and narrowing of blood vessels (atherosclerosis). Nerve damage (neuropathy) in limbs.

Concept note-5: -More carbohydrate than usual can cause blood glucose levels to go too high, and less than usual can cause a hypo (low blood glucose levels).
