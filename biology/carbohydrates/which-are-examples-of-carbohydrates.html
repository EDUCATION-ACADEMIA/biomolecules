[SOURCES]
Which are examples of carbohydrates?

(A) sugar

(B) ** starch

(C) oils

(D) ** cellulose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Polysaccharides include starch, glycogen, cellulose, and chitin as primary examples.

Concept note-2: -Wood, paper, and cotton all contain cellulose. Cellulose is a white fibrous substance with no flavor and odor, insoluble in water and organic solvents. It is the main ingredient of plant cell walls.

Concept note-3: -cellulose, a complex carbohydrate, or polysaccharide, consisting of 3, 000 or more glucose units.

Concept note-4: -Carbohydrates are found in a wide array of both healthy and unhealthy foods-bread, beans, milk, popcorn, potatoes, cookies, spaghetti, soft drinks, corn, and cherry pie. They also come in a variety of forms. The most common and abundant forms are sugars, fibers, and starches.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: Cellulose, commonly called fiber, is a complex carbohydrate because it is a long, long chain of many monosaccharides. Cellulose molecules are generally at least 3, 000 glucose molecules long.
