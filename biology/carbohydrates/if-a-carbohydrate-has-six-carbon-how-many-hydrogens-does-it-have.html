If a carbohydrate has six carbon, how many hydrogens does it have?

(A) 3

(B) 6

(C) 9

(D) ** 12

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Glucose has a chemical formula of: C6H12O6 That means glucose is made of 6 carbon atoms, 12 hydrogen atoms and 6 oxygen atoms.

Concept note-2: -Glucose is a hexose, with six carbon atoms (Fig. 5.41), and an aldehyde-aldose monosaccharide. Fructose is also a hexose, but a ketose.

Concept note-3: -Atomic mass of carbon is 12gm. 12 gm of carbon contains 6 . 02 × 1 0 23 no of atoms. If 12 gm of C contains Avogadro’s no of atoms then 6gm of C will have half of avogadro’s no of atoms(1/2×6.02× 1 0 23 ) = 3 . 01 × 1 0 23 no of atoms.

Concept note-4: -This glucose chain forms a ring in aqueous solutions, e.g., in body fluids, as shown below. Fructose is another sugar that also has 6 carbons, 12 hydrogens, and 6 oxygen atoms.

Concept note-5: -The ratio of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen in most carbohydrates is 1:2:1. This means for every one carbon atom there are two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom. This forms the structure of even the most complex carbohydrates such as starch and glycogen.
