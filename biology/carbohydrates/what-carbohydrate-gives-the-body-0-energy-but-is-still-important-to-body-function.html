[SOURCES]
What carbohydrate gives the body 0 energy but is still important to body function?

(A) ** Fiber

(B) Glucose

(C) Glycogen

(D) B12

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Most carbs are broken down into glucose in the digestive system and provide the body with fuel to perform essential functions. Each gram of carbs provides the body with four calories. The exception to this is fiber, which generally does not provide many calories.

Concept note-2: -Carbohydrates-fiber, starches and sugars-are essential food nutrients that your body turns into glucose to give you the energy to function. Complex carbs in fruits, vegetables and whole-grain products are less likely to spike blood sugar than simple carbs (sugars).

Concept note-3: -Fiber is a type of carbohydrate found mainly in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes. It helps keep you regular, but it offers many other health benefits as well, especially for people with diabetes or prediabetes.

Concept note-4: -Carbohydrates provide the body with glucose, which is converted to energy used to support bodily functions and physical activity.
