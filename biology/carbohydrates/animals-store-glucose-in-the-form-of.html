Animals store glucose in the form of

(A) cellulose

(B) ** glycogen

(C) wax

(D) lipids

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Glycogen is a homopolysaccharide found in the liver and muscles of animals, where it is used to store energy. Chemically, glycogen is related to starch and closely resembles amylopectin.

Concept note-2: -Animals store glucose as glycogen in hepatocytes (liver cells) and myocytes (muscle cells) while the plants store glucose as starch in their leaves. Glycogen is a highly branched polymer and contains many non-reducing ends (regions where both degradation and synthesis occur) while starch is not highly branched.

Concept note-3: -Statement 1: Glucose is stored in animals in the form of glycogen.

Concept note-4: -Glycogen is a multibranched polysaccharide of glucose that serves as a form of energy storage in animals, fungi, and bacteria.

Concept note-5: -Glycogen is an extensively branched glucose polymer that animals use as an energy reserve. It is the animal analog to starch. Glycogen does not exist in plant tissue. It is highly concentrated in the liver, although skeletal muscles contain the most glycogen by weight.
