What monosaccharide is the most important abundant in nature?

(A) galactose

(B) ** glucose

(C) fructose

(D) sucrose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Glucose is the most abundant monosaccharide. Galactose, mannose, fructose and ribose are also of major biological importance.

Concept note-2: -The hexose D-glucose is the most abundant monosaccharide in nature. Other very common and abundant hexose monosaccharides are galactose, used to make the disaccharide milk sugar lactose, and the fruit sugar fructose.

Concept note-3: -d-Glucose, considering all of its combined forms, is the most abundant monosaccharide that naturally occurs in nature [13]. The most abundant ketose is d-arabino-hexulose, known more commonly by its trivial name, d-fructose [2].

Concept note-4: -Presumably, glucose is the most abundant natural monosaccharide because it is less glycated with proteins than other monosaccharides.

Concept note-5: -Glucose is the most abundant monosaccharide found in nature, while cellulose is the most abundant polysaccharide.
