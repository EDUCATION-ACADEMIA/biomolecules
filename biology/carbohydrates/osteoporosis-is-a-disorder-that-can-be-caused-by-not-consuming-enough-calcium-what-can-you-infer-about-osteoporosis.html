Osteoporosis is a disorder that can be caused by not consuming enough calcium. What can you infer about osteoporosis?

(A) It affects the nervous system

(B) It affects the skin

(C) It affects the stomach

(D) ** It affects the bones

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If you do not take in enough calcium, the body takes it from the bones, which can lead to bone loss. This can make bones weak and thin, leading to osteoporosis. Good sources of calcium include: Low-fat dairy products.

Concept note-2: -Osteoporosis weakens bones to the point that they can break easily. It is called a “silent disease” because people who develop it may not notice any changes until a bone breaks-usually a bone in the hip, spine, or wrist. Bones are made of living tissue.

Concept note-3: -When these organs demand calcium, they’ll steal it from the mineral storehouse in your bones. Over time, as you deplete the mineral reservoir in your bones, you end up with thin, brittle bones.
