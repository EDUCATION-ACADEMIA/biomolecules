What is the energy source of the cell?

(A) ** ATP

(B) DNA

(C) RNA

(D) mRNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adenosine triphosphate (ATP) is the source of energy for use and storage at the cellular level. The structure of ATP is a nucleoside triphosphate, consisting of a nitrogenous base (adenine), a ribose sugar, and three serially bonded phosphate groups.

Concept note-2: -Adenosine triphosphate, also known as ATP, is the cellular energy currency. They are molecules that carry energy and are created as a byproduct of photophosphorylation reactions. Minerals, carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, etc. are the main energy sources for ATP.

Concept note-3: -Currently, cell biology is based on glucose as the main source of energy.
