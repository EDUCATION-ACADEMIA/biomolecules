When you eat a plant, you derive energy from the stored ____, but not from the ____ component.

(A) starch ____ chitin

(B) cellulose ____ chitin

(C) ** starch ____ cellulose

(D) glycogen ____ cellulose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When you eat a plant, you derive energy from the stored sugars and starches, but not from the cellulose (cell wall) component.

Concept note-2: -Starch and cellulose are two types of polysaccharides that are found in plants. However, they have different structures and functions. Starch is primarily used for energy storage, whereas cellulose is used for structure and support.

Concept note-3: -Plants store their excess glucose in the form of starch. Glucose is stored in the form of glycogen in humans and animals. Starch and glycogen are examples of storage polysaccharides whereas chitin and cellulose are examples of structural polysaccharides.

Concept note-4: -Starch is a storage form of energy in plants. It contains two polymers composed of glucose units: amylose (linear) and amylopectin (branched). Glycogen is a storage form of energy in animals. It is a branched polymer composed of glucose units.

Concept note-5: -"Cellulose is a storage form of glucose for plants and animals‚Äù is a FALSE statement. Cellulose is not a storage polysaccharide, rather it is a structural polysaccharide.
