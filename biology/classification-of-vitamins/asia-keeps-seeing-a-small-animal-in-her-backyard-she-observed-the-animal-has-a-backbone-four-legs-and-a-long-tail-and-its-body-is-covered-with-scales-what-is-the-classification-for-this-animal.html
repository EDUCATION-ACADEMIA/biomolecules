BIOLOGY

CLASSIFICATION OF VITAMINS

[SOURCES]
Asia keeps seeing a small animal in her backyard. She observed the animal has a backbone, four legs and a long tail, and its body is covered with scales. What is the classification for this animal?

(A) bird

(B) mammal

(C) ** reptile

(D) amphibian

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Reptiles are a class of vertebrates made up mostly of snakes, turtles, lizards, and crocodilians. These animals are most easily recognized by their dry, scaly skin. Almost all reptiles are cold-blooded, and most lay eggs-though some, like the boa constrictor, give birth to live young.

Concept note-2: -They are all vertebrates. Vertebrates are animals with backbones (vertebrae). The 5 classes of vertebrates are: Fish, Amphibians, Reptiles, Mammals, and Birds.

Concept note-3: -Animals are generally classified into two categories on the basis of the presence or absence of backbone or vertebral column i.e., vertebrates and invertebrates.

Concept note-4: -Reptiles are animals with dry skin and scales. They are carnivores. They lay eggs. For example, lizards, crocodiles, snakes, etc.
