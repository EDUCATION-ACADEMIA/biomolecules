CLASSIFICATION OF VITAMINS

[SOURCES]
The more classification groups that two organisms have in common

(A) ** the more alike they are

(B) shows how vastly different they are

(C) shows they are unrelated

(D) shows that they are the exact same organism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The more synapomorphies two species have in common, the more closely related they are hypothesized to be.

Concept note-2: -Genus. The genus (plural, genera) is the taxonomic rank between family and species. The groups of organisms in a genus share many structural similarities and are very closely related.

Concept note-3: -These final two levels of classification are the genus and the species. A species is a group of organisms that can mate, or interbreed. They can produce offspring that can also reproduce. A species is the smallest group into which organisms are classified.

Concept note-4: -If two species belong to the same class, they also belong to the same taxon higher in the hierarchy. Here, They belong to the same phylum and kingdom.
