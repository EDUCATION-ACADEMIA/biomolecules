Which kingdom is Prokaryotic?

(A) Kingdom Animal

(B) Kingdom Fungi

(C) Kingdom Protista

(D) ** Kingdom Eubacteria

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Complete answer: Eubacteria are the organisms that fall under the kingdom Monera . They are also known as ‘True bacteria’. They are single-celled and are prokaryotic in nature.

Concept note-2: -Cell Type: Eubacteria are prokaryotic cells(no nucleus). Body Structure: Eubacteria are single-celled organisms.

Concept note-3: -Eubacteria are one domain within the phylogenetic tree, and this domain encompasses the kingdom bacteria. Bacteria are ubiquitous. These single-celled organisms form a foundation on which life can thrive.

Concept note-4: -All prokaryotic organisms are included to kingdom Monera. Prokaryotes do not have membrane-bound organelles. According to R.H. Whittaker’s five kingdom classification, all the bacteria were placed under the Kingdom Monera.
