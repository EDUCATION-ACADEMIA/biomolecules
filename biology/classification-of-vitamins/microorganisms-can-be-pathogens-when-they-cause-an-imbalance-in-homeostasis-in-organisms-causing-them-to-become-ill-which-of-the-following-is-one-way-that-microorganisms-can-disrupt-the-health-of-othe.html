Microorganisms can be pathogens when they cause an imbalance in homeostasis in organisms, causing them to become ill. Which of the following is one way that microorganisms can disrupt the health of other organisms?

(A) They can produce antibiotics.

(B) They can release vitamins.

(C) They can infect a virus.

(D) ** They can release harmful toxins.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Microorganisms can be pathogens when they cause an imbalance in homeostasis in organisms, causing them to become ill. Which of the following is one way that microorganisms can disrupt the health of other organisms? They can produce antibiotics.

Concept note-2: -Which of the following best explains one way that microorganisms contribute to maintaining the health of humans? As microorganisms break down organic matter, they release nutrients into the environment that are essential for the growth of organisms that humans rely on for nutrients.

Concept note-3: -The harmful microorganisms are called pathogens. The diseases causing microorganisms include bacteria, viruses, protozoa, fungi and a few variations of worms. Once they invade the host cell, they disrupt or damage the normal cellular activities.

Concept note-4: -Some microbes cause disease. Pathogens can make us sick. These pathogenic bacteria, virus, and other microorganisms cause infections or make toxins that harm our bodies.
