CLASSIFICATION OF VITAMINS

[SOURCES]
Cells that have a true nucleus are called ____

(A) ** eukaryotes

(B) prokaryotes

(C) unicellular

(D) multicellular

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Eukaryotic cells, on the other hand, have chromosomes that are enclosed in a true nucleus, and are therefore separated from the cytoplasm by the nuclear membrane . Eukaryotic cells also have a wide range of organelles that perform specialized functions.

Concept note-2: -The main difference between eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells is that eukaryotic cells have a nucleus. The nucleus is where cells store their DNA, which is the genetic material. The nucleus is surrounded by a membrane. Prokaryotic cells do not have a nucleus.

Concept note-3: -A eukaryotic cell is a cell that has a membrane-bound nucleus and other membrane-bound compartments or sacs, called organelles, which have specialized functions. The word eukaryotic means “true kernel” or “true nucleus, ” alluding to the presence of the membrane-bound nucleus in these cells.

Concept note-4: -Prokaryotes are single-celled organisms belonging to the domains Bacteria and Archaea. Prokaryotic cells are much smaller than eukaryotic cells, have no nucleus, and lack organelles.
