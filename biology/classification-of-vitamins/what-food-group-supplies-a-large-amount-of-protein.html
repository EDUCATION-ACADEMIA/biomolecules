What food group supplies a large amount of protein?

(A) Dairy

(B) Fruit

(C) ** Meats

(D) Vegetables

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Animal-based foods (meat, poultry, fish, eggs, and dairy foods) tend to be good sources of complete protein, while plant-based foods (fruits, vegetables, grains, nuts, and seeds) often lack one or more essential amino acid.

Concept note-2: -Meats Ranked in Protein Levels. Turkey. It looks like turkey is our winner for having the most protein per serving. Youâ€™ll get 30 grams of protein in one serving of turkey, making this lean white meat a great addition to your weekly meal planning.

Concept note-3: -What foods are in the Protein Foods Group? Protein Foods include all foods made from seafood; meat, poultry, and eggs; beans, peas, lentils; and nuts, seeds, and soy products. Beans, peas, and lentils are also part of the Vegetable Group.

Concept note-4: -The 5 main groups The Eatwell Guide divides the foods and drinks we consume into 5 main groups: fruit and vegetables. potatoes, bread, rice, pasta and other starchy carbohydrates. beans, pulses, fish, eggs, meat and other proteins.
