Which type of fruit is characterized by having a large seed surrounded by edible flesh?

(A) pomes

(B) ** drupes

(C) tropical

(D) berries

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A drupe is a type of fruit in which an outer fleshy part surrounds a shell (what we sometimes call a pit) with a seed inside. Some examples of drupes are peaches, plums, and cherries-but walnuts, almonds, and pecans are also drupes. They’re just drupes in which we eat the seed inside the pit instead of the fruit!

Concept note-2: -drupe, in botany, simple fleshy fruit that usually contains a single seed, such as the cherry, peach, and olive.

Concept note-3: -In botanical language, a berry is a simple fruit having seeds and fleshy pulp (the pericarp) produced from the ovary of a single flower.

Concept note-4: -The fleshy part of a fruit is called the mesocarp. It is between the fruit’s skin (exocarp) and the seeds. The juicy yellow part of the mango, for example, is the “fleshy” part of the mango. Usually, when we eat a fruit, we eat the “fleshy” part.
