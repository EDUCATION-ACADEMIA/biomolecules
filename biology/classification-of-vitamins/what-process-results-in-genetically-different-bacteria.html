What process results in genetically different bacteria?

(A) binary fission

(B) mitosis

(C) meiosis

(D) ** conjugation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Transduction is the process of transfer of genes from the recipient to the donor through bacteriophage.

Concept note-2: -The three most common ways that bacteria diversify their DNA are transformation, conjugation, and transduction.

Concept note-3: -Conjugation is the process by which one bacterium transfers genetic material to another through direct contact. During conjugation, one bacterium serves as the donor of the genetic material, and the other serves as the recipient. The donor bacterium carries a DNA sequence called the fertility factor, or F-factor.

Concept note-4: -Conjugation is a gene transfer process in which a recipient bacterium receives DNA from a donor bacterium by cell-to-cell contact through conjugative pili. Conjugation is mediated by certain plasmids or transposons.

Concept note-5: -Transduction uses bacterial viruses, called bacteriophages, to transfer DNA from one infected cell to another. Transformation is the ability of some cells to take up freely floating DNA found in the environment. Conjugation allows for the transfer of DNA through a structure called a pilus from one cell to another.
