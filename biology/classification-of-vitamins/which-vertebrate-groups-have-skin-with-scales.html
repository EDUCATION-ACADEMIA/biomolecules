Which vertebrate groups have skin with scales?

(A) Only fish

(B) Only reptiles

(C) ** Both fish and reptiles

(D) Fish, reptiles and amphibians

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Dermal scales are found almost exclusively in fishes and some reptiles. They are bony plates that fit closely together or overlap and form the dermal skeleton. Highly developed dermal scales are seen in turtles, where the bony plates form a rigid dermal skeleton that is attached to the true skeleton.

Concept note-2: -Reptiles live on land, have scaly skin and lay eggs with shells. They have lungs rather than gills and may or may not have teeth. Some examples of reptiles are turtles, tortoises, lizards, snakes, crocodiles, and dinosaurs (now extinct).

Concept note-3: -REPTILES: Reptiles are vertebrates that are cold blooded. They have dry, scaly skin and lay eggs.

Concept note-4: -For example, lizards, crocodiles, snakes, etc.
