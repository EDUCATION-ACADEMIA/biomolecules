Which biomolecule has sugars and starches?

(A) Lipids

(B) Proteins

(C) ** Carbohydrates

(D) Nucleic Acid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Polysaccharides may be very large molecules. Starch, glycogen, cellulose, and chitin are examples of polysaccharides. Starch is the stored form of sugars in plants and is made up of amylose and amylopectin (both polymers of glucose).

Concept note-2: -Carbohydrates are biochemical compounds that include sugar, starch, glycogen, and cellulose.

Concept note-3: -Therefore, glucose belongs to the carbohydrate biomolecule.

Concept note-4: -They are starch, glycogen, and cellulose. Starch is the most important source of carbohydrates in the human diet. Starch is formed in plants. Starch is commonly found in seeds where it serves as a storage form of carbohydrate.
