MONOSACCHARIDES

[SOURCES]
A carbohydrate that cannot be split or hydrolyzed into smaller carbohydrates.

(A) Polysaccharide

(B) Disaccharide

(C) Sucrose

(D) ** Monosaccharide

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Monosaccharides are carbohydrates that cannot be hydrolyzed to simpler carbohydrates. These compounds may be aldehydes or ketones, or acetals or ketals which yield an alcohol and a monosaccharide when hydrolyzed.

Concept note-2: -9.2. Monosaccharides are the simplest carbohydrates since they cannot be hydrolyzed to smaller carbohydrates.

Concept note-3: -26.2 Classification of Carbohydrates Monosaccharides are carbohydrates that cannot be hydrolyzed to simpler carbohydrates.

Concept note-4: -A monosaccharide is the simplest carbohydrate and cannot be hydrolyzed to produce a smaller carbohydrate molecule. Disaccharides contain two monosaccharide units, and polysaccharides contain many monosaccharide units.

Concept note-5: -A polysaccharide cannot be split into smaller carbohydrates b. A polysaccharide is composed of two monosaccharide units.
