What kind of glycosidic linkage do sucrose have?

(A) ** (alpha 1 → beta 2)

(B) (alpha 1 → alpha 2)

(C) (Beta 1 → beta 2)

(D) (Beta 1 → Alpha 2)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sucrose is composed of a molecule of glucose joined to a molecule of fructose by an -1, -2-glycosidic linkage.

Concept note-2: -The bond between the two sugars in sucrose is an alpha-1, 2-glycosidic bond.

Concept note-3: -There are are two types of glycosidic bonds-1, 4 alpha and 1, 4 beta glycosidic bonds. 1, 4 alpha glycosidic bonds are formed when the OH on the carbon-1 is below the glucose ring; while 1, 4 beta glycosidic bonds are formed when the OH is above the plane.

Concept note-4: -Sucrose: A disaccharide composed of -glucose and -fructose connected through an -1, -2-glycosidic linkage. -1-4 glycosidic linkages: Linkages in which carbon atoms 1 and 4 of the two monomers form a glycosidic bond.

Concept note-5: -Cellulose is a linear homopolysaccharide of D-glucose units, which are linked together by beta-1, 4 glycosidic bonds.
