What are the symptoms of deficiency of Vitamin A?

(A) Person gets tired easily.

(B) Swelling of gland in neck.

(C) Bleeding of gums and swelling of joints.

(D) ** Person is not able to see in dim light.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Night blindness is one of the first signs of vitamin A deficiency. In its more severe forms, vitamin A deficiency contributes to blindness by making the cornea very dry, thus damaging the retina and cornea.

Concept note-2: -Vitamin A plays an important role in your vision. To see the full spectrum of light, your eye needs to produce certain pigments for your retina to work properly. Vitamin A deficiency stops the production of these pigments, leading to night blindness.

Concept note-3: -Vitamin B2, B3, and B6 deficiencies are associated with dry eyes and eye infections in the front of the eye. Vitamin B12 deficiency is associated with pernicious anemia and may cause retinal bleeding, destruction of nerve tissue, and lead to permanent vision loss.

Concept note-4: -Some of the first signs of vitamin A deficiency are dry eyes and night blindness.
