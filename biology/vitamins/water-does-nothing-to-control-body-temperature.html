Water does nothing to control body temperature.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Body water serves as a thermoregulator, controlling overall body temperature by assisting in heat dissipation. Water is lost through perspiration when the body becomes too hot, and the evaporation of this sweat from the skin surface removes heat from the body.

Concept note-2: -The body water has an important role as a thermoregulator, regulating the overall body temperature by helping dissipate heat.

Concept note-3: -The hypothalamus helps keep the body’s internal functions in balance. It helps regulate: Appetite and weight. Body temperature.

Concept note-4: -Hypohydration increases heat storage and reduces one’s ability to tolerate heat strain. The increased heat storage is mediated by reduced sweating rate (evaporative heat loss) and reduced skin blood flow (dry heat loss) for a given core temperature.
