Sodium, chloride and potassium are also known as ____

(A) B vitamins

(B) Enzymes

(C) ** Electrolytes

(D) Fat Soluble

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sodium, calcium, potassium, chloride, phosphate, and magnesium are all electrolytes. You get them from the foods you eat and the fluids you drink. The levels of electrolytes in your body can become too low or too high. This can happen when the amount of water in your body changes.

Concept note-2: -Potassium and sodium are electrolytes that help your body function normally by maintaining fluid and blood volume.

Concept note-3: -Electrolytes are minerals in your blood and other body fluids that carry an electric charge.

Concept note-4: -Sodium, potassium, and chloride are the significant electrolytes along with magnesium, calcium, phosphate, and bicarbonates. Electrolytes come from our food and fluids.

Concept note-5: -Sodium, chloride, potassium and calcium are all types of electrolytes.
