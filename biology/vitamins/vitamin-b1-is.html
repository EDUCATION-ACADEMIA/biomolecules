VITAMINS

[SOURCES]
Vitamin B1 is

(A) Niacin

(B) Riboflavin

(C) ** Thiamin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Thiamin (thiamine), or vitamin B1, is a water-soluble vitamin found naturally in some foods, added to foods, and sold as a supplement. Thiamin plays a vital role in the growth and function of various cells. [1] Only small amounts are stored in the liver, so a daily intake of thiamin-rich foods is needed.

Concept note-2: -It is named B1 because it was the first B vitamin discovered. Thiamine is found in both plants and animals and plays a crucial role in certain metabolic reactions. Your body needs it to form adenosine triphosphate (ATP), which every cell of the body uses for energy.

Concept note-3: -Thiamin (vitamin B1) helps the bodyâ€™s cells change carbohydrates into energy. The main role of carbohydrates is to provide energy for the body, especially the brain and nervous system. Thiamin also plays a role in muscle contraction and conduction of nerve signals.

Concept note-4: -Thiamine (vitamin B1) is found in many foods and is used to treat low thiamine, beriberi, certain nerve diseases, and Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome (WKS). Thiamine is required by our bodies to properly use carbohydrates. It also helps maintain proper nerve function.
