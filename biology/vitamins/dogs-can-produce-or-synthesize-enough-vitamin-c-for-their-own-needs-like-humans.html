VITAMINS

[SOURCES]
Dogs can produce, or synthesize, enough vitamin C for their own needs, like humans.

(A) true

(B) ** false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Most mammals, including dogs, synthesize vitamin C in the liver.

Concept note-2: -Safe Vitamins Vitamin C, niacin, biotin, and folic acid are also safe for dogs.

Concept note-3: -Nearly all animals have a gene that allows them to make their own Vitamin C: the GULO gene. And as our question-asker mentioned, some animals have lost this gene over the course of evolution. This includes humans and other apes, insects, bats, guinea pigs, and some birds and fish.

Concept note-4: -The average dog normally produces about 18 milligrams of vitamin C per pound of body weight per day.
