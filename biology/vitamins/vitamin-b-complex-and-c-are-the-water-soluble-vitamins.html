Vitamin B complex and C are the water-soluble vitamins.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The water-soluble vitamins include Vitamin C and Vitamin B complex (thiamine, riboflavin, niacin, pantothenic acid, pyridoxine, biotin, folate, and cobalamin).

Concept note-2: -All eight of the B vitamins and vitamin C are water-soluble. These four vitamins-A, D, E and K-are fat-soluble. The main differences between water-soluble and fat-soluble vitamins are this: how they dissolve and are absorbed by your body and how they are stored.

Concept note-3: -Even though the B-group vitamins are found in many foods, they are water soluble and are generally quite delicate.

Concept note-4: -Vitamin C (ascorbic acid) is a water-soluble vitamin that supports growth and development. It also helps your body absorb iron.

Concept note-5: -1 Answer. Vitamin b and c are water soluble vitamins. This is because they have many polar groups therefore making them soluble in polar solvents such as water.
