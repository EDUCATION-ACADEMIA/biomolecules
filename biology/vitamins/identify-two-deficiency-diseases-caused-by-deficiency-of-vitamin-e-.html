Identify two deficiency diseases caused by deficiency of vitamin E .

(A) Night blindness

(B) ** Rupture of red blood cells

(C) Rickets

(D) ** Nerve disease

(E) Blood not able to clot properly

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vitamin E deficiency is very rare in healthy people. It is almost always linked to certain diseases in which fat is not properly digested or absorbed. Examples include Crohnâ€™s disease, cystic fibrosis, and certain rare genetic diseases such as abetalipoproteinemia and ataxia with vitamin E deficiency (AVED).

Concept note-2: -Vitamin E deficiency can cause nerve pain (neuropathy). The recommended daily amount of vitamin E for adults is 15 milligrams a day.

Concept note-3: -Vitamin E is essential for normal neurological function. It is the major lipid-soluble, chain-breaking antioxidant in the body, protecting the integrity of membranes by inhibiting lipid peroxidation.

Concept note-4: -Its deficiency is related to anaemia due to reduced RBCs lifespan due to increased vulnerability to free-radical-induced RBCs. It causes increased fragility of RBCs and muscular weakness in the body. The correct answer of this question is option (c) which says deficiency of vitamin E causes muscular weakness.
