____ is needed for the absorption of calcium, which is needed to form strong bones and teeth.

(A) Vitamin A

(B) Vitamin B

(C) Vitamin C

(D) ** Vitamin D

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vitamin D helps your body absorb calcium. Eat foods that provide the right amounts of calcium, vitamin D, and protein. This kind of diet will give your body the building blocks it needs to make and maintain strong bones.

Concept note-2: -Vitamin D. Vitamin D helps your body absorb calcium while boosting bone mineral density, so itâ€™s crucial to get an adequate amount of vitamin D to get the most out of your calcium intake.

Concept note-3: -Vitamin D enhances intestinal absorption of calcium and phosphate. Low concentrations of vitamin D are associated with impaired calcium absorption, a negative calcium balance, and a compensatory rise in parathyroid hormone (PTH), which results in excessive bone resorption.

Concept note-4: -A calcium-rich diet (including dairy, nuts, leafy greens and fish) helps to build and protect your bones. Calcium is a mineral that is necessary for life.

Concept note-5: -To absorb calcium, your body also needs vitamin D. A few foods naturally contain small amounts of vitamin D, such as canned salmon with bones and egg yolks. You can also get vitamin D from fortified foods and sun exposure. The RDA for vitamin D is 600 international units (15 micrograms) a day for most adults.
