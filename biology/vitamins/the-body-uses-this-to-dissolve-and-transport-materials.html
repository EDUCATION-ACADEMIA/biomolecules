[SOURCES]
The body uses this to dissolve and transport materials.

(A) Carbohydrates

(B) ** Water

(C) Minerals

(D) Vitamins

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is our circulatory system that transports water and nutrients to the rest of our body. This is a system of tubes which carry blood around the body. When you digest food, your small intestine absorbs the nutrients from your food and passes them into the blood stream.

Concept note-2: -The excellent ability of water to dissolve so many substances allows our cells to use valuable nutrients, minerals, and chemicals in biological processes. Water’s “stickiness” (from surface tension) plays a part in our body’s ability to transport these materials all through ourselves.

Concept note-3: -Water transports valuable nutrients to your body Water allows these nutrients to pass through the capillaries within the intestinal walls to the blood and circulatory system where the valuable nutrients and oxygen can be distributed throughout the body to all the cells and organs.

Concept note-4: -Water As a Transportation Vehicle Water is called the “universal solvent” because more substances dissolve in it than any other fluid. The dissolved substances (solutes) include many materials including ions, sugars, amino acids, vitamins, and minerals.
