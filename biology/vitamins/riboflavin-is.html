Riboflavin is

(A) Vitamin B1

(B) ** Vitamin B2

(C) Vitamin B3

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vitamin B2, or riboflavin, is naturally present in foods, added to foods, and available as a supplement.

Concept note-2: -Antioxidants, such as riboflavin, can fight free radicals and may reduce or help prevent some of the damage they cause. Riboflavin is also needed to help the body change vitamin B6 and folate into forms it can use. It is also important for growth and red blood cell production.

Concept note-3: -Riboflavin (vitamin B2) Riboflavin, also known as vitamin B2, helps: keep skin, eyes and the nervous system healthy. the body release energy from food.

Concept note-4: -(Vitamin B2 Deficiency) Riboflavin deficiency usually occurs with other B vitamin deficiencies. Symptoms and signs include sore throat, lesions of the lips and mucosa of the mouth, glossitis, conjunctivitis, seborrheic dermatitis, and normochromic-normocytic anemia. Diagnosis is usually clinical.
