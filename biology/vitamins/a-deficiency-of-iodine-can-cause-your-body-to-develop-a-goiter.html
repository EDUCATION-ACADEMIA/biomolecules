A deficiency of iodine can cause your body to develop a goiter.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Iodine deficiency is the most common cause of goiter. The body needs iodine to produce thyroid hormone. If you do not have enough iodine in your diet, the thyroid gets larger to try and capture all the iodine it can, so it can make the right amount of thyroid hormone.

Concept note-2: -Iodine deficiency is the most common cause of thyroid disease and, if serious, can cause permanent brain damage and intellectual disability in babies. Iodine is a mineral found naturally in seawater and soil. The body needs iodine to make thyroid hormone in the thyroid gland.

Concept note-3: -Assertion :Iodine deficiency causes goitre. Reason: Reduced iodine intake decreases thyroxine production. Q. Goitre is caused due to deficiency of iodine.

Concept note-4: -Iodine deficiency is the main cause of goiters. Iodine is essential to helping your thyroid produce thyroid hormones. When you donâ€™t have enough iodine, the thyroid works extra hard to make thyroid hormone, causing the gland to grow larger.

Concept note-5: -Iodine is essential for thyroid hormone synthesis. High iodine intakes are well tolerated by most healthy individuals, but in some people, excess iodine intakes may precipitate hyperthyroidism, hypothyroidism, goiter, and/or thyroid autoimmunity.
