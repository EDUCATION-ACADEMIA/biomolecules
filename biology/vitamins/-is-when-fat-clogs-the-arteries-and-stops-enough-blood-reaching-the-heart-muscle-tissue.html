[SOURCES]
____ is when fat clogs the arteries and stops enough blood reaching the heart muscle tissue.

(A) Heart attack

(B) ** Heart disease

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Atherosclerosis is the buildup of fats, cholesterol and other substances in and on the artery walls. This buildup is called plaque. The plaque can cause arteries to narrow, blocking blood flow.

Concept note-2: -These blockages starve tissues of blood and oxygen. This can result in damage or tissue death. It is a common cause of heart attack and stroke.

Concept note-3: -Cholesterol deposits, or plaques, are almost always to blame. These buildups narrow your arteries, decreasing blood flow to your heart. This can cause chest pain, shortness of breath or even a heart attack.

Concept note-4: -How does obesity increase the risk of heart and circulatory diseases? Excess weight can lead to fatty material building up in your arteries (the blood vessels that carry blood to your organs). If the arteries that carry blood to your heart get damaged and clogged, it can lead to a heart attack.
