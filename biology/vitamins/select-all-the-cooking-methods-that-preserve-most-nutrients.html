Select all the cooking methods that preserve most nutrients:

(A) Boil

(B) ** Simmer

(C) ** Microwave

(D) ** Bake

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Steaming. Steaming is one of the best cooking methods for preserving nutrients, including water-soluble vitamins, which are sensitive to heat and water ( 4, 5, 6, 17 ). Researchers have found that steaming broccoli, spinach, and lettuce reduces their vitamin C content by only 9–15% (5).

Concept note-2: -Roasting or baking does not have a significant effect on most vitamins and minerals, with the exception of B vitamins.

Concept note-3: -Researchers found steaming kept the highest level of nutrients. “Boiling vegetables causes water soluble vitamins like vitamin C, B1 and folate to leach into the water, ” Magee said.
