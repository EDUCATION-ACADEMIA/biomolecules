The three classes of nutrients that supply our body with energy are

(A) Glucose, Sucrose, Lactose

(B) ** Fat, proteins, carbohydrates

(C) Vitamins, minerals, fiber

(D) Protein, water, carbohydrates

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Carbohydrates, proteins, and fats are the main types of macronutrients in food (nutrients that are required daily in large quantities). They supply 90% of the dry weight of the diet and 100% of its energy.

Concept note-2: -Macronutrients are eaten in large amounts and include the primary building blocks of your diet-protein, carbohydrates, and fat-which provide your body with energy.

Concept note-3: -There are six classes of essential nutrients required for the body to function and maintain overall health. These six classes of essential nutrients are: carbohydrates, lipids (fats), proteins, water, vitamins, and minerals.
