What is Metabolism?

(A) ** A process where the body converts energy in food into energy the body can use.

(B) Element essential for good health

(C) Organic compounds control many body functions

(D) Energy providing nutrient that helps the body store some vitamins

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your metabolism involves two processes, catabolism, and anabolism. These two processes are carefully balanced to help you stay healthy: Catabolism is the process of breaking down food into simpler forms, releasing energy. Anabolism is the process of using this energy to build and repair cells in your body.

Concept note-2: -Metabolism is the process by which the body changes food and drink into energy. During this process, calories in food and drinks mix with oxygen to make the energy the body needs. Even at rest, a body needs energy for all it does.

Concept note-3: -Cellular respiration is the process by which the energy in food is converted into energy that can be used by the bodyâ€™s cells. The energy stored in glucose is transferred to ATP. The process can be summarized as: glucose + oxygen carbon dioxide + water.

Concept note-4: -(meh-TA-buh-lih-zum) The chemical changes that take place in a cell or an organism. These changes make energy and the materials cells and organisms need to grow, reproduce, and stay healthy. Metabolism also helps get rid of toxic substances.
