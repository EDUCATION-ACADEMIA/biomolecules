You encounter a patient deficient in vitamin B2 (riboflavin). She is complaining of a sore throat/mouth as well as photophobia. Which of the following is likely to be another of her complaints?

(A) ** Cheilosis

(B) Dry beriberi

(C) mental confusion

(D) paresthesia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Riboflavin deficiency can cause fatigue, swollen throat, blurred vision, and depression. It can affect the skin by causing skin crack, itching, and dermatitis around the mouth. Hyperemia and edema around the throat, liver degeneration, and hair loss can also occur along with reproductive issues.

Concept note-2: -Vitamin B2 and Health Because riboflavin assists many enzymes with various daily functions throughout the body, a deficiency can lead to health problems. Animal studies show that the brain and heart disorders and some cancers can develop from long-term riboflavin deficiency.

Concept note-3: -(Vitamin B2 Deficiency) Riboflavin deficiency usually occurs with other B vitamin deficiencies. Symptoms and signs include sore throat, lesions of the lips and mucosa of the mouth, glossitis, conjunctivitis, seborrheic dermatitis, and normochromic-normocytic anemia.

Concept note-4: -Lack of riboflavin may lead to itching and burning eyes, sensitivity of eyes to light, sore tongue, itching and peeling skin on the nose and scrotum, and sores in the mouth. Your doctor may treat this condition by prescribing riboflavin for you. Some conditions may increase your need for riboflavin.
