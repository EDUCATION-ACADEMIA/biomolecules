Which two female hormones control secondary sex characteristics?

(A) Estrogen and Oxytocin

(B) Progesterone and Oxytocin

(C) ** Estrogen and Progesterone

(D) Progesterone and Melatonin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Estradiol and progesterone are steroid hormones that prepare the body for pregnancy. Estradiol produces secondary sex characteristics in females, while both estradiol and progesterone regulate the menstrual cycle.

Concept note-2: -Estrogens are essential for the multifarious processes involved in reproduction and development of secondary sexual characteristics of the female.

Concept note-3: -Estrogen and progesterone are the sex hormones in females. Estrogen is responsible for the development of secondary sexual characters in females and progesterone is required to maintain pregnancy and delivery. Q. Secondary sexual characters in females develop in response to hormone.

Concept note-4: -Along with progesterone, estrogen plays a key role in your reproductive health. The development of secondary sex characteristics (breasts, hips, etc.), menstruation, pregnancy and menopause are all possible, in part, because of estrogen.

Concept note-5: -Secondary sexual characteristics, such as pubic and armpit hair, also start to grow when estrogen levels rise. Many organ systems, including the musculoskeletal and cardiovascular systems, and the brain are affected by estrogen.
