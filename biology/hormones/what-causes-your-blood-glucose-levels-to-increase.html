What causes your blood glucose levels to increase?

(A) Exercise

(B) Drinking water

(C) ** Eating food

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hyperglycemia, or high blood glucose, occurs when there is too much sugar in the blood. This happens when your body has too little insulin (the hormone that transports glucose into the blood), or if your body canâ€™t use insulin properly. The condition is most often linked with diabetes.

Concept note-2: -Mealtime and Glucose After you eat, your body starts to break down the food in your stomach into fuel. That means the amount of glucose in your blood will go up. If you have diabetes, you probably use a meter to check your blood sugar level. That tool usually uses a drop of your blood to measure your blood sugar.

Concept note-3: -If you consume more calories than you burn in a day, those calories will be converted into fat and stored in your body. The more weight you gain, the less sensitive your body becomes to insulin. As a result, your blood sugar levels can rise.
