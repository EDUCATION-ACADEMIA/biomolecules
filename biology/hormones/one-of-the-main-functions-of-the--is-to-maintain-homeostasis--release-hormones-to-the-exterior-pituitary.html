[SOURCES]
One of the main functions of the ____ is to maintain homeostasis & release hormones to the exterior pituitary

(A) Nervous system

(B) Pituitary gland

(C) ** Hypothalamus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your hypothalamus, a structure deep in your brain, acts as your body’s smart control coordinating center. Its main function is to keep your body in a stable state called homeostasis. It does its job by directly influencing your autonomic nervous system or by managing hormones.

Concept note-2: -The function of the hypothalamus is to maintain your body’s internal balance, which is known as homeostasis. To do this, the hypothalamus helps stimulate or inhibit many of your body’s key processes, including: Heart rate and blood pressure.

Concept note-3: -The endocrine system maintains homeostasis through a series of feedback loops, the most important of which are controlled by the hypothalamus interacting with the pituitary gland . Often, the hypothalamus stimulates the pituitary gland to secrete an activating hormone to another gland.

Concept note-4: -The hypothalamus maintains homeostasis by releasing hormones as well as by stimulating certain cells. The posterior region of the hypothalamus is involved in temperature regulation because it can trigger shivering in response to cold temperatures which helps with temperature homeostasis.

Concept note-5: -What Is the Hypothalamus? The hypothalamus is a gland in your brain that controls your hormone system. It releases hormones to another part of your brain called the pituitary gland, which sends hormones out to your different organs.
