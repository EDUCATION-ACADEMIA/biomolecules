How does gibberellin increase the size of fruit?

(A) Allows them to take in more water

(B) The plant synthesises sucrose more

(C) ** The distance between each fruit is increases

(D) Increases phloem concentration to transport more sugars to fruit.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A model integrating the role of auxin and gibberellin in tomato fruit set. The levels of both hormones increase after pollination, resulting in the activation of auxin and gibberellin response genes, which, in turn, will trigger fruit growth by regulating cell division and cell expansion.

Concept note-2: -Gibberellins are tetracyclic diterpenoid hormones that stimulate aspects of plant development, such as root and shoot elongation, seed germination, flower transition, and fruit set and expansion (1). They stimulate these processes by disrupting proteins from the DELLA family of negative regulators (2, 3).

Concept note-3: -Gibberellic acid increases fruit firmness, fruit size, and delays maturity of ‘sweetheart’ sweet cherry.

Concept note-4: -Fruit growth depends on highly coordinated hormonal activities. The phytohormone gibberellin (GA) promotes growth by triggering degradation of the growth-repressing DELLA proteins; however, the extent to which such proteins contribute to GA-mediated fruit development remains to be clarified.

Concept note-5: -Potassium is the key driver of fruit size and alongside nitrogen is required in large quantities throughout fruit development and growth. However, too much nitrogen at a late growth stage can restrict fruit size; therefore a balanced nutrient application is crucial. Phosphorus can restrict fruit growth too.
