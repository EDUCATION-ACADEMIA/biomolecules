What antibiotics is used to control worms

(A) Dichlorvos

(B) Penicillin

(C) ** Antihelmtics

(D) Poloxidine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Macrocyclic lactones; avermectins and milbemycins; ivermectin and moxidectin.

Concept note-2: -Anthelmintics are a group of antiparasitic antibiotics that treat infections by parasitic worms or helminths. They are roughly divided into two groups: vermifuges, which stun helminths; and vermicides, which kill them.

Concept note-3: -Anthelmintics are drugs used for treating parasitic infections. They kill parasites by: Binding to nerves and muscle cells and causing paralysis and eventually death of the parasite. Blocking the transport of glucose by the cells and thus causing paralysis of the parasite.

Concept note-4: -ALBENDAZOLE is an antiparasitic. It is used to treat infections of tapeworms or other parasites. Emverm (mebendazole) is used to treat adults and children 2 years of age and older with intestinal worm infections caused by pinworm, whipworm, roundworm, or hookworm. PRAZIQUANTEL is an anti-infective.
