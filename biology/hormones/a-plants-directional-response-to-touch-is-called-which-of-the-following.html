A plants directional response to touch is called which of the following?

(A) Phototropism

(B) ** Thigmotropism

(C) Gravitropism

(D) None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The movement of the plant due to touch is called thigmotropic movement. Thigmotropism is a directional growth movement which occurs as a mechanosensory response to a touch stimulus.

Concept note-2: -Thigmotropism. Thigmotropism is when plants grow in response to touch or contact with objects. For example, vines have small tendrils that curl around walls or trellises.

Concept note-3: -Thigmotropism is plant growth response to touch. An example of this tropism is the curling of a vine tendril around objects that it touches.

Concept note-4: -The correct option is D. Thigmotropism. The movement of a plant due to touch is called thigmotropic movement (Thigmotropism).

Concept note-5: -An example of negative thigmotropism is the growth of roots underneath the soil. When an elongating root comes in contact with an object, e.g. rock, it grows away from the object. The drooping of leaflets of Mimosa pudicawhen touched is not considered thigmotropism but a nastic movement.
