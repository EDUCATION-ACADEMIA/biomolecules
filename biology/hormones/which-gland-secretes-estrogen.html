Which gland secretes estrogen?

(A) Anterior pituitary

(B) Adrenal medulla

(C) ** Ovaries

(D) Pineal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your ovaries make most of your estrogen during your reproductive years. Your adrenal glands (the glands on your kidneys) and adipose tissue (body fat) secrete estrogen, too.

Concept note-2: -What is estrogen? Estrogens are a group of hormones that play an important role in the normal sexual and reproductive development in women. They are also sex hormones. The womanâ€™s ovaries make most estrogen hormones, although the adrenal glands and fat cells also make small amounts of the hormones.

Concept note-3: -Estrogens are produced primarily by the ovaries. They are released by the follicles on the ovaries and are also secreted by the corpus luteum after the egg has been released from the follicle and from the placenta.

Concept note-4: -Your ovaries secrete estrogen and progesterone. These hormones play an important role in reproductive development and menstruation. Estrogen production is highest in the first half of your menstrual cycle before ovulation.
