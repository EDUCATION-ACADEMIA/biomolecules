Which part of a plant produces the auxin that allows a plant to respond to light and gravity?

(A) ** Shoot tip

(B) ** Root tip

(C) Stem

(D) Leaf

(E) Tubers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Auxins are a family of plant hormones . An example of a specific auxin is IAA, which is known as Indole acetic acid. They are mostly made in the tips of the growing stems and roots, which are known as apical meristems, and can diffuse to other parts of the stems or roots.

Concept note-2: -Auxin is made in the tip region of the plant stem which is made up of meristematic tissue and this hormone promotes the growth of the plant.

Concept note-3: -Auxin promotes cell elongation, causing the plant to grow more on the shady side and bend in the direction of the light source.

Concept note-4: -Auxins are mostly made in the tips of the shoots and roots, and can diffuse to other parts of the shoots or roots. They change the rate of elongation in plant cells, controlling how long they become.

Concept note-5: -Auxins are a powerful growth hormone produced naturally by plants. They are found in shoot and root tips and promote cell division, stem and root growth.
