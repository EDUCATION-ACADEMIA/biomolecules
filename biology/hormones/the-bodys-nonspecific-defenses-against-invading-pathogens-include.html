The body’s nonspecific defenses against invading pathogens include

(A) antibiotics.

(B) ** mucus, sweat and tears.

(C) antibodies.

(D) Killer T cells.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Skin, tears and mucus are part of the first line of defence in fighting infection. They help to protect us against invading pathogens. You have beneficial bacteria growing on your skin, in your bowel and other places in the body (such as the mouth and the gut) that stop other harmful bacteria from taking over.

Concept note-2: -Nonspecific defense mechanisms include the skin, mucous membranes, secretions, excretions, enzymes, inflammatory responses, genetic factors, hormonal responses, nutritional status, behavior patterns, and the presence of other diseases.

Concept note-3: -Nonspecific defenses include anatomic barriers, inhibitors, phagocytosis, fever, inflammation, and IFN.

Concept note-4: -Among the nonspecific chemical defenses of the body are the secretions of lubricating glands. The tears and saliva contain the enzyme lysozyme, which breaks down the peptidoglycan of the cell wall of Gram-positive bacteria.

Concept note-5: -NON SPECIFIC DEFENSES: Skin and Mucous membranes, antimicrobial chemicals, natural killer cells, phagocytosis, inflammation and fever.
