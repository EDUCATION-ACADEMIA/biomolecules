Which of the following are true of steroid hormones?I. they diffuse across the plasma membraneII. they activate enzymesIII. their hormone-receptor complex travels to the nucleus

(A) I and II only

(B) ** I and III only

(C) II and III only

(D) I, II and III

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: A) Steroid hormones are derived from cholesterol and can pass right into a cell to have effects is the TRUE statement.

Concept note-2: -Steroid hormones are generally thought to pass easily across a plasma membrane into a cell, interacting once inside with soluble nuclear receptors, but recent experiments have demonstrated the importance of membrane-bound receptors in mediating the activity and the metabolism of steroid hormones.

Concept note-3: -Lipid-derived (soluble) hormones such as steroid hormones diffuse across the membranes of the endocrine cell. Once outside the cell, they bind to transport proteins that keep them soluble in the bloodstream.

Concept note-4: -(a) The classical model. The steroid hormone dissociates from its plasma carrier protein and diffuses across the cell membrane. After gaining entry to the cell, the free hormone binds to an intracellular receptor and alters gene transcription.
