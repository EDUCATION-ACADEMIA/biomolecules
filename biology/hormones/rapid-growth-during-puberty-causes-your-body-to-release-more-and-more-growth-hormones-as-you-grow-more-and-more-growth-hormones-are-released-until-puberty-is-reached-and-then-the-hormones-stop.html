HORMONES

[SOURCES]
Rapid growth during puberty causes your body to release more and more growth hormones. As you grow, more and more growth hormones are released until puberty is reached, and then the hormones stop.

(A) ** Positive Feedback Response

(B) Negative Feedback Loop

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The age at which the sex hormones (or gametes) begin to be produced, and the boy and the girl become sexually mature (able to reproduce) is called puberty. When the brain begins to release a hormone called gonadotropin-releasing hormone, puberty begins.

Concept note-2: -Pubertal growth results from increased sex steroid and growth hormone (GH) secretion. Estrogens appear to play an important role in the regulation of pubertal growth in both girls and boys.

Concept note-3: -Height increase averages out to be about 6 centimeters (2.4 inches) per year throughout childhood. Then there is a period of slow growth right before puberty. Once puberty starts, there is a sharp increase in growth of about 8 centimeters/year.

Concept note-4: -Human growth hormone, also known as hGH and somatotropin, is a natural hormone your pituitary gland makes and releases that acts on many parts of the body to promote growth in children.
