HORMONES

[SOURCES]
Does the following apply to steroid hormones only, peptide hormones only, both or none?"Forms a hormone-receptor complex".

(A) steroid hormones

(B) peptide hormones

(C) ** both

(D) none

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hormones are derived from amino acids or lipids. Amine hormones originate from the amino acids tryptophan or tyrosine. Larger amino acid hormones include peptides and protein hormones. Steroid hormones are derived from cholesterol.

Concept note-2: -Chemically, hormones may be classified as either proteins or steroids. All of the hormones in the human body, except the sex hormones and those from the adrenal cortex, are proteins or protein derivatives.

Concept note-3: -Steroid hormones bind receptors in the cell nucleus and in the cell membrane. The most widely studied class of steroid hormone receptors are the nuclear receptors, named for their function as ligand-dependent transcription factors in the cell nucleus.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: A) Steroid hormones are derived from cholesterol and can pass right into a cell to have effects is the TRUE statement.
