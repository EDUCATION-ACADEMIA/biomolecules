HORMONES

[SOURCES]
pg 120 What do we call a chemical that effects how plants grow and develop?

(A) ** hormone

(B) auxin

(C) photoperiodism

(D) cnl

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The chemical substances that control the growth in plants are called ‘plant hormones ’, also known as Phytohormones. Auxins, gibberellins and cytokinins are the plant hormones which promote growth of plants.

Concept note-2: -Auxin and cytokinin are critical growth hormones in plant development and are naturally present within the plant at variable concentrations throughout the season.

Concept note-3: -Hormones respond to increased sunlight competition by stimulating increased stem elongation. Increased competition can cause plants to put more energy into stem elongation growth versus expanded leaf area.

Concept note-4: -Hormones shape the plant and affect seed growth, time of flowering, sex of flowers, and senescence of leaves and fruits. They affect which tissues grow upward and which grow downward and even plant death. Hormones are vital to plant growth and lacking them, plants would be mostly a mass of undifferentiated cells.

Concept note-5: -The plant hormones are auxin, gibberellin, cytokinin, abscisic acid and ethylene.
