The process by which an organism’s internal environment is kept stable in spite of changes in the external environment.

(A) Positive Feedback

(B) Negative Feedback

(C) ** Homeostasis

(D) Targeted Cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Homeostasis has become the central unifying concept of physiology and is defined as a self-regulating process by which an organism can maintain internal stability while adjusting to changing external conditions.

Concept note-2: -Homeostasis refers to the ability of an organism to maintain the internal environment of the body within limits that allow it to survive.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: In homeostasis the internal environment is the living system. The internal environment can be the inside of an organelle, a cell, the inside of our body, or within a population of organisms or an entire ecosystem.

Concept note-4: -Homeostasis is maintained by a series of control mechanisms functioning at the organ, tissue or cellular level. These control mechanisms include substrate supply, activation or inhibition of individual enzymes and receptors, synthesis and degradation of enzymes, and compartmentalization.

Concept note-5: -Homeostasis, in a general sense, refers to stability, balance, or equilibrium. Physiologically, it is the body’s attempt to maintain a constant and balanced internal environment, which requires persistent monitoring and adjustments as conditions change.
