What is the optimum temperature for most enzymes in humans

(A) 0 C

(B) 20 C

(C) ** 37C

(D) 80 C

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In this article I describe the relevance of a temperature near 37 degrees C to the activities of all enzymes in man, intracellular and extracellular. I conclude that 37 degrees C is to be favored as the temperature for assaying all parameters in human biochemistry affected by temperature.

Concept note-2: -Enzymes are highly temperature sensitive, and are not functional at low temperatures and undergo denaturation at very high temperatures. Most of the enzymes in human body function optimally at the physiological temperature that is 37∘C.

Concept note-3: -This optimal temperature is usually around human body temperature (37.5 oC) for the enzymes in human cells. Above this temperature the enzyme structure begins to break down (denature) since at higher temperatures intra-and intermolecular bonds are broken as the enzyme molecules gain even more kinetic energy.

Concept note-4: -Scientists have found the reason why our body temperature is 37°C. Apparently it’s the perfect balance, as it’s warm enough to prevent fungal infection but not so hot that we need to eat nonstop to maintain our metabolism. Scientists have always wondered why advanced mammals are so hot compared to other animals.

Concept note-5: -Optimum temperature is the temperature at which enzyme activity is maximum. In humans, this temperature is 37.5oC that is same as the normal body temperature.
