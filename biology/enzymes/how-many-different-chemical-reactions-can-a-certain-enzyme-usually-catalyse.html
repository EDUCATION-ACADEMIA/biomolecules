How many different chemical reactions can a certain enzyme usually catalyse?

(A) a large number

(B) about 10

(C) ** only 1

(D) 3 to 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In addition, enzymes are highly specific in their action; that is, each enzyme catalyzes only one type of reaction in only one compound or a group of structurally related compounds.

Concept note-2: -Types of catalytic reactions Catalysts can be divided into two main types-heterogeneous and homogeneous. In a heterogeneous reaction, the catalyst is in a different phase from the reactants. In a homogeneous reaction, the catalyst is in the same phase as the reactants.

Concept note-3: –The same catalyst can’t be used for more than one reaction. . Either less or more pH, the activity of enzymes gets declined. Hence, one enzyme for one reaction.

Concept note-4: -Enzymes are proteins, which have a specific 3D tertiary structure, with a specifically shaped active site. The active site can only bind one substrate to form an enzyme-substrate complex, so can therefore only catalyse one reaction.

Concept note-5: -One enzyme can be used for many different types of chemical reactions. Enzymes are highly specific for the substrate.
