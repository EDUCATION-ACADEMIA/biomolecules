Where is protease produced? (you can select more than one)

(A) Salivary Glands

(B) ** Small Intestine

(C) ** Pancreas

(D) ** Stomach

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Proteases are released by the pancreas into the proximal small intestine, where they mix with proteins already denatured by gastric secretions and break them down into amino acids, the building blocks of protein, which will eventually be absorbed and used throughout the body.

Concept note-2: -Protease is produced in the stomach, pancreas, and small intestine. Most of the chemical reactions occur in the stomach and small intestine. In the stomach, pepsin is the main digestive enzyme attacking proteins. Several other pancreatic enzymes go to work when protein molecules reach the small intestine.

Concept note-3: -Protease enzymes are produced in your stomach, pancreas and small intestine.

Concept note-4: -Specific cells within the gastric lining, known as chief cells, release pepsin in an inactive form, or zymogen form, called pepsinogen.
