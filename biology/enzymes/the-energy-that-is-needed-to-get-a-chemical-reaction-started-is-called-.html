The energy that is needed to get a chemical reaction started is called ____

(A) exothermic energy

(B) ** activation energy

(C) activation catalyst

(D) endothermic energy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Activation energy is the minimum energy required to cause a process (such as a chemical reaction) to occur.

Concept note-2: -Activation Energy is the minimum amount of energy needed to start a chemical reaction. All chemical reactions need a certain amount of activation energy to get started. Usually, once a few molecules react, the rest will quickly follow. The first few reactions provide the activation energy for more molecules to react.

Concept note-3: -Activation Energy Even reactions that release energy need a boost of energy in order to begin. The energy needed to start a chemical reaction is called activation energy. Activation energy is like the push a child needs to start going down a playground slide.
