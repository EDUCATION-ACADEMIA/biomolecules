Name the monomer of proteins.

(A) ** amino acids

(B) polypeptides

(C) nucleotides

(D) monosaccharides

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -For example, proteins are composed of monomers called amino acids. They are linked together to form a polypeptide chain, which folds into a three dimensional (3D) structure to constitute a functional protein (Figure 1).

Concept note-2: -Amino acids do not have single monomers. They are basic compounds bound to the same molecule, with an amino group and a group of carboxylic acids. Instead, amino acids are monomers of proteins, long chains of amino acids that are bound together by amide bonds.

Concept note-3: -Amino acids are the monomers that make up proteins. Specifically, a protein is made up of one or more linear chains of amino acids, each of which is called a polypeptide.

Concept note-4: -There are four main types of monomer, including sugars, amino acids, fatty acids, and nucleotides.
