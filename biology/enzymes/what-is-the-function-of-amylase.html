What is the function of amylase?

(A) to break down protein into fatty acids

(B) to break down carbohydrates into amino acids

(C) ** to break down starch into glucose

(D) to break down protein into glucose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amylases digest starch into smaller molecules, ultimately yielding maltose, which in turn is cleaved into two glucose molecules by maltase. Starch comprises a significant portion of the typical human diet for most nationalities.

Concept note-2: -The Role of the Amylase Enzyme The end goal of amylase is to break down carbohydrates into simple sugars that the body can use for energy, and this starts in the mouth. As food is chewed and mixed with saliva, amylase starts working to break down food into smaller molecules (1).

Concept note-3: -The digestive enzyme amylase secreted by the pancreas and some salivary glands is responsible for the initial process of digestion of dietary starch.

Concept note-4: -Attacking Starch It takes starch chains and breaks them into smaller pieces with two or three glucose units.
