The monomer of a protein is called ____

(A) ** an amino acid

(B) a monosaccharide

(C) a fatty acid

(D) a nucleotide

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amino acids are the monomers that make up proteins. Specifically, a protein is made up of one or more linear chains of amino acids, each of which is called a polypeptide.

Concept note-2: -For example, proteins are composed of monomers called amino acids.

Concept note-3: -The name “amino acid” is derived from the amino group and carboxyl-acid-group in their basic structure. There are 21 amino acids present in proteins, each with a specific R group or side chain.

Concept note-4: -Amino acids do not have single monomers. They are basic compounds bound to the same molecule, with an amino group and a group of carboxylic acids. Instead, amino acids are monomers of proteins, long chains of amino acids that are bound together by amide bonds.

Concept note-5: -A protein consists of one or more chains of amino acids (called polypeptides) whose sequence is encoded in a gene.
