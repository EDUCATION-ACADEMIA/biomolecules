How many oxygen atoms will be produced at the end of a reaction starting with 6 oxygen atoms?

(A) 12

(B) 3

(C) ** 6

(D) 0

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: 24 oxygen atoms are in the products of the reaction. Overall, the process requires 6 molecules of oxygen for every molecule of glucose. The chemical formula is 6O2 + C6H12O6–> 6CO2 + 6H2O + ATP energy.

Concept note-2: -Answer. Answer: 24 oxygen atoms are in the products of the reaction. Thus chemical equations are balanced.

Concept note-3: -There are two oxygen atoms in the reactants and two atoms of oxygen in the product. The law of conservation of matter has been satisfied.

Concept note-4: -The sixth type of chemical reaction is an acid-base reaction.
