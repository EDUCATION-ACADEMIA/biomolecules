Lipase breaks Fats into what?

(A) Amino acids

(B) Sugars

(C) Fatty glycerol and acids

(D) ** Fatty acids and glycerol

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lipase converts emulsified fats into fatty acids and glycerol.

Concept note-2: -Lipases hydrolyze triglycerides (fats) into their component fatty acid and glycerol molecules. Initial lipase digestion occurs in the lumen (interior) of the small intestine.

Concept note-3: -Lipase has the crucial function of breaking fats down into fatty acids and glycerol, products that can be carried in water-based fluids like blood and lymph. These components are used throughout the body to provide energy (1). There are some types of lipase with more specific roles as well.

Concept note-4: -Lipases break down lipids into fatty acids and glycerol. They are made in the pancreas and secreted into the small intestine.

Concept note-5: -Lipase is an enzyme the body uses to break down fats in food so they can be absorbed in the intestines. Lipase is produced in the pancreas, mouth, and stomach.
