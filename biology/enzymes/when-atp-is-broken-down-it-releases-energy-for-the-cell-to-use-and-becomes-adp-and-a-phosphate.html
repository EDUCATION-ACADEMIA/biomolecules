When ATP is broken down, it releases energy for the cell to use and becomes ADP and a phosphate

(A) ** ATP → ADP + P + energy

(B) ** when energy is given off this is an exothermic reaction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If a cell needs to spend energy to accomplish a task, the ATP molecule splits off one of its three phosphates, becoming ADP (Adenosine di-phosphate) + phosphate. The energy holding that phosphate molecule is now released and available to do work for the cell.

Concept note-2: -When one phosphate group is removed by breaking a phosphoanhydride bond in a process called hydrolysis, energy is released, and ATP is converted to adenosine diphosphate (ADP). Likewise, energy is also released when a phosphate is removed from ADP to form adenosine monophosphate (AMP).

Concept note-3: -ATP to ADP – Energy Release When the bond connecting the phosphate is broken, energy is released.

Concept note-4: -The hydrolysis of one ATP molecule releases 7.3 kcal/mol of energy (∆G = −7.3 kcal/mol of energy).
