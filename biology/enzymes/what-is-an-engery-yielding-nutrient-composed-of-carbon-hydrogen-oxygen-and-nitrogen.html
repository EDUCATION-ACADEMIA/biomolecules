What is an engery-yielding nutrient composed of carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen?

(A) carbohydrate

(B) lipid (fat)

(C) ** protein

(D) none of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Protein is an energy-yielding nutrient composed of carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen. Proteins differs from carbohydrates and fats because of the presence of nitrogen. They are the building blocks of all protein molecules are amino acids. Protein is vital to the optimal growth and development of kids.

Concept note-2: -Carbohydrates, fats, and proteins are also referred to as energy-yielding macronutrients becausethey supply the body with energy.

Concept note-3: -Which Nutrient Provides the Most Energy? Looking at the energy densities of each of the energy-yielding nutrients, fat has 9 Calories/gram, carbohydrates have 4 Calories/gram, and proteins have 4 Calories/gram. The most energy rich nutrient is fat.

Concept note-4: -carbohydrate A nutrient made of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen and that supplies energy.

Concept note-5: -The option that is not an energy-yielding nutrient is b. Nitrogen. Nutrients are responsible for entering a cell and then, through cellular metabolic processes, are used for production of energy.
