Question:The energy needed to get a reaction started is the

(A) chemical energy.

(B) ** activation energy.

(C) adhesion energy.

(D) cohesion energy.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Activation energy is defined as the minimum energy required by a reacting molecule to be converted into a product. The amount of activation energy is inversely proportional to the rate of reaction. Activation energy is required to start a reaction.

Concept note-2: -All chemical reactions, including exothermic reactions, need activation energy to get started. Activation energy is needed so reactants can move together, overcome forces of repulsion, and start breaking bonds. activation energy: Energy needed to start a chemical reaction.

Concept note-3: -In chemistry and physics, activation energy is the minimum amount of energy that must be provided for compounds to result in a chemical reaction. The activation energy (Ea) of a reaction is measured in joules per mole (J/mol), kilojoules per mole (kJ/mol) or kilocalories per mole (kcal/mol).
