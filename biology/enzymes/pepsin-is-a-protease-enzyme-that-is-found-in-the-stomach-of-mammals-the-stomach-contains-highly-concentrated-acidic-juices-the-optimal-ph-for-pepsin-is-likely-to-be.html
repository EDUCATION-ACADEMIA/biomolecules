Pepsin is a protease enzyme that is found in the stomach of mammals. The stomach contains highly concentrated acidic juices. The optimal pH for pepsin is likely to be

(A) more than 7

(B) 7

(C) ** less than 3

(D) between 5 and 8

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The parietal cells in the stomach lining release hydrochloric acid, which lowers the stomach’s pH. Pepsin is activated by a low pH (1.5 to 2). Pepsin works best at a pH of 1.8, which is very acidic.

Concept note-2: -Pepsin is an aspartic protease that acts in food digestion in the mammal stomach. An optimal pH of around 2 allows pepsin to operate in its natural acidic environment, while at neutral pH the protein is denatured.

Concept note-3: -The optimum pH for vertebrate pepsin activity lies between 1 and 2, which is acidic but within the normal pH range observed in the stomach following stimulation of acid secretion. Pepsin is inactive above a pH of about 4.5.

Concept note-4: -Pepsin has an optimal activity temperature of   37°C and a pH of 1.6–2.5 depending on the substrate.

Concept note-5: -Pepsin benefits digestion by breaking down each protein (via enzyme activity) into a smaller peptide or amino acid. This allows the nutrients that the food proteins contain to be more readily absorbed and assimilated by the small intestine.
