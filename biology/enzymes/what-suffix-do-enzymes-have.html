What suffix do enzymes have?

(A) -ose

(B) -ide

(C) -ate

(D) ** -ase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The suffix “-ase” indicates an enzyme. In the procedure of naming an enzyme, suffix “-ase” is added after the name of the substrate specific to an enzyme. This identifies the class of an enzyme catalyzing a specific reaction.

Concept note-2: -The suffix-ase is used in biochemistry to form names of enzymes.

Concept note-3: -The suffix “-ase” is used to signify an enzyme. In enzyme naming, an enzyme is denoted by adding-ase to the end of the name of the substrate on which the enzyme acts. It is also used to identify a particular class of enzymes that catalyze a specific type of reaction.

Concept note-4: –ase. A suffix used to form the names of enzymes. It is often added to the name of the compound that the enzyme breaks down, as in lactase, which breaks down lactose.

Concept note-5: -Examples of the few enzymes without-ase names include: peptin, trypsin, and chrymotrypsin.
