Enzymes lower activation energy required.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Enzymes are biological catalysts. Catalysts lower the activation energy for reactions. The lower the activation energy for a reaction, the faster the rate. Thus enzymes speed up reactions by lowering activation energy.

Concept note-2: -Enzymes allow activation energies to be lowered. Enzymes lower the activation energy necessary to transform a reactant into a product. On the left is a reaction that is not catalyzed by an enzyme (red), and on the right is one that is (green).

Concept note-3: -This statement about enzymes is true: Enzymes accelerate reactions by lowering the activation energy. Enzymes are proteins whose three-dimensional form is key to their function. Enzymes do not alter the overall change in free energy for a reaction.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The only incorrect statement in this set of options is A; Catalysts raise a reactionâ€™s activation energy. This is precisely the opposite of what a catalyst (such as an enzyme) does in a reaction.
