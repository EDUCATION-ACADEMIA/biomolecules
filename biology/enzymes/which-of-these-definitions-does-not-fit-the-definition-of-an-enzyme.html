Which of these definitions does NOT fit the definition of an enzyme?

(A) An enzyme is a protein that acts as a catalyst

(B) ** An enzyme is a substance that acts as a lock with the substrate as a key

(C) An enzyme is a substance that lowers the activation energy of reacting molecules and creates an alternate pathway for the reaction

(D) An enzyme is a substance that is induced to fit the shape of the substrate molecules

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Enzyme activity is regulated. Enzyme activity is not regulated and thus it is not characteristic of an enzyme.

Concept note-2: -Listen to pronunciation. (EN-zime) A protein that speeds up chemical reactions in the body.

Concept note-3: -A Lock and Key analogy may be used to describe the fundamental action of a single substrate enzyme. In this case, the enzyme is the lock, and the substrate is the key. Only the correct size key, which is the substrate, enters the keyhole, which is the active site of the lock, which is the enzyme.

Concept note-4: -So, the correct answer is ‘Enzymes are used up in reactions’.
