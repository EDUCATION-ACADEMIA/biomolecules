What phase does DNA replicate in

(A) exothermic

(B) telophate

(C) ** S phase

(D) metaphase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -G1 is followed by S phase (synthesis), during which DNA replication takes place. The completion of DNA synthesis is followed by the G2 phase (gap 2), during which cell growth continues and proteins are synthesized in preparation for mitosis.

Concept note-2: -The G2 phase follows the S-phase. In this phase, the cell prepares for mitotic division. Before DNA replication, the chromosome number remains the same. After the S-phase, the DNA replicates, and the number of chromosomes doubles.

Concept note-3: -S phase is the period of wholesale DNA synthesis during which the cell replicates its genetic content; a normal diploid somatic cell with a 2N complement of DNA at the beginning of S phase acquires a 4N complement of DNA at its end.

Concept note-4: -S phase (Synthesis Phase) is the phase of the cell cycle in which DNA is replicated, occurring between G1 phase and G2 phase. Since accurate duplication of the genome is critical to successful cell division, the processes that occur during S-phase are tightly regulated and widely conserved.

Concept note-5: -S phase. In S phase, the cell synthesizes a complete copy of the DNA in its nucleus. It also duplicates a microtubule-organizing structure called the centrosome. The centrosomes help separate DNA during M phase.
