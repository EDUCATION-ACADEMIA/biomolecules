Which statement is FALSE about enzymes?

(A) Enzymes are biological catalysts.

(B) Enzymes are essential to many of the processes in our body.

(C) ** Enzymes can only break down larger molecules into smaller ones.

(D) Enzymes are specific to the substrates they react with.

(E) Enzymes are not used up after catalyzing a reaction.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An enzyme provides an alternate pathway having lower activation energy. The statement (C) is false with respect to an enzymeâ€™s ability to catalyses a reaction.

Concept note-2: -Enzymes provide support for many important processes within the body. Some examples include: The digestive system: Enzymes help the body break down larger complex molecules into smaller molecules, such as glucose, so that the body can use them as fuel.

Concept note-3: -Some enzymes help break large molecules into smaller pieces that are more easily absorbed by the body. Other enzymes help bind two molecules together to produce a new molecule. Enzymes are highly selective catalysts, meaning that each enzyme only speeds up a specific reaction.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The breakdown of large molecules by enzymes, through the addition of water is called a hydrolysis reaction. This type of reaction breaks down large polymers (such as proteins) into smaller fragments through breaking of the bonds. Energy release also accompanies this type of reaction.
