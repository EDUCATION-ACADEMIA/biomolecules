is more energy in products than reactants endothermic or exothermic

(A) ** Endothermic

(B) Exothermic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In exothermic reactions, more energy is released when the bonds are formed in the products than is used to break the bonds in the reactants.

Concept note-2: -In the case of an exothermic reaction, the reactants are at a higher energy level as compared to the products, as shown below in the energy diagram. In other words, the products are more stable than the reactants.

Concept note-3: -Energy Diagrams In endothermic reactions, the reactants have higher bond energy (stronger bonds) than the products.

Concept note-4: -Exothermic reactions give off energy, so energy is a product. Endothermic reactions require energy, so energy is a reactant.
