Starch is the stored form of energy in plants. Which of these structural arrangements does starch represent?

(A) Polypeptide chain of amino acids

(B) ** Repeating units of a simple sugar

(C) Polymer of fatty acids attached to phosphate groups

(D) Polymer of nitrogenous bases attached to phosphate groups

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Starch molecules are polymer with repeating glucose units.

Concept note-2: -Starch is comprised principally of two glucose polymers, amylose and amylopectin. Both polymers are made up of d-glucose units in cyclic pyranose form, linked through glycosidic bonds between carbons 1 and 4 of adjacent glucose monomers (Figure 1). The asymmetric carbon 1 is in rather than configuration.

Concept note-3: -Starch is a glucose polymer in which glucopyranose units are bonded by alpha-linkages. It is made up of a mixture of amylose (15–20%) and amylopectin (80–85%).

Concept note-4: -For example starch is poly(glucose) formed by repeating glucose units. Glycosidic bond: A covalent bond joining a sugar molecule to another molecule. Starch is formed by successive glycosidic bonds between glucose molecules forming polymer chains.
