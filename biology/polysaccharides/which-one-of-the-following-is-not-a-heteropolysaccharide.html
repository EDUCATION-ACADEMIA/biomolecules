Which one of the following is not a heteropolysaccharide?

(A) Hyaluronic acid

(B) Chondroitin sulfate A

(C) Dermatan sulfate

(D) ** Inulin

(E) Pectin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -D. Inulin. Hint: In homopolysaccharides, all sugar components are the same. This variety includes cellulose, starch, glycogen, and para-mil.

Concept note-2: -Inulin is a heteropolymer made of (2, 1) linked glucosyl and repetitive fructosyl units. Hence, Inulin is not a polymer of just glucose.

Concept note-3: -Homopolysaccharides or homoglycans are those complex carbohydrates which are formed by polymerisation of only one type of monosaccharide monomers. For example, starch, glycogen, inulin, cellulose, chitin, etc. Agar, pectin, hyaluronic acid, heparin, etc., are hetero polysaccharides.

Concept note-4: -Some examples of heteropolysaccharides:i) Peptidoglycans, ii) Agarose, and iii) Glycosaminoglycans (GAGs). Peptidoglycan (murein) is a part of the bacterial cell wall which is found on the outside of almost all of the bacteria’s cytoplasmic membrane.
