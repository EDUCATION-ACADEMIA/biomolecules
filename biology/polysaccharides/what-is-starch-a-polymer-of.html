What is starch a polymer of?

(A) ** Glucose

(B) Lipids

(C) Carbohydrates

(D) Minerals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Starch is comprised principally of two glucose polymers, amylose and amylopectin . Both polymers are made up of d-glucose units in cyclic pyranose form, linked through glycosidic bonds between carbons 1 and 4 of adjacent glucose monomers (Figure 1).

Concept note-2: -Starch is a semi-crystalline polysaccharide natural polymer with a complex structure. Starch consists of two-component polymers: amylose (AM) and amylopectin (AP).

Concept note-3: -Glycogen is a glucose polymer that is primarily stored in the liver and muscle and is required for glucose homeostasis during fasting.

Concept note-4: -Correct option: a Starch is a polymer of -glucoseExplanation:Starch is also know as amylum which occurs in all green plants. A molecule of starch C6H10O5 n is built of a large number of -glucose ring joined through oxygen-atom.

Concept note-5: -Starch is a natural polymer, or polysaccharide, meaning that it is a long chain comprising one type of molecule. Starch consists of glucose molecules. It can occur in two forms: amylose and amylopectin.
