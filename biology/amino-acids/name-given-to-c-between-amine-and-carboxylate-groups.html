AMINO ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Name given to C between amine and carboxylate groups

(A) center C

(B) ** alpha carbon

(C) side chain C

(D) Cindy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The “Alpha Carbon” Is The Carbon Adjacent To The Carbonyl The functional group C=O is called a “carbonyl”. The carbon itself is called the “carbonyl carbon”, and the oxygen is called “the carbonyl oxygen”.

Concept note-2: -Chemically, an amino acid is a molecule that has a carboxylic acid group and an amine group that are each attached to a carbon atom called the carbon. Each of the 20 amino acids has a specific side chain, known as an R group, that is also attached to the carbon.

Concept note-3: -The alpha carbon is the first carbon that attaches to a functional group, in this case a carbonyl. It is not the carbon that is itself part of that carbonyl.

Concept note-4: -The alpha carbon (C) in organic molecules refers to the first carbon atom that attaches to a functional group, such as a carbonyl. An carbon is the one which is attached directly to the functional group. and the hydrogen attached to the carbon is called hydrogen.
