Which test will you use for the presence of protein?

(A) Iodine test

(B) Benedicts test

(C) Ethanol Emulsion test

(D) ** Biurets test

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The biuret test is a chemical test that can be used to check for the presence of peptide bonds in a given analyte. Therefore, the biuret test can also be used to gauge the amount of protein present in the analyte.

Concept note-2: -Biuret Reagents Copper sulphate (CuSO4), sodium hydroxide (NaOH), and sodium-potassium tartrate are used to make the biuret reagent (also known as Rochelle salt).

Concept note-3: -Biuret reagent is an alkaline solution of 1% CuSO4, copper sulfate. The violet color is a positive test for the presence of protein, and the intensity of the color is proportional to the number of peptide bonds in the solution.
