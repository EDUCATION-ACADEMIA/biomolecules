AMINO ACIDS

[SOURCES]
This color is produced upon the reaction of ninhydrin solution with the amino acids in paper chromatography.

(A) Methy red

(B) Bromocresol green

(C) ** Ruhemann purple

(D) Crystal violet

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The reaction of ninhydrin with an amino acid results in the creation of Ruhemann’s purple, a dark purple compound.

Concept note-2: -The reaction between ninhydrin and an amino acid, leading to the production of a dark purple compound known as Ruhemann’s purple.

Concept note-3: -The ninhydrin test is a chemical test useful to identify ammonia, primary/secondary amines, or amino acids. In this test, ninhydrin reagent is added to the test material, resulting in the production of deep blue colour, also known as Ruhemann’s purple, in the presence of an amino group.

Concept note-4: -Ruhemann’s purple is formed from nitrite, ascorbate, and ninhydrin with the formation of variable amounts of ammonia. The reaction begins with the nitrosation of ninhydrin to form nitrosoninhydrin, which will release nitric oxide.

Concept note-5: -Ninhydrin (2, 2-dihydroxyindane-1, 3-dione) is an organic compound with the formula C6H4(CO)2C(OH)2. It is used to detect ammonia and amines. Upon reaction with these amines, ninhydrin gets converted into deep blue or purple derivatives, which are called Ruhemann’s purple.
