AMINO ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Are these statements about amino acids true or false?Amino acids behave as both acids and bases

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amino acids can act as both an acid and a base due to the presence of the amino and carboxyl functional groups. The pH at which a given amino acid exists in solution as a zwitterion is called the isoelectric point (pI).

Concept note-2: -Amino acids all have very similar physical properties. This statement is false. The physical properties of Amino Acids vary considerably.

Concept note-3: -All other amino acids contain four different groups. Hence all other amino acids are optically active. Therefore the correct statement is, all amino acids except glycine are optically active.

Concept note-4: -Another important feature of free amino acids is the existence of both a basic and an acidic group at the -carbon. Compounds such as amino acids that can act as either an acid or a base are called amphoteric.

Concept note-5: -Amino acids behave like salts rather than simple amines or carboxylic acids.
