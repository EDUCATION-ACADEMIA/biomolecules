The R groups of polar, uncharged amino acids are hydrophobic.

(A) TRUE

(B) ** FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Polar amino acids are hydrophilic, meaning that their side chains interact strongly with water.

Concept note-2: -Aliphatic R groups are nonpolar and hydrophobic. Hydrophobicity increases with increasing number of C atoms in the hydrocarbon chain. Although these amino acids prefer to remain inside protein molecules, alanine and glycine are ambivalent, meaning that they can be inside or outside the protein molecule.

Concept note-4: -The R-groups of hydrophilic amino acids contain electronegative atoms like O and N. Some amino acids are non-polar (hydrophobic) because their side chains are made up mostly of hydrocarbon chains.
