Which group doesn’t contain an amino acid?

(A) Methionine, Tryptophane & Lysine

(B) ** Glycine, Formalin & Proline

(C) Glutamine, Hisidine & Serine

(D) Threonine, Cysteine & Valine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Proline is unique among the standard amino acids in that it does not have both free -amino and free -carboxyl groups. Instead, its side chain forms a cyclic structure as the nitrogen atom of proline is linked to two carbon atoms.

Concept note-2: -Like all of the amino acids, glycine has two functional groups, a carboxyl group (COOH) and an amine group (NH2). It has the simplest side chain of all of the amino acids, composed of a single hydrogen atom (-H).

Concept note-3: -Although the keto group is the most versatile of the functional groups in organic chemistry, it is absent in the genetically encoded amino acids.
