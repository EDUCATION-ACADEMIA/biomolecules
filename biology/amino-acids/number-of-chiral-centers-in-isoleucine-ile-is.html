Number of chiral centers in isoleucine (Ile) is?

(A) 1

(B) ** 2

(C) 3

(D) 0

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These two amino acids, isoleucine and threonine, have in common the fact that they have two chiral centers.

Concept note-2: -In the case of isoleucine and threonine, both chiral centers must be inverted to generate the mirror image amino acids d-isoleucine and d-threonine.

Concept note-3: -Threonine has two chiral centers and therefore four possible stereoisomers.

Concept note-4: -We can observe that there are a total of two chiral centres in isoleucine at the second and third carbon atoms. Since, there are two chiral centres, we will use our formula to find out the number of optical isomers.

Concept note-5: -Ans:Thr and Ile have two chiral centers.
