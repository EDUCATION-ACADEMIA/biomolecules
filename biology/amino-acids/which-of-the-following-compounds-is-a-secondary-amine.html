Which of the following compounds is a secondary amine?

(A) CH3CH2CH2NH2

(B) (CH3CH2)3N

(C) ** CH3CH2NHCH3

(D) (CH3CH2)2NCH3

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The compound CH3CH2NHCH3 is classified as a tertiary amine.

Concept note-2: -The Two hydrogens are substituted by two methyl groups. Therefore the above amine is a secondary amine.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The amine is primary because the nitrogen atom has two hydrogen atom substituents and one carbon-containing substituent.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is N-Methylethanamine or N-Methylaminoethane.

Concept note-5: -It is an amine. 2 alkyl groups are attached to N atom, so, it is a secondary amine.
