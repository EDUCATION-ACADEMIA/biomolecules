Which of the following amino acids is acid?

(A) Histidine

(B) Arginine

(C) Lysine

(D) ** Aspartic acid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Two amino acids have acidic side chains at neutral pH. These are aspartic acid or aspartate (Asp) and glutamic acid or glutamate (Glu).

Concept note-2: -Aspartic Acid☆ Aspartic acid (or aspartate) is a non-essential amino acid, meaning that it is readily and naturally synthesized by mammals. It is one of the 20 building-block amino acids of proteins, 3-letter code is ASP, one letter code is D. The DNA codons encoding aspartic acid are GAC and GAU.

Concept note-3: -Aspartic acid, like glutamic acid, is classified as an acidic amino acid, with a pKa of 3.9, however in a peptide this is highly dependent on the local environment, and could be as high as 14. Asp is pervasive in biosynthesis. A molecular entity capable of donating a hydron to an acceptor (Br o nsted base).

Concept note-4: -The L-isomer of Asp is one of the 22 proteinogenic amino acids, i.e., the building blocks of proteins.
