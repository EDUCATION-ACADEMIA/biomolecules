What is the enzyme in the stomach that begins chemical breakdown of protein?

(A) Denaturation

(B) Hydrolysis

(C) Proteases

(D) ** Pepsin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pepsin is a stomach enzyme that serves to digest proteins found in ingested food. Gastric chief cells secrete pepsin as an inactive zymogen called pepsinogen. Parietal cells within the stomach lining secrete hydrochloric acid that lowers the pH of the stomach. A low pH (1.5 to 2) activates pepsin.

Concept note-2: -Of these five components, pepsin is the principal enzyme involved in protein digestion. It breaks down proteins into smaller peptides and amino acids that can be easily absorbed in the small intestine.

Concept note-3: -The role of enzymes Once a protein source reaches your stomach, hydrochloric acid and enzymes called proteases break it down into smaller chains of amino acids. Amino acids are joined together by peptides, which are broken by proteases.

Concept note-4: -An enzyme made in the stomach that breaks down proteins in food during digestion.

Concept note-5: -Pepsin is an endopeptidase that initiates the process of protein digestion in the stomach. Pepsin action results in the release of small peptides and amino acids (peptones) that, as noted above, stimulate the release of gastrin from antral G cells; these peptones also stimulate CCK release from duodenal I cells.
