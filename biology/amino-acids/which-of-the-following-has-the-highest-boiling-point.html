Which of the following has the highest boiling point?

(A) CH3CH2COOH

(B) CH3CH2CH2NH2

(C) ** NH2CH2COOH

(D) NH2CH2COCl

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Therefore, 1-chloropentane has the highest boiling point.

Concept note-2: -Acetic acid shows the highest boiling point among the given compound due to the intermolecular hydrogen bonding.

Concept note-3: -(c) Both CH3OH and CH3CH2OH can H-bond but CH3CH2OH has more CH bonds for greater dispersion force interactions. Therefore, CH3CH2OH has the higher boiling point.

Concept note-4: -Therefore, CH3CH2CH2NH2 has a higher boiling point and larger Hvap, while N(CH3)3 has a higher vapor pressure. N(CH3)3 exhibits hydrogen bonding while CH3CH2CH2NH2 does not, and N(CH3)3 has a greater surface area to mass ratio, which increases london dispersion forces compared to CH3CH2CH2NH2 .
