AMINO ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Basicity of α − amino acids is due to

(A) Free-COOH

(B) Free-NH2

(C) ** Carboxylate ion

(D) Ammonium ion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These are aspartic acid or aspartate (Asp) and glutamic acid or glutamate (Glu). Their side chains have carboxylic acid groups whose pKa’s are low enough to lose protons, becoming negatively charged in the process.

Concept note-2: -Some examples of basic amino acids are lysine, arginine.

Concept note-3: -An amino acid is an organic molecule that is made up of a basic amino group (−NH2), an acidic carboxyl group (−COOH), and an organic R group (or side chain) that is unique to each amino acid.

Concept note-4: --Amino acids are simple molecules that are made of a central C-atom, labelled Ca, that is bound to a primary amine group NH2 and to a carboxylic group COOH. From: The Hydrogen Bond and the Water Molecule, 2007.
