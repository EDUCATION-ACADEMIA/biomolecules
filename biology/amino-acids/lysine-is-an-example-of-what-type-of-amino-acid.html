AMINO ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Lysine is an example of what type of amino acid?

(A) Acidic Amino acid

(B) ** Basic Amino acid

(C) Neutral Amino acid

(D) It is not amino acid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lysine and arginine are the basic amino acid as they contain more amino group than acidic group.

Concept note-2: -There are three amino acids that have basic side chains at neutral pH. These are arginine (Arg), lysine (Lys), and histidine (His). Their side chains contain nitrogen and resemble ammonia, which is a base.

Concept note-3: -Lysine, or L-lysine, is an essential amino acid, meaning it is necessary for human health, but the body cannot make it. You have to get lysine from food or supplements. Amino acids like lysine are the building blocks of protein.

Concept note-4: -Lysine is an amino acid, one of the building blocks of proteins.
