AMINO ACIDS

How many essential amino acids are there?

(A) 3

(B) ** 9

(C) 12

(D) 20

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 9 essential amino acids are: histidine, isoleucine, leucine, lysine, methionine, phenylalanine, threonine, tryptophan, and valine.

Concept note-2: -Amino acids are required for the synthesis of body protein and other important nitrogen-containing compounds, such as creatine, peptide hormones, and some neurotransmitters. Although allowances are expressed as protein, a the biological requirement is for amino acids.

Concept note-3: -The essential amino acids are arginine (required for the young, but not for adults), histidine, isoleucine, leucine, lysine, methionine, phenylalanine, threonine, tryptophan, and valine.

Concept note-4: -Meat, poultry, eggs, dairy, and fish contain all nine essential amino acids. This means they are ‘complete’ sources of protein.

Concept note-5: -Of these 20 amino acids, nine amino acids are essential: Phenylalanine. Valine. Tryptophan.
