Which of the following amino acids will yield a positive result on Millon’s Test?

(A) Glycine

(B) Phenylalanine

(C) Tryptophan

(D) ** Tyrosine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -However, to solely use the qualitative test to determine the presence of this specific amino acid residi D This is because the test will yield a positive result for any compound that has a phenol structure.

Concept note-2: -Millon’s Test Millon’s test is given by any compound containing a phenolic hydroxy group. Consequently, any protein containing tyrosine will give a positive test of a pink to dark-red colour.

Concept note-3: -Millon’s test is a test specific for tyrosine, the only amino acid containing a phenol group, a hydroxyl group attached to a benzene ring.

Concept note-4: -Xanthoproteic test is used to detect amino acids containing an aromatic nucleus (tyrosine, tryptophan and phenylalanine) in a protein solution which gives yellow color nitro derivatives on heating with conc. HNO3. The aromatic benzene ring undergoes nitration to give yellow colored product.

Concept note-5: -Positive result: A positive result in the Millon’s test is demonstrated by the formation of a red or pink colored precipitate.
