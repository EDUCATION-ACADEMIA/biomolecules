____ is a molecule that has a positive charge on one atom and a negative charge on another atom but which has no net charge.

(A) Cation

(B) Isoelectric point

(C) ** Zwitterion

(D) Anion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A chemical molecule consisting of an isolated and equal number of positive and negative charged functional groups. The net charge of a zwitterion is zero.

Concept note-2: -A zwitterion is an ion that contains two functional groups. In simple terms, it is an ion possessing both positive and negative electrical charges. Therefore, zwitterions are mostly electrically neutral (the net formal charge is usually zero).

Concept note-3: -The most acidic carboxyl will be deprotonated and negatively charged, the less acidic carboxyl remains protonated and thus neutral. The pH is still too acidic for the amino group, which remains protonated and positive. This gives us a net charge of 0 and our zwitterion form.

Concept note-4: -A zwitterion is a molecule that includes both positive and negative regions of charge. The existence of amino acids as dipolar ions in a solid-state is termed as zwitterions.
