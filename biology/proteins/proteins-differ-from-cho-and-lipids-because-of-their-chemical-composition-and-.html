PROTEINS

[SOURCES]
Proteins differ from CHO and lipids because of their chemical composition and ____

(A) they are only present in meat

(B) they are the only nutrient that provides energy

(C) they are inorganic

(D) ** they contain nitrogen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Proteins are different from fats and carbohydrates due to the presence of nitrogen, carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen in them. Carbohydrates only contain carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen whereas fats contain fatty acids which contain a carboxyl group and an alkyl group, thus containing only carbon, oxygen, and hydrogen.

Concept note-2: -Carbohydrates and lipids are made of only carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen (CHO). Proteins are made of carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen (CHON). Nucleic acids such as DNA and RNA contain carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, and phosphorus (CHON P).

Concept note-3: -Carbohydrates and lipids both contain carbon (C), hydrogen (H), and oxygen (0); proteins contain these three elements plus one or more from nitrogen (N), sulphur (S) and phosphorous (P).

Concept note-4: -Proteins contain the elements carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen just as carbohydrates and lipids do, but proteins are the only macronutrient that contains nitrogen.

Concept note-5: -The difference is that lipids contain fatty acids and glycerol, while proteins contain amino acids, which have nitrogen.
