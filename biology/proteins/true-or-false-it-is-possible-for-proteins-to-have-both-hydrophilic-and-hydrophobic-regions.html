True or False. It is possible for proteins to have both hydrophilic and hydrophobic regions.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In many multipass transmembrane proteins, some of the transmembrane helices contain both hydrophobic and hydrophilic amino acid side chains. The hydrophobic side chains lie on one side of the helix, exposed to the lipid of the membrane.

Concept note-2: -The molecules or proteins are considered hydrophilic. These molecules are also referred to as water-loving molecules.

Concept note-3: -A phospholipid is an amphipathic molecule which means it has both a hydrophobic and a hydrophilic component.

Concept note-4: -Proteins are also part of the cell membrane. Like phospholipids, some proteins also have hydrophilic and hydrophobic regions.

Concept note-5: -A phospholipid consists of a hydrophilic (water-loving) head and hydrophobic (water-fearing) tail. In water, phospholipids spontaneously form a double layer called a lipid bilayer, in which the hydrophobic tails of phospholipid molecules are sandwiched between two layers of hydrophilic heads.
