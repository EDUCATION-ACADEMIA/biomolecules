What conditions usually do not cause denaturation?

(A) Extreme heat

(B) Extreme pH

(C) ** Dissolving in water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -addition of water will not cause denaturation of protein.

Concept note-2: -Proteins are denatured by treatment with alkaline or acid, oxidizing or reducing agents, and certain organic solvents.

Concept note-3: -Denaturation defines the unfolding or breaking up of a protein, modifying its standard three-dimensional structure. Proteins may be denatured by chemical action, heat or agitation causing a protein to unfold or its polypeptide chains to become disordered typically leaving the molecules non-functional.

Concept note-4: -Protein denaturation disrupts the secondary, tertiary, and quaternary levels of structure. Only primary structure is unaffected by denaturation.
