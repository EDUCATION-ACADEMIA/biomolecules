what is mRNA

(A) ** complementary copy of DNA

(B) an exact copy of DNA

(C) What genes are made of

(D) what brings amino acids to the ribosome

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -cDNA (short for copy DNA; also called complementary DNA) is synthetic DNA that has been transcribed from a specific mRNA through a reaction using the enzyme reverse transcriptase. While DNA is composed of both coding and non-coding sequences, cDNA contains only coding sequences.

Concept note-2: -mRNA → DNA mRNA is a complementary copy of a DNA segment (gene) and consequently can be used to deduce the gene sequence. For converting a sequence from mRNA to the original DNA code, apply the rules of complementary base pairing: Cytosine (C) is replaced with Guanine (G) – and vice versa.

Concept note-3: -The coding strand for a gene may be located in either strand of a given chromosome, 5’ to 3’ strand or 3’ to 5’ strand. An mRNA strand cannot be complementary to mRNA strand itself. Thus, the correct answer is C.
