Meat that comes from calves younger than one year.

(A) mutton

(B) ** veal

(C) beef

(D) stew

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is Veal? Veal is the meat from a calf or young beef animal. A veal calf is raised until about 16 to 18 weeks of age, weighing up to 450 pounds. Male dairy calves are used in the veal industry.

Concept note-2: -Slink veal is the youngest possible veal, harvested from stillborn or unborn calves. It is normally collected from cows that are pregnant when slaughtered. Slink veal is illegal to consume in the U.S. and Canada.

Concept note-3: -Veal is the meat from calves, mostly pure-bred male dairy calves. In many countries, including the UK, veal production is closely linked to the dairy industry; male dairy calves cannot produce milk and are often considered unsuitable for beef production.

Concept note-5: -Veal is meat from young calves, which are usually slaughtered at about six months old. The word ‘veal’ comes from the French, ‘veau’. It was only the Norman-French aristocracy of medieval England who were privileged enough to eat the meat, and hence it was them who named it.
