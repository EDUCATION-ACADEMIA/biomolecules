The amine group of an amino acid (NH2) typically bonds to an H+, making the group a(n)

(A) anion

(B) ** cation

(C) R group

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The amine group is located by the position number. Groups that are attached to the nitrogen atom are located using “N” as the position number. More complex primary amines are named with-NH2 as the amino substituent.

Concept note-2: -The substituent −NH 2 is called an amino group.

Concept note-3: -All of the amines can form hydrogen bonds with water-even the tertiary ones. Although the tertiary amines don’t have a hydrogen atom attached to the nitrogen and so can’t form hydrogen bonds with themselves, they can form hydrogen bonds with water molecules just using the lone pair on the nitrogen.

Concept note-4: -Amino acids are bifunctional organic compounds, hence it contains both carboxylic group (−COOH) as well as amino group (−NH2)
