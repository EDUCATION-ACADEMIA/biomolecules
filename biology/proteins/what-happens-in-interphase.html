What happens in Interphase?

(A) Spindle fibers form in the cell.

(B) The nuclear membrane dissolves.

(C) ** The chromosomes are duplicated.

(D) Nuclear membranes form around the chromosomes.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Then, at a critical point during interphase (called the S phase), the cell duplicates its chromosomes and ensures its systems are ready for cell division. If all conditions are ideal, the cell is now ready to move into the first phase of mitosis.

Concept note-2: -Duplication of chromosomes occurs during the interphase. During interphase, the cell grows and the nuclear DNA is duplicated. The cell duplicates its chromosomes and ensures its systems are ready for cell division.

Concept note-3: -In the eukaryotic cell cycle, chromosome duplication occurs during “S phase” (the phase of DNA synthesis) and chromosome segregation occurs during “M phase” (the mitosis phase).

Concept note-4: -S phase (DNA Synthesis)-Each of the 46 chromosomes are duplicated by the cell.
