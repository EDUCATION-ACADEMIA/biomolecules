which of the following terms refers to the order in which amino acids are linked together in a protein.

(A) ** Primary

(B) Secondary

(C) Tertiary

(D) Quaternary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Primary structure. The simplest level of protein structure, primary structure, is simply the sequence of amino acids in a polypeptide chain.

Concept note-2: -A peptide bond forms when the amino group of one amino acid bonds to the carboxyl group of another amino acid. The actual order of the amino acids in the protein is called its primary structure (Figure 19.1.

Concept note-3: -The linking of two amino acids entails the release of a water molecule. Hence, the process that links two amino acids together is called condensation. The condensation reaction is also called a dehydration reaction.
