The graph below shows the effect of substrate concentration on the action of enzyme X. This enzyme is functioning at its optimal temperature, 36◦C, and at its optimal pH, 5.5. When the substrate concentration increases from 0.4% to 0.5%, the rate of the reaction

(A) decreases

(B) increases

(C) ** remains the same

(D) increases, then decreases

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Enzyme kinetics graph showing rate of reaction as a function of substrate concentration.

Concept note-2: -So, the correct answer is ‘Velocity of reaction’

Concept note-3: -Enzymes will work best if there is plenty of substrate. As the concentration of the substrate increases, so does the rate of enzyme activity. However, the rate of enzyme activity does not increase forever.

Concept note-4: -If we plot a graph with substrate concentration on X-axis and corresponds velocity in Y-axis. It is observed that as there is an increase in the concentration of the substrate, there is a corresponding increase in the reaction. It forms an arc-shaped curve.
