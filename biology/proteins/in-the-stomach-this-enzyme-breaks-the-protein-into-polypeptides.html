In the stomach, this enzyme breaks the protein into polypeptides.

(A) Carboxypeptidase

(B) Amylase

(C) ** Pepsin

(D) Rennin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pepsin Pearls Pepsin is a stomach enzyme that serves to digest proteins found in ingested food. Gastric chief cells secrete pepsin as an inactive zymogen called pepsinogen. Parietal cells within the stomach lining secrete hydrochloric acid that lowers the pH of the stomach. A low pH (1.5 to 2) activates pepsin.

Concept note-2: -The function of pepsin in gastric digestion is to break down proteins into smaller fragments of polypeptides.

Concept note-3: -An enzyme made in the stomach that breaks down proteins in food during digestion.

Concept note-4: -Pepsin is a proteolytic enzyme, secreted as its precursor pepsinogen by chief cell of gastric pits of vertebrate stomach. It gets activated due to the acid (HCl) present in the stomach. It converts proteins into polypeptides.
