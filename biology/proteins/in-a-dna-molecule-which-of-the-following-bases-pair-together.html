In a DNA molecule, which of the following bases pair together?

(A) adenine and cytosine

(B) ** thymine and adenine

(C) thymine and guanine

(D) cytosine and thymine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Under normal circumstances, the nitrogen-containing bases adenine (A) and thymine (T) pair together, and cytosine (C) and guanine (G) pair together. The binding of these base pairs forms the structure of DNA.

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: In mRNA, three non-overlapping nucleotide base pairs are often sequenced to code a specific amino acid and are called triplet codons.

Concept note-3: -Adenine (A) is one of the four nucleotide bases in DNA, with the other three being cytosine (C), guanine (G) and thymine (T). Within a double-stranded DNA molecule, adenine bases on one strand pair with thymine bases on the opposite strand. The sequence of the four nucleotide bases encodes DNAâ€™s information.

Concept note-4: -Base pairing between adenine and thymine can be found in DNA only. There are two hydrogen bonds holding the two nitrogenous bases together. One of the hydrogen bonds is formed between one of the Hydrogen atoms of the amino group at C-6 of adenine and the Oxygen atom of the keto group at C-4 of thymine.
