In step 2 of transcription, the RNA polymerases ____ and separate the 2 strands of the double helix, exposing the DNA ____ on each strand.

(A) unwind, promotes

(B) wind, nucleotides

(C) wind, promotes

(D) ** unwind, nucleotides

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Step 2: Elongation Elongation is the addition of nucleotides to the mRNA strand. RNA polymerase reads the unwound DNA strand and builds the mRNA molecule, using complementary base pairs. During this process, an adenine (A) in the DNA binds to an uracil (U) in the RNA.

Concept note-2: -Helicases are enzymes that bind and may even remodel nucleic acid or nucleic acid protein complexes. There are DNA and RNA helicases. DNA helicases are essential during DNA replication because they separate double-stranded DNA into single strands allowing each strand to be copied.

Concept note-3: -As the RNA polymerase approaches the end of the gene being transcribed, it hits a region rich in C and G nucleotides. The RNA transcribed from this region folds back on itself, and the complementary C and G nucleotides bind together. The result is a stable hairpin that causes the polymerase to stall.

Concept note-4: -Then, a protein known as helicase attaches to and breaks apart the hydrogen bonds between the bases on the DNA strands, thereby pulling apart the two strands.
