Which statement correctly identifies the difference between laws and theories?

(A) Laws are a statement of fact, while theories are a statement of opinion.

(B) Laws explain why phenomena exist, while theories explain how.

(C) Laws are a prediction of phenomena, while theories are an explanation.

(D) ** Laws describe phenomena, while theories explain why phenomena exist.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which statement below correctly identifies the difference between laws and theories? Laws describe phenomena, while theories explain why phenomena exist.

Concept note-2: -Laws describe phenomena, while theories explain why phenomena exist. Laws are a statement of fact, while theories are a statement of opinion. Laws explain why phenomena exist, while theories explain how. Laws are a prediction of phenomena, while theories are an explanation.

Concept note-3: -Generally, laws describe what will happen in a given situation as demonstrable by a mathematical equation, whereas theories describe how the phenomenon happens.

Concept note-4: -Theories are explanations of a phenomenon. Theory explains how nature works. Law explains what is nature doing under certain conditions and it can predict the future if the conditions are satisfied. Theory is often descriptive and complex than laws with detailed explanation or contains several supported hypothesis.
