How many fatty acid residues are normally present in a phospholipid molecule?

(A) A 1

(B) ** B 2

(C) C 3

(D) D 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Phospholipids contain glycerol, two fatty acids, a phosphate and a basic component.

Concept note-2: -Like fats, they are typically composed of fatty acid chains attached to a backbone of glycerol. Instead having three fatty acid tails, however, phospholipids generally have just two, and the third carbon of the glycerol backbone is occupied by a modified phosphate group.

Concept note-3: -Fat is stored reserve for the body which provides energy. These are triesters of glycerol. It is obtained from fatty acids and glycerol. It consists of one glycerol molecule and three fatty acid molecules.

Concept note-4: -The phospholipid may have two esterified fatty acids and are called diacyl-phospholipids, whereas phospholipids with one fatty acid are called monoacylphospholipids or lyso-phospholipids.

Concept note-5: -2 Triglycerides and triglyceride-rich lipoproteins Triglycerides are tri-esters consisting of a glycerol bound to three fatty acid molecules.
