If a polypeptide contains just 4 amino acids, calculate how many different polypeptides can be created.

(A) 420 = 1, 099, 511, 600, 000

(B) ** 204 = 160, 000

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Since each of the 20 amino acids is chemically distinct and each can, in principle, occur at any position in a protein chain, there are 20 × 20 × 20 × 20 = 160, 000 different possible polypeptide chains four amino acids long, or 20n different possible polypeptide chains n amino acids long.

Concept note-2: -A tripeptide composed of three different amino acids can be made in 6 different constitutions, and the tetrapeptide shown above (composed of four different amino acids) would have 24 constitutional isomers.

Concept note-3: -For a protein with just 4 amino acids the number of possible combinations rises to 2 0 4 or 160 000 possible combinations. This is why a diverse spectrum of proteins can be synthesized with the same 20 amino acids.

Concept note-4: -Each group of three nucleotides encodes one amino acid. Since there are 64 combinations of 4 nucleotides taken three at a time and only 20 amino acids, the code is degenerate (more than one codon per amino acid, in most cases). The adaptor molecule for translation is tRNA.
