Jason is trying to build muscle by consuming excessive amounts of protein. How is this likely to impact the body?

(A) The extra protein will be converted back to amino acids.

(B) ** The extra protein will convert to fat and be stored in the fatty tissues of the body.

(C) Excess protein will be stored in the blood.

(D) Excess protein will be stored in the muscles for later use.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Excess protein consumed is usually stored as fat, while the surplus of amino acids is excreted. This can lead to weight gain over time, especially if you consume too many calories while trying to increase your protein intake.

Concept note-2: -Eating too much saturated fats in your diet can raise “bad” LDL cholesterol in your blood, which can increase the risk of heart disease and stroke. “Good” HDL cholesterol has a positive effect by taking cholesterol from parts of the body where there’s too much of it to the liver, where it’s disposed of.

Concept note-3: -Once your glycogen stores are full, your body stores excess calories from carbohydrate as fat. Excess calories from fat and protein intake get stored as fat in the body as well.

Concept note-4: -You need protein in your diet to help your body repair cells and make new ones. Protein is also important for growth and development in children, teens, and pregnant women.

Concept note-5: -After a meal, carbohydrates are broken down into glucose, an immediate source of energy. Excess glucose gets stored in the liver as glycogen or, with the help of insulin, converted into fatty acids, circulated to other parts of the body and stored as fat in adipose tissue.
