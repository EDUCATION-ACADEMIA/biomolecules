(A) ** Enzymes

(B) Starch

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They help the body fight infection and other diseases. Types of white blood cells are granulocytes (neutrophils, eosinophils, and basophils), monocytes, and lymphocytes (T cells and B cells).

Concept note-2: -Granulocytes are white blood cells that have small granules containing proteins. There are three types of granulocyte cells: Basophils: These represent less than 1% of white blood cells in the body and are typically present in increased numbers after an allergic reaction.

Concept note-3: -Connectin is known to be an anchoring protein of actomyosin filaments in skeletal muscle. Attempts were made to extract this protein from white blood cells and platelets in order to investigate its properties.

Concept note-4: -Plasma proteins, such as albumin and globulin, that help maintain the colloidal osmotic pressure at about 25 mmHg. Electrolytes like sodium, potassium, bicarbonate, chloride, and calcium help maintain blood pH. Immunoglobulins help fight infection and various other small amounts of enzymes, hormones, and vitamins.
