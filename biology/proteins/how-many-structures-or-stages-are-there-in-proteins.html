How many structures or stages are there in proteins?

(A) 1

(B) 2

(C) 3

(D) ** 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The four levels of protein structure are primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary. It is helpful to understand the nature and function of each level of protein structure in order to fully understand how a protein works.

Concept note-2: -The complete structure of a protein can be described at four different levels of complexity: primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary structure.

Concept note-3: -To understand how a protein gets its final shape or conformation, we need to understand the four levels of protein structure: primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary.

Concept note-4: -Quaternary (4˚) Structure Quaternary structure in proteins is the most intricate degree of organization still considered a single molecule. To be considered to have quaternary structure, a protein must have two or more peptide chains forming subunits.

Concept note-5: -There are seven types of proteins: antibodies, contractile proteins, enzymes, hormonal proteins, structural proteins, storage proteins, and transport proteins.
