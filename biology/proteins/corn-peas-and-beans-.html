[SOURCES]
Corn, peas, and beans ____

(A) are complete proteins

(B) contain no protein

(C) lose protein when cooked

(D) ** are incomplete proteins

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Beans are incomplete proteins because they are low in one key amino acid (amino acids are used by the body to build protein) called methionine. Cereal grains-including corn, rice and wheat-are high in methionine yet lack another amino acid found in beans.

Concept note-2: -Corn, as well as most other plant-based types of protein, does have protein, but it is incompleteâ€“lacking in a few of the essential amino acids.

Concept note-3: -Neither beans nor corn alone, of course, is such a complete food because neither is a complete protein. Beans, however, contain all the essential amino acids but one, methionine, which just happens to be the amino acid that corn does have.

Concept note-4: -Then there are foods known as incomplete proteins, including beans, whole grains, nuts, seeds, peas, and corn.

Concept note-5: -Examples of incomplete proteins They include: Legumes (beans, peas, lentils). Nuts. Seeds.
