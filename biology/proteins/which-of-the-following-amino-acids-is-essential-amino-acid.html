Which of the following amino acids is essential amino acid

(A) Asparagine

(B) ** Threonine

(C) Serine

(D) Cysteine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 9 essential amino acids are: histidine, isoleucine, leucine, lysine, methionine, phenylalanine, threonine, tryptophan, and valine.

Concept note-2: -Only four essential amino acids are likely to affect the protein quality of mixed human diets: lysine, the sulfur-containing amino acids (methionine plus cystine), threonine, and tryptophan.

Concept note-3: -Threonine (Thr), otherwise known as -amino--hydroxybutyric acid, is an indispensable amino acid in animals that must be obtained from the diet [1].

Concept note-4: -Isoleucine, leucine, threonine, lysine, methionine, phenylalanine, tryptophan, and valine are essential amino acids. The non-essential amino acids are alanine, asparagine, aspartic acid, cysteine, glutamine, glutamic acid, glycine, proline, serine and tyrosine. Q.
