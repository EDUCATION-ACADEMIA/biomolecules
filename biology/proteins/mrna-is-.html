PROTEINS

[SOURCES]
mRNA is ____

(A) ** a form of RNA that carries the instructions for making a protein from a gene and delivers it to the site of translation.

(B) single strands of RNA that temporarily carry a specific amino acid on one end.

(C) molecules that are part of the structure of ribosomes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Once an mRNA has been produced, by transcription and processing the information present in its nucleotide sequence is used to synthesize a protein.

Concept note-2: -The role of mRNA is to carry protein information from the DNA in a cell’s nucleus to the cell’s cytoplasm (watery interior), where the protein-making machinery reads the mRNA sequence and translates each three-base codon into its corresponding amino acid in a growing protein chain.

Concept note-3: -During translation, ribosomal subunits assemble together like a sandwich on the strand of mRNA, where they proceed to attract tRNA molecules tethered to amino acids (circles). A long chain of amino acids emerges as the ribosome decodes the mRNA sequence into a polypeptide, or a new protein.

Concept note-4: -During transcription, the enzyme RNA polymerase (green) uses DNA as a template to produce a pre-mRNA transcript (pink). The pre-mRNA is processed to form a mature mRNA molecule that can be translated to build the protein molecule (polypeptide) encoded by the original gene.
