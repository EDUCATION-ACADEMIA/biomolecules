The ____ region of a protein would orient away from the watery surface of a cell.

(A) ** Hydrophobic

(B) Hydrophilic

(C) Charged

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The portions of an integral membrane protein found inside the membrane are hydrophobic, while those that are exposed to the cytoplasm or extracellular fluid tend to be hydrophilic.

Concept note-2: -The hydrophobic part of the cell membrane is the tail. The cell membrane is made of a phospholipid bilayer. Each phospholipid has two parts, a hydrophilic head and a hydrophobic tail.

Concept note-3: -The hydrophobic part of the solvent-accessible surface of a typical monomeric globular protein consists of a single, large interconnected region formed from faces of apolar atoms and constituting approximately 60% of the solvent-accessible surface area.

Concept note-4: -Thus, both surfaces of the plasma membrane are hydrophilic (“water-loving”). In contrast, the interior of the membrane, between its two surfaces, is a hydrophobic (“water-hating”) or nonpolar region because of the fatty acid tails.
