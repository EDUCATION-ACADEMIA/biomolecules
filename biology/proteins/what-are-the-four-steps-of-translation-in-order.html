What are the four steps of translation in order?

(A) Elongation, initiation, activation, termination

(B) Activation, elongation, initiation, termination

(C) ** Activation, initiation, elongation, termination

(D) Initiation, activation, elongation, termination

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Initiation, elongation, and termination.

Concept note-2: -As the name suggests, initiation is the beginning of the translation process, where elongation and termination represent the middle and the last process, respectively. These processes take place in transcription as well as in synthesizing of mRNA.

Concept note-3: -Transcription has three stages: initiation, elongation, and termination.

Concept note-4: -The molecules involved in translation are mRNA, ribosomes, and tRNA. During translation, messenger RNA (mRNA) attaches to the ribosome. The ribosome reads the mRNA in groups of three nucleotides called codons. Each codon codes for one amino acid.
