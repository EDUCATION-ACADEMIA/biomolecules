What will be the resulting mRNA from this DNA sequence:GAT-TAC-AGA-TTA-CAT?

(A) CTA-ATG-TCT-AAT-GTA

(B) GAU-UAG-UGA-UUA-GUT

(C) ** CUA-AUG-UCU-AAU-GUA

(D) CAT-ACA-TAC-ATA-TAG

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -DNA utilizes four bases, adenine (A), guanine (G), cytosine (C), and thymine (T), in its code. RNA also uses four bases. However, instead of using ‘T’ as DNA does, it uses uracil (U). Therefore, if your DNA sequence is 3’ T C G T T C A G T 5’, the mRNA sequence would be 5’ A G C A A G U C A 3’.

Concept note-2: -mRNA is synthesized in the nucleus using the nucleotide sequence of DNA as a template. This process requires nucleotide triphosphates as substrates and is catalyzed by the enzyme RNA polymerase II. The process of making mRNA from DNA is called transcription, and it occurs in the nucleus.

Concept note-3: -Transcription occurs in the nucleus of eukaryotic cells. During transcription, a copy of mRNA is made that is complementary to a strand of DNA.
