If you do repeats then what three things does this allow you to do?

(A) ** calculate an average

(B) ** identify outliers

(C) ** see if there is repeatability

(D) assess accuracy

(E) assess validity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are many factors that come into play that affect the ability to repeat measurements including poor instrument quality, instruments out of calibration, inadequate methods of data collection and operator inefficiency or inexperience.

Concept note-2: -Precision and repeatability can be seen easily from a table of results containing repeat measurements. If the repeat measurements are close together, the data is precise and repeatable.

Concept note-3: -Repeatability is the variation that occurs when a person uses a gage to measure the same part repeatedly with the same measurement tool. When a gage is acceptable, the variation due to repeatability is small. For example, when we measure a washer over and over again, the values should group closely together.
