type of nucleic acid that is shaped like a double helix and is composed of long strands of alternating Deoxyribose sugars and phosphate groups, along with nitrogenous bases.

(A) RNA

(B) ** DNA

(C) Protein

(D) Amino acid

(E) Gene

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -DNA is made of two linked strands that wind around each other to resemble a twisted ladder-a shape known as a double helix. Each strand has a backbone made of alternating sugar (deoxyribose) and phosphate groups.

Concept note-2: -DNA is a double helix formed by base pairs attached to a sugar-phosphate backbone.

Concept note-3: -Each molecule of DNA is a double helix formed from two complementary strands of nucleotides held together by hydrogen bonds between G-C and A-T base pairs.

Concept note-4: -The base pairs are stabilized by hydrogen bonds; adenine and thymine form two hydrogen bonds between them, whereas cytosine and guanine form three hydrogen bonds between them. Figure 22.2. 7: Hydrogen bonds form between complementary nitrogenous bases on the interior of DNA.

Concept note-5: -DNA is made up of a double-stranded helix held together by weak hydrogen bonds between purine-pyrimidine nucleotide base pairs: adenine (A) paired with thymine (T), and guanine (G) paired with cytosine (C). Also called deoxyribonucleic acid.
