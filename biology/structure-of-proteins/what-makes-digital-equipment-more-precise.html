What makes digital equipment more precise?

(A) ** Can measure to 2 decimal places

(B) Can be turned off and on

(C) Can be set to zero

(D) Can be calibrated

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Roughly speaking, more digits to the right of the decimal point means more precision. The more digits you track on the right of the decimal point, the less you can have on the left of the decimal point.

Concept note-2: -Precision of measured values refers to how close the agreement is between repeated measurements. The precision of a measuring tool is related to the size of its measurement increments. The smaller the measurement increment, the more precise the tool.

Concept note-3: -In mathematics, precision describes the level of exactness in a number’s digits, such as number 54.6 having precision 1 (one decimal digit). A number with end zeroes ("00") has a negative precision, such as 500 having precision-2, or 4, 000 as precision-3. A whole number (not ending in “0") has precision 0.

Concept note-4: -Precision is a mathematical term, and we express it using latitude/longitude. The more decimal places you have the more precise it is.
