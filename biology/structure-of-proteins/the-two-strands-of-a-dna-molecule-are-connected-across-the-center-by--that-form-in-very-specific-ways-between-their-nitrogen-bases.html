The two strands of a DNA molecule are connected across the center by ____ that form in very specific ways between their nitrogen bases.

(A) strong bonds

(B) ** weak bonds

(C) deoxyriose

(D) phosphate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hydrogen bond (H-bond) binds the nitrogen bases between the two strands of DNA.

Concept note-2: -Base Pair The two strands are held together by hydrogen bonds between pairs of bases: adenine pairs with thymine, and cytosine pairs with guanine.

Concept note-3: -First, a so-called initiator protein unwinds a short stretch of the DNA double helix. Then, a protein known as helicase attaches to and breaks apart the hydrogen bonds between the bases on the DNA strands, thereby pulling apart the two strands.

Concept note-4: -hydrogen. Covalent bonds occur within each linear strand and strongly bond the bases, sugars, and phosphate groups (both within each component and between components). Hydrogen bonds occur between the two strands and involve a base from one strand with a base from the second in complementary pairing.
