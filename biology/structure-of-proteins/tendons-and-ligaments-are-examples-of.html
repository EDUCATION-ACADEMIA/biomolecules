STRUCTURE OF PROTEINS

[SOURCES]
Tendons and ligaments are examples of

(A) recognition proteins.

(B) enzymatic proteins.

(C) transport proteins.

(D) ** structural proteins.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tendons and ligaments are mainly composed of type I collagen fibers surrounded by a mesh of loose connective tissue . The whole tendon transmits forces from muscle to bone.

Concept note-2: -Collagen is principal protein of the skin, tendons, ligaments, cartilage, bone, and connective tissue.

Concept note-3: -The bodies of humans and animals owe their strength especially to a fibrous structural protein called collagen. Collagen is abundant in bones, tendons, ligaments and skin.

Concept note-4: -The connective tissue connects various parts of the body. Tendon, ligament, bone and cartilage are examples of different connective tissues.

Concept note-5: -Tendons consist of collagen (mostly type I collagen) and elastin embedded in a proteoglycan-water matrix with collagen accounting for 65-80% and elastin approximately 1-2% of the dry mass of the tendon.
