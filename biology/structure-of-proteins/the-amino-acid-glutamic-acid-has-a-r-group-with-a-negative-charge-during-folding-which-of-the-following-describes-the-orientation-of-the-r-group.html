The amino acid glutamic acid has a R group with a negative charge. During folding, which of the following describes the orientation of the R group?

(A) ** Exterior of protein due to negative charge providing hydrophilic properties

(B) Exterior of protein due to negative charge providing hydrophobic properties

(C) Interior of protein due to negative charge providing hydrophobic properties

(D) Interior of protein due to negative charge providing hydrophilic properties

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When protein folding takes place (in an aqueous compartment), the hydrophobic R groups of nonpolar amino acids will cluster together in the interior of the protein, whereas the hydrophilic R groups lay on the outside. These types of interactions are known as hydrophobic interactions.

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: At physiological pH (around 7.35 to 7.45), there are two amino acids that are negatively-charged: aspartic acid and glutamic acid.

Concept note-3: -What role could an uncharged amino functional group in an amino acid side chain (R group) play in the three-dimensional structure of a protein? B. It could form an ionic bond with an amino group in the backbone.
