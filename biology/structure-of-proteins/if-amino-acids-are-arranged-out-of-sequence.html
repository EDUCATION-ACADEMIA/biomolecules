If amino acids are arranged out of sequence:

(A) ** The protein may not work

(B) There is immediate death

(C) You can process carbohydrates

(D) You cannot make ATP

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The significance of the unique sequence, or order, of amino acids, known as the proteinâ€™s primary structure, is that it dictates the 3-D conformation the folded protein will have. This conformation, in turn, will determine the function of the protein.

Concept note-2: -If one amino acid in a protein sequence is changed the protein could change its function or become non-functional entirely. Amino acids make up the structure of proteins. If even one amino acid is changed, the three-dimensional structure of the protein can change, thus changing its function.

Concept note-3: -Because amino acid sequence determines protein structure and structure dictates biochemical function, proteins that share a similar amino acid sequence usually perform similar biochemical functions, even when they are found in distantly related organisms.

Concept note-4: -The amino acids contain R groups that interact with each other creating a three-dimensional structure. This higher-order structure decides the biological activity of that protein. if the wrong amino acid joins the polypeptide chain the higher-order structure will change, effecting the functioning of the protein.
