The sequence of ____ in a DNA molecule determine what protein will be produced.

(A) ** Nucleotides

(B) amino acids

(C) Proteins

(D) RNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The four types of nitrogen bases found in nucleotides are: adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G) and cytosine (C). The order, or sequence, of these bases determines what biological instructions are contained in a strand of DNA.

Concept note-2: -Structure of DNA: double-stranded helix held by complementary base pairs. DNA carries the genetic information for making proteins. The four bases A, T, C and G make up the genetic code. The base sequence determines amino acid sequence in protein.

Concept note-3: -Genetic Code: The sequence of nucleotides, coded in triplets (codons) along the mRNA, that determines the sequence of amino acids in protein synthesis.
