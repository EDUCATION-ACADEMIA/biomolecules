Which of these is not a monosaccharide?

(A) glucose

(B) ribose

(C) glyceraldehyde

(D) ** maltose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: Sucrose is not a monosaccharide.

Concept note-2: -Is maltose a monosaccharide or disaccharide? Maltose is a disaccharide.

Concept note-3: -Glucose is a monosaccharide whereas sucrose and maltose are disacharides.

Concept note-4: -Disaccharides. Disaccharides consist of two monosaccharide units, linked together with glycosidic bonds in the or orientation. The most important of them are sucrose, lactose, and maltose. Sucrose is the most abundant and consists of a molecule of -glucose and -fructose linked together (Figure 2(a)).

Concept note-5: -Dextrose (glucose) is a monosaccharide. It does not represent a disaccharide.
