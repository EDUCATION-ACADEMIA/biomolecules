STRUCTURE OF PROTEINS

[SOURCES]
Proteins that store amino acids ____

(A) defense proteins

(B) structural proteins

(C) enzymatic proteins

(D) ** storage proteins

(E) motor proteins

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Some storage proteins store amino acids. Storage proteins’ amino acids are used in embryonic development of animals or plants. Two amino acid storage proteins in animals are casein and ovalbumin. Seeds, particularly of leguminous plants, contain high concentrations of storage proteins.

Concept note-2: -Hence, the correct answer is ‘Glutelin’.

Concept note-3: -Plant storage proteins can be classified into two classes; seed storage proteins (SSPs) and vegetative storage proteins (VSPs).

Concept note-4: -When in excess, the amino acids are processed and stored as glucose or ketones. The nitrogen waste that is liberated in this process is converted to urea in the urea acid cycle and eliminated in the urine.
