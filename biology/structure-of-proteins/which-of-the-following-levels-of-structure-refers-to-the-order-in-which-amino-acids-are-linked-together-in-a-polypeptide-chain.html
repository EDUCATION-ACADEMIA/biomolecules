STRUCTURE OF PROTEINS

[SOURCES]
Which of the following levels of structure refers to the order in which amino acids are linked together in a polypeptide chain?

(A) ** Primary

(B) Secondary

(C) Tertiary

(D) Quaternary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Primary structure. The simplest level of protein structure, primary structure, is simply the sequence of amino acids in a polypeptide chain.

Concept note-2: -Primary Structure (1˚) The primary structure of a peptide or protein is the linear sequence of its amino acids (AAs). By convention, the primary structure of a protein is read and written from the amino-terminal (N) to the carboxyl-terminal (C) end. Each amino acid is connected to the next by a peptide bond.

Concept note-3: -A protein’s primary structure refers to the amino acid sequence in the polypeptide chain. Peptide bonds that are made during the protein biosynthesis process hold the primary structure together.

Concept note-4: -Primary structure refers to the linear sequence of the amino acids connected by the peptide bonds. The secondary structure consists of local packing of polypeptide chain into -helices and -sheets due to hydrogen bonds between peptide bond – central carbon backbone.

Concept note-5: -The actual order of the amino acids in the protein is called its primary structure and is determined by DNA. The order of deoxyribonucleotide bases in a gene determines the amino acid sequence of a particular protein.
