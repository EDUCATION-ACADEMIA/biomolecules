Which base is used in synthesis of RNA, but not DNA?

(A) Cytosine

(B) Adenine

(C) Thymine

(D) ** Uracil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Uracil is the nitrogenous base present only in RNA, but not in DNA. Thymine is in DNA. DNA have thymine, guanine, adenine and cytosine.

Concept note-2: -Uracil is present in RNA but not in DNA.

Concept note-3: -One of the most important differences between DNA and RNA apart from the sugar molecule is the nitrogenous base Uracil, which is seen only in RNA. Instead of Uracil, DNA has Thymine, while the other three nitrogenous bases (Adenine, Guanine, Cytosine) are the same in both RNA and DNA.

Concept note-4: -Uracil (U) is one of the four nucleotide bases in RNA, with the other three being adenine (A), cytosine (C) and guanine (G). In RNA, uracil pairs with adenine. In a DNA molecule, the nucleotide thymine (T) is used in place of uracil.

Concept note-5: -In RNA, the thymine is replaced by uracil (U).
