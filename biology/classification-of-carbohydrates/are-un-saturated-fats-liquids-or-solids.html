Are Un-Saturated Fats Liquids or Solids

(A) Solids

(B) ** Liquids

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The building blocks of unsaturated fats have bends or kinks that donâ€™t allow the blocks to be tightly stacked and thus, appear more fluid and are liquid at room temperature.

Concept note-2: -Unsaturated fats, which are liquid at room temperature, are considered beneficial fats because they can improve blood cholesterol levels, ease inflammation, stabilize heart rhythms, and play a number of other beneficial roles.

Concept note-3: -Saturated fat is solid at room temperature. It is found mostly in animal-based foods (such as meat, butter, milk, and cheese) and in coconut oil, palm oil, and cocoa butter. Saturated fat can raise your cholesterol.

Concept note-4: -Saturated fats (including a type called trans fat) tend to stay solid and can cause fatty deposits in blood vessels, leading to atherosclerosis ("hardening of the arteries"). By contrast, unsaturated fats stay liquid at room temperature and are less likely to clog your arteries.
