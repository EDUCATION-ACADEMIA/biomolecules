Whats the number 1 function of lipids?

(A) give you energy (fruits and vegetables)

(B) ** Long term energy (stores energy)

(C) Helps with cell structures

(D) Be water proof

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -While glycogen provides a ready source of energy, lipids primarily function as an energy reserve.

Concept note-2: -Lipids perform many different functions in a cell. Cells store energy for long-term use in the form of lipids called fats.

Concept note-3: -Lipids are fatty compounds that perform a variety of functions in your body. Theyâ€™re part of your cell membranes and help control what goes in and out of your cells. They help with moving and storing energy, absorbing vitamins and making hormones.

Concept note-4: -The main biological functions of lipids include storing energy, as lipids may be broken down to yield large amounts of energy. Lipids also form the structural components of cell membranes, and form various messengers and signaling molecules within the body.

Concept note-5: -Because of these differences, lipids are used in long-term energy storage (adipose, or fat, tissue), and carbohydrates are used in short-term energy storage (glycogen).
