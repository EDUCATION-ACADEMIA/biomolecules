Which substance is an inorganic molecule?

(A) starch

(B) DNA

(C) ** water

(D) fat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Water is definitely an inorganic compound (dihydrogen oxide) and methyl alcohol is definitely an organic compound. As with all empirical classification schemes, the distinction is not particularly clear when the lower molecular weight substances are considered.

Concept note-2: -Inorganic substances are a group of chemicals that contain no carbon. Examples include ammonia, hydrogen sulfide, all metals, and most elements (such as calcium).

Concept note-3: -H2O means each molecule of water contains two atoms of hydrogen (H) joined to one atom of oxygen (O). In order for something to be organic-as in alive-it needs carbon. So water, by definition is inorganic.

Concept note-4: -Inorganic compounds essential to human functioning include water, salts, acids, and bases. These compounds are inorganic; that is, they do not contain both hydrogen and carbon.

Concept note-5: -Because water is two hydrogen molecules and one oxygen molecule, by definition it is then an inorganic compound, and cannot be organic.
