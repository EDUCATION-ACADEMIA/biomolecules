CLASSIFICATION OF CARBOHYDRATES

[SOURCES]
As you add 10 drops Lactose + 2 mL Seliwanoff’s reagent, what was the final color of the solution?

(A) orange

(B) blue

(C) ** no changes in color

(D) red

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Aldose sugars react with the Seliwanoff’s reagent to give faint pink color which intensifies with time.

Concept note-2: -It is named after Theodor Seliwanoff, the chemist who devised the test. When added to a solution containing ketoses, a red color is formed rapidly indicating a positive test.

Concept note-3: -Lactose and also maltose hydrolyzes upon prolonged heating and gives positive result with Seliwanoff’s reagent.

Concept note-4: -The formation of the cherry red-colored complex indicates a positive result which means that the given sample contains ketoses. The absence of such color or the appearance of the color after a prolonged period of time indicates a negative result which means that the test sample doesn’t have ketoses.
