What does Poly mean?

(A) ** Many

(B) One

(C) Large

(D) Small

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Prefix ‘poly’ means ‘many’ for example, ‘polygon’ means a figure with many sides.

Concept note-2: -The origin of the prefix poly-is from an ancient Greek word which meant “many.” This prefix appears in, well, “many” English vocabulary words, such as polysyllabic, polyhedron, and Polynesia.

Concept note-3: -poly-in American English combining form. a combining form with the meanings “much, many” and, in chemistry, “ polymeric, ” used in the formation of compound words. polyandrous. polyculture.

Concept note-4: -By definition, mono-specifies the quantity (one); but poly-does not specify the quantity-it simply says “more than one” or “much/many".

Concept note-5: -Linguists as well like to use the prefix poly-, which means “many.” For instance, a polysyllabic word has “many” syllables, such as the 19-syllable word pneumonoultramicroscopicsilicovolcanoconiosis, widely regarded as the longest word in the English language.
