[SOURCES]
What are the three elements in CHO

(A) Carbon, Hydrogen, Nitrogen

(B) Carbon, Oxygen, Hydrogen

(C) Oxygen, Carbon, Hydrogen

(D) ** Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Carbohydrates contain only carbon, hydrogen and oxygen atoms; prior to any oxidation or reduction, most have the empirical formula Cm(H2O)n.

Concept note-2: -Carbohydrates and lipids are made of only carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen (CHO).

Concept note-3: -CHON is a mnemonic acronym for the four most common elements in living organisms: carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen.

Concept note-4: -CHO: An abbreviation for the aldehyde functional group. Molecular structure of (D)-(+)-glyceraldehyde, illustrating the use of CHO as an abbreviation for an aldehyde group.
