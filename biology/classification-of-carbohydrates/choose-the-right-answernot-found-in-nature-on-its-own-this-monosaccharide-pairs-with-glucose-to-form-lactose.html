Choose the right answer:Not found in nature on its own, this monosaccharide pairs with glucose to form lactose.

(A) a-Lactose

(B) b-Maltose

(C) ** c-Galactose

(D) d-Sucrose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Glucose and fructose are the most available monosaccharide in nature.

Concept note-2: -The three most common monosaccharides are glucose, fructose, and galactose. In addition to these three sugars, the monosaccharide category also includes sugar alcohols and pentoses.

Concept note-3: -Glucose is the most common monosaccharide found in nature. In plants, it is generated through photosynthesis. Some plants store glucose in linked chains. These chains are called starch.

Concept note-4: -Correct answer: Explanation: Out of all the sugars listed, lactose is the only one that is not a monosaccharide. Lactose is composed of a glucose and galactose bound together in a glycosidic linkage.
