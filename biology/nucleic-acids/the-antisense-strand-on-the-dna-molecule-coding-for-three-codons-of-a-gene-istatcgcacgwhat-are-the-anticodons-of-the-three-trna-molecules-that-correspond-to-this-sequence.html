The antisense strand on the DNA molecule coding for three codons of a gene isTATCGCACGWhat are the anticodons of the three tRNA molecules that correspond to this sequence?

(A) ** UAU, CGC and ACG

(B) ATA, GCG and TGC

(C) AUA, GCG and UGC

(D) TAT, CGC and ACG

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So the right answer is option ‘UAG’.

Concept note-2: -A sequence of three consecutive bases in a tRNA molecule which specifically binds to a complementary codon sequence in mRNA is known an anticodon.

Concept note-3: -Anticodon. An anticodon is a unit of three nucleotides corresponding to the three bases of an mRNA codon. Each tRNA has a distinct anticodon triplet sequence that can form 3 complementary base pairs to one or more codons for an amino acid.

Concept note-4: -A codon is a DNA or RNA sequence of three nucleotides (a trinucleotide) that forms a unit of genomic information encoding a particular amino acid or signaling the termination of protein synthesis (stop signals).
