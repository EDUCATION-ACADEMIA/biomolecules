How many different kinds of polypeptides, each composed of 12 amino acids, could be synthesized using the 20 common amino acids?

(A) 412

(B) 1220

(C) 20

(D) ** 2012

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How many different kinds of polypeptides, each composed of 12 amino acids, could be synthesized using the 20 common amino acids? The book’s answer is 2012.

Concept note-2: -Since each of the 20 amino acids is chemically distinct and each can, in principle, occur at any position in a protein chain, there are 20 × 20 × 20 × 20 = 160, 000 different possible polypeptide chains four amino acids long, or 20n different possible polypeptide chains n amino acids long.

Concept note-3: -Therefore making a protein on 50 amino acids long from 20 different amino acids, we get 2 0 50 distinct proteins.

Concept note-4: -For a polypeptide containing 25 amino acids, the number of polypeptide bonds will be 24.

Concept note-5: -Because amino acids can be arranged in many different combinations, it’s possible for your body to make thousands of different kinds of proteins from just the same 21 amino acids.
