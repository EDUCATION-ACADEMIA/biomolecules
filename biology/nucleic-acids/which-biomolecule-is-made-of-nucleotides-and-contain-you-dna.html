NUCLEIC ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Which biomolecule is made of nucleotides and contain you DNA?

(A) Carbohydrate

(B) Lipids

(C) Protein

(D) ** Nucleic Acid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nucleic acids, macromolecules made out of units called nucleotides, come in two naturally occurring varieties: deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) and ribonucleic acid (RNA). DNA is the genetic material found in living organisms, all the way from single-celled bacteria to multicellular mammals like you and me.

Concept note-2: -Nucleic acids are giant biomolecules made of monomers called nucleotides.

Concept note-3: -DNA and RNA are made up of monomers known as nucleotides. The nucleotides combine with each other to form a polynucleotide, DNA or RNA.

Concept note-4: -Nucleic acids are polynucleotides-that is, long chainlike molecules composed of a series of nearly identical building blocks called nucleotides. Each nucleotide consists of a nitrogen-containing aromatic base attached to a pentose (five-carbon) sugar, which is in turn attached to a phosphate group.
