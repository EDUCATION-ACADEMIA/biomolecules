Which of the following is/are ways that transcription is different to DNA replication?None, some or all may be correct.

(A) In transcription, both strands act as templates.

(B) Transcription involves the base adenine.

(C) Transcription happens in the nucleus.

(D) ** Transcription produces a single-stranded product.

(E) ** Transcription involves RNA polymerase.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Both DNA Replication and Transcription involve the generation of a new copy of the DNA in a cell. DNA transcription is involved in replicating the DNA into RNA, while DNA replication makes another copy of DNA.

Concept note-2: -Which statement correctly describes the difference between DNA transcription and DNA replication? DNA replication results in a single-stranded RNA molecule, while DNA transcription results in two copies of a double helix of DNA.

Concept note-3: -Transcription differs from DNA replication because of the following differences. First, replication is the duplication of two strands of DNA, whereas transcription is the formation of a single identical DNA from the two stranded DNA.

Concept note-4: -DNA synthesis is the process of synthesizing a double stranded DNA through semi-conservative replication by using enzymes. RNA synthesis is the process of synthesizing an RNA through the process of transcription using an enzyme-mediated method.
