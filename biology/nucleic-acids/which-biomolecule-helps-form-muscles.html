Which biomolecule helps form muscles?

(A) Nucleic Acid

(B) Carbohydrate

(C) ** Protein

(D) Lipids

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Proteins are the primary building materials of the body. Your hair, skin, muscles, and organs are composed mostly of proteins. Proteins are strong yet flexible, and they have a complex 3-D structure. Amino acids are the basic building blocks of proteins.

Concept note-2: -In biology, a protein is a biomolecule comprised of amino acid residues joined together by peptide bonds.

Concept note-3: -PROTEINS AND NUCLEIC ACIDS! Of protein that we consume. Proteins make up the structure of living things… Hair, nails, skin, bones, muscle, etc are all built by protein!

Concept note-4: -Biomolecules are an organic molecule that includes carbohydrates, protein, lipids, and nucleic acids. They are important for the survival of living cells.

Concept note-5: -There are four major classes of biological macromolecules (carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids), and each is an important component of the cell and performs a wide array of functions.
