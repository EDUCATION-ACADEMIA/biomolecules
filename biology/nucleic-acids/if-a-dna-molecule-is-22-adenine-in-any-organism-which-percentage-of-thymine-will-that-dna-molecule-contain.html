If a DNA molecule is 22% adenine in any organism, which percentage of thymine will that DNA molecule contain?

(A) ** 22%

(B) 28%

(C) 44%

(D) 56%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adenine always pairs with thymine, so their percentages will be equal. Cytosine always pairs with guanine, so their percentages will also be equal. The sum of all four percentages must equal 100%. We know that the sample is 22% adenine; this tells us it is also 22% thymine.

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: If a double strand is 22% guanine, then automatically we know that there will be 22% cysteine.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: A double-stranded DNA molecule with 20% thymine would contain 30% guanine.

Concept note-4: -In a typical DNA molecule, the proportion of thymine is 30% of the N bases.
