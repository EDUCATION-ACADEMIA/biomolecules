What is mutation?

(A) ** A change in the DNA sequence, Can be a single nucleotide or a large chromosomal change to the DNA, Silent, missense, and nonsense are examples of these

(B) This process is where peptide bonds are formed between amino acids

(C) This process only opens a portion of the DNA called a gene

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A mutation is a change in the DNA sequence of an organism. Mutations can result from errors in DNA replication during cell division, exposure to mutagens or a viral infection.

Concept note-2: -Substitution Substitution, as related to genomics, is a type of mutation in which one nucleotide is replaced by a different nucleotide. The term can also refer to the replacement of one amino acid in a protein with a different amino acid.

Concept note-3: -An insertion changes the DNA sequence by adding one or more nucleotides to the gene. As a result, the protein made from the gene may not function properly. A deletion changes the DNA sequence by removing at least one nucleotide in a gene.

Concept note-4: -A mutation (Section 14.1) is a change in the nucleotide sequence of a short region of a genome (Figure 14.1A). Many mutations are point mutations that replace one nucleotide with another; others involve insertion or deletion of one or a few nucleotides.
