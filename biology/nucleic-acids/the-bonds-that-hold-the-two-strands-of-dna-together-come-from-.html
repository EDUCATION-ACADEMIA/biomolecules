The bonds that hold the two strands of DNA together come from ____

(A) the attraction of phosphate groups

(B) strong bonds between bases and sugars

(C) carbon to carbon bonds in the sugar

(D) ** weak hydrogen bonds between the bases

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -hydrogen. Covalent bonds occur within each linear strand and strongly bond the bases, sugars, and phosphate groups (both within each component and between components). Hydrogen bonds occur between the two strands and involve a base from one strand with a base from the second in complementary pairing.

Concept note-2: -Molecules called nucleotides, on opposite strands of the DNA double helix, that form chemical bonds with one another. These chemical bonds act like rungs in a ladder and help hold the two strands of DNA together. There are four nucleotides, or bases, in DNA: adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G), and thymine (T).

Concept note-3: -The answer is B: hydrogen bonds are easier to break allowing for DNA copying. Hydrogen bonds formed between complementary base pairs on opposite DNA strands form the “rungs on a ladder” portion of DNA’s double-helical structure.

Concept note-4: -DNA is made up of a double-stranded helix held together by weak hydrogen bonds between purine-pyrimidine nucleotide base pairs: adenine (A) paired with thymine (T), and guanine (G) paired with cytosine (C).

Concept note-5: -Weak hydrogen bonds hold together the two strands of DNA to form a double helix. These hydrogen bonds are formed between the nitrogenous bases. Two hydrogen bonds can form between thymine and adenine while three hydrogen bonds can form between guanine and cytosine.
