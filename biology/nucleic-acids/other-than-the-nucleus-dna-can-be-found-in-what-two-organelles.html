Other than the nucleus, DNA can be found in what two organelles?

(A) mitochondria and ribosomes

(B) mitochondria and lysosomes

(C) ** mitochondria and chloroplasts

(D) chloroplasts & ribosomes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The nucleus is particularly important among eukaryotic organelles because it is the location of a cell’s DNA. Two other critical organelles are mitochondria and chloroplasts, which play important roles in energy conversion and are thought to have their evolutionary origins as simple single-celled organisms.

Concept note-2: -The three organelles that contain DNA are the nucleus, mitochondria and chloroplasts. Organelles are membrane-bound subunits within a cell–analogous to organs in the body–that perform specific functions.

Concept note-3: -Most DNA is located in the cell nucleus (where it is called nuclear DNA), but a small amount of DNA can also be found in the mitochondria (where it is called mitochondrial DNA or mtDNA).

Concept note-4: -Reason: Endoplasmic reticulum and Golgi body are the cell organelles which have their own DNA.

Concept note-5: -Final answer: Organelle other than nucleus, containing DNA is mitochondria.
