Which base is not found in RNA?

(A) adenine

(B) cytosine

(C) ** thymine

(D) uracil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Uracil is present only in RNA, whereas thymine is present only in DNA. Thus, thymine is the nitrogenous base which is not present in RNA.

Concept note-2: -In RNA, the base thymine is not found and is instead replaced by a different base called uracil, abbreviated U; the other three bases are present in both DNA and RNA.

Concept note-3: -The bases used in DNA are adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G) and thymine (T). In RNA, the base uracil (U) takes the place of thymine.

Concept note-4: -Uracil is present in RNA but not in DNA.

Concept note-5: -However, since mRNA is relatively short-lived and any potential faults don’t result in any lasting damage, RNA employs uracil because the instability doesn’t matter as much for RNA. Thymine is also quickly oxidized. The thymine in the nucleus is shielded from oxygen.
