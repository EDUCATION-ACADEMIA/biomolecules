True or false:DNA can be coiled around to form structures called chromosomes, in which a particular segment codes for a gene, and the final product for many genes are proteins.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the nucleus of each cell, the DNA molecule is packaged into thread-like structures called chromosomes. Each chromosome is made up of DNA tightly coiled many times around proteins called histones that support its structure.

Concept note-2: -[1] The entire DNA strand must fit within the nucleus of a cell, so it must be very tightly packaged to fit. This is accomplished by wrapping the DNA around structural histone proteins, which act as scaffolding for the DNA to be coiled around.

Concept note-3: -As shown in the animation, a DNA molecule wraps around histone proteins to form tight loops called nucleosomes. These nucleosomes coil and stack together to form fibers called chromatin. Chromatin, in turn, loops and folds with the help of additional proteins to form chromosomes.

Concept note-4: -DNA is wrapped around proteins known as histones to form structures called nucleosomes. This statement is true.
