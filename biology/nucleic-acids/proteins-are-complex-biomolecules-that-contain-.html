NUCLEIC ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Proteins are complex biomolecules that contain ____

(A) sugars

(B) nucleic acids

(C) lipids

(D) ** amino acids

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Proteins are high molecular mass complex biomolecules of amino acids. The important proteins required for our body are enzymes, hormones, antibodies, transport proteins, structural proteins, contractile proteins etc. Except for glycine, all alpha-amino acids have chiral carbon atom and have L-configuration.

Concept note-2: -Proteins can be classified as simple or complex. A simple protein is composed only of amino acids. Complex proteins, which are far more common, incorporate other non-amino acid groups in their structure. Continue and read about some nutrition.

Concept note-3: -Proteins are complex molecules composed of: C) Amino acids. Proteins are one of the important biomolecules. Each protein consists of one or more long chains of amino acids. At least 20 different amino acids may exist in the protein.

Concept note-4: -Both animal and plant proteins are made up of about 20 common amino acids. The proportion of these amino acids varies as a characteristic of a given protein, but all food proteins-with the exception of gelatin-contain some of each.

Concept note-5: -Chromatin is a complex biomolecule, highly dynamic during the cell cycle and development, and essential for numerous cellular processes. The sequential ChIP method is an important component of the technical arsenal of chromatin researchers, as it allows for the study of the co-occurrence of multiple chromatin proteins.
