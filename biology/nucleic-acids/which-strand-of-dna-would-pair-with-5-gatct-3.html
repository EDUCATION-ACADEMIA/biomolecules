Which strand of DNA would pair with 5’-GATCT-3’

(A) 5’-CTAGA-3’

(B) 5’-CUAGA-3’

(C) ** 5’-AGATC-3’

(D) 5’-TCTAG-3’

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The given strand has a sequence 5’-GAATTG-3’. Hence the complementary strand will have a sequence 5’-CAATTC-3’.

Concept note-2: -If one strand of a DNA molecule has the sequence of bases 5’ATTGCA3’, the other complementary, antiparallel strand of DNA would have the sequence: 5’TGCAAT3’.

Concept note-3: -So, the sequence of the complimentary strand in 5’ to 3’ direction is 5’-GCATGCATGCATGCATGCATGCATGCAT− 3’.

Concept note-4: -It synthesizes the RNA strand in the 5’ to 3’ direction, while reading the template DNA strand in the 3’ to 5’ direction. The template DNA strand and RNA strand are antiparallel. RNA polymerase always builds a new RNA strand in the 5’ to 3’ direction.
