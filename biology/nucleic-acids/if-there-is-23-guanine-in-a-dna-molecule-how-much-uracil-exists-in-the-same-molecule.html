If there is 23% Guanine in a DNA molecule, how much Uracil exists in the same molecule?

(A) 23%

(B) 46%

(C) 54%

(D) 27%

(E) ** 0%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer: A DNA molecule with 24% guanine would have 26% adenine.

Concept note-2: -DNA bases bond in pairs with Thymine to Adenine and Guanine to Cytosine. For every one Thymine there will be one Adenine. So, 23% Thymine would mean that there is also 23% Guanine. Guanine would equal 54%/2 or 27%.

Concept note-3: -If a cell contains 20% guanine, it will contain 30% adenine. According to Chargaff’s rules, %G = %C and %A = %T. The bases G and C are complementary and are always equal in amount in double-stranded DNA.

Concept note-4: -Therefore the base percentage of GC is 54% in the whole DNA sequence.
