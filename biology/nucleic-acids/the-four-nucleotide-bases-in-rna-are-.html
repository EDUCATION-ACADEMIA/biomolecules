NUCLEIC ACIDS

[SOURCES]
The four nucleotide bases in RNA are ____

(A) adenine, thymine, guanine, cytosine

(B) cytosine, thymine, adenine, uracil

(C) uracil, thymine, guanine, cytosine

(D) ** adenine, uracil, guanine, cytosine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -RNA consists of four nitrogenous bases: adenine, cytosine, uracil, and guanine. Uracil is a pyrimidine that is structurally similar to the thymine, another pyrimidine that is found in DNA.

Concept note-2: -Nucleotide The bases used in DNA are adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G) and thymine (T). In RNA, the base uracil (U) takes the place of thymine. DNA and RNA molecules are polymers made up of long chains of nucleotides.

Concept note-3: -In DNA, adenine always pairs with thymine (A-T), and guanine always pairs with cytosine (G-C). RNA is the same, except that adenine always pairs with uracil (A-U).

Concept note-4: -Definition. Uracil (U) is one of the four nucleotide bases in RNA, with the other three being adenine (A), cytosine (C) and guanine (G). In RNA, uracil pairs with adenine. In a DNA molecule, the nucleotide thymine (T) is used in place of uracil.
