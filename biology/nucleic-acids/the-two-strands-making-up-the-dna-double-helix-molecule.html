The two strands making up the DNA double helix molecule

(A) contain ribose and deoxyribose in opposite strands.

(B) ** are held together by hydrogen bonds.

(C) are attached through a phosphate to hold the strands together.

(D) contain uracil but not thymine.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Each molecule of DNA is a double helix formed from two complementary strands of nucleotides held together by hydrogen bonds between G-C and A-T base pairs.

Concept note-2: -The two strands are connected by chemical bonds between the bases: adenine bonds with thymine, and cytosine bonds with guanine.

Concept note-3: -The DNA double helix is held together by hydrogen bonds between the bases attached to the two strands. The DNA double helix. The two sides are the sugar-phosphate backbones, composed of alternating phosphate groups and deoxyribose sugars. The nitrogenous bases face the center of the double helix.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The answer is B: hydrogen bonds are easier to break allowing for DNA copying. Hydrogen bonds formed between complementary base pairs on opposite DNA strands form the “rungs on a ladder” portion of DNA’s double-helical structure.
