What is the complementary DNA base to Thymine?

(A) ** Adenine

(B) Cytosine

(C) Guanine

(D) Thymine

(E) Uracil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adenine always bonds with thymine, making them DNA’s other pair of complementary bases. These pairs form the “rungs” of the DNA ladder.

Concept note-2: -Final answer: In DNA the complementary bases are adenine and thymine, guanine, and cytosine.

Concept note-3: -DNA base pair. Under normal circumstances, the nitrogen-containing bases adenine (A) and thymine (T) pair together, and cytosine (C) and guanine (G) pair together.

Concept note-4: -In DNA, adenine always pairs with thymine (A-T), and guanine always pairs with cytosine (G-C).

Concept note-5: -In DNA the complementary bases are adenine and thymine; guanine and cytosine.
