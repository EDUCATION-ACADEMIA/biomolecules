Which of the following is not part of a DNA nucleotide?

(A) ** double helix

(B) five-carbon sugar

(C) nitrogen base

(D) phosphate group

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nucleotides that make the DNA strands contain deoxyribose sugar, phospate molecule and one nitrogenous base out of adenine, guanine, thymine and cytosine. Uracil is never a part of nucleotide of DNA.

Concept note-2: -The double helix describes the appearance of double-stranded DNA, which is composed of two linear strands that run opposite to each other, or anti-parallel, and twist together. Each DNA strand within the double helix is a long, linear molecule made of smaller units called nucleotides that form a chain.

Concept note-3: -DNA double helix does not contain uracil because DNA molecules only have 4 nucleotides, A, G, C and T.

Concept note-4: -DNA is made up of four building blocks called nucleotides: adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), and cytosine (C).

Concept note-5: -Final answer: Uracil is not found in DNA.
