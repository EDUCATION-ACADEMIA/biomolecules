BIOLOGY

NUCLEIC ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Nucleic Acids are ____ ?

(A) ** RNA

(B) bacteria

(C) ** DNA

(D) blood cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nucleic acids, deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) and ribonucleic acid (RNA), carry genetic information which is read in cells to make the RNA and proteins by which living things function. The well-known structure of the DNA double helix allows this information to be copied and passed on to the next generation.

Concept note-2: -The term nucleic acid is the overall name for DNA and RNA, members of a family of biopolymers, and is synonymous with polynucleotide. Nucleic acids were named for their initial discovery within the nucleus, and for the presence of phosphate groups (related to phosphoric acid).

Concept note-3: -Key Points. The two main types of nucleic acids are DNA and RNA. Both DNA and RNA are made from nucleotides, each containing a five-carbon sugar backbone, a phosphate group, and a nitrogen base.

Concept note-4: -As you already know, the letters in DNA stand for “Deoxyribonucleic Acid.” DNA is a polymer made up of monomers called nucleic acids, which are linked together in long chains. Each nucleic acid monomer is made up of a sugar (deoxyribose in DNA), a nitrogenous base, and a phosphate group.

Concept note-5: -Ribonucleic acid (abbreviated RNA) is a nucleic acid present in all living cells that has structural similarities to DNA. Unlike DNA, however, RNA is most often single-stranded. An RNA molecule has a backbone made of alternating phosphate groups and the sugar ribose, rather than the deoxyribose found in DNA.
