NUCLEIC ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Which organic compounds necessary for life are formed by a sugar bonded to a phosphate group and a nitrogenous base?

(A) lipids

(B) proteins

(C) ** nucleic acids

(D) carbohydrates

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nucleotides are organic molecules composed of a nitrogenous base, a pentose sugar and a phosphate. They serve as monomeric units of the nucleic acid polymers – deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) and ribonucleic acid (RNA), both of which are essential biomolecules within all life-forms on Earth.

Concept note-2: -Nucleotide A nucleotide consists of a sugar molecule (either ribose in RNA or deoxyribose in DNA) attached to a phosphate group and a nitrogen-containing base. The bases used in DNA are adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G) and thymine (T).

Concept note-3: -A molecule consisting of a nitrogen-containing base (adenine, guanine, thymine, or cytosine in DNA; adenine, guanine, uracil, or cytosine in RNA), a phosphate group, and a sugar (deoxyribose in DNA; ribose in RNA).

Concept note-4: -Nucleotide-A small organic molecule consisting of a five-carbon sugar bonded to a nitrogenous base and a phosphate group. Nucleotides (adenine, guanine, cytosine, thymine, and uracil) are the monomers which make up DNA and RNA.

Concept note-5: -DNA consists of a pair of chains of a sugar-phosphate backbone linked by pyrimidine and purine bases to form adouble helix (Fig. 96.1). The sugar in DNA is deoxyribose.
