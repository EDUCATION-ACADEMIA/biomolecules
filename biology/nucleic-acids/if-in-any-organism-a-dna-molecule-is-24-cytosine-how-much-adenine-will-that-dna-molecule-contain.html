If in any organism a DNA molecule is 24% cytosine, how much adenine will that DNA molecule contain?

(A) 24%

(B) ** 26%

(C) 48%

(D) 52%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer: A DNA molecule with 24% guanine would have 26% adenine. The rules for complementary DNA base pairing are that adenine pairs with thymine and guanine pairs with cytosine.

Concept note-2: -Therefore, given 27 percent of thymine, there is also 27 percent of adenine. Thus, the amount of guanine and cytosine would have 100-27(2)= 46, which follows that there is 23 percent of cytosine (46/2).

Concept note-3: -Since adenine is 20%, then thymine is 20% as well. The total of both is 40%. From 100 remains 60% which is divided equally between guanine and cytosine, so each is 30%.

Concept note-4: -The percentage of guanine is 20%.

Concept note-5: -Answer: There would be 20% Cytosine. Adenine (30%) = Thymine (30%) Guanine (20%) = Cytosine (20%)
