Which is a single stranded molecule?

(A) DNA

(B) ** RNA

(C) Both

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -However, unlike DNA, RNA is usually a single-stranded molecule. Also, the sugar in RNA is ribose instead of deoxyribose (ribose contains one more hydroxyl group on the second carbon), which accounts for the moleculeâ€™s name. RNA consists of four nitrogenous bases : adenine, cytosine, uracil, and guanine.

Concept note-2: -The chemical structure of RNA is very similar to that of DNA, but differs in three primary ways: Unlike double-stranded DNA, RNA is usually a single-stranded molecule (ssRNA) in many of its biological roles and consists of much shorter chains of nucleotides.

Concept note-3: -Whereas DNA always occurs in cells as a double-stranded helix, RNA is single-stranded. RNA chains therefore fold up into a variety of shapes, just as a polypeptide chain folds up to form the final shape of a protein (Figure 6-6).

Concept note-4: -There are two reasons why RNA is generally single-stranded: RNAses are extremely abundant in most cells. Usually, double-stranded RNA is associated with a viral infection and is destroyed rapidly. Double-stranded RNA is not as stable as double-stranded DNA.

Concept note-5: -The nitrogenous bases include adenine, guanine, uracil, and cytosine. RNA mostly exists in the single-stranded form, but there are special RNA viruses that are double-stranded.
