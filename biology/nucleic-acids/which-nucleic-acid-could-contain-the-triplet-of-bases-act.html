Which nucleic acid, could contain the triplet of bases ACT?

(A) ** DNA

(B) mRNA

(C) tRNA

(D) rRNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which nucleic acid could contain the triplet of bases ACT? RNA (no matter what the type) doesnâ€™t contain the base T (thymine). Only DNA contains this base.

Concept note-2: -A codon is a triplet of bases (or nucleotides) in the DNA coding for one amino acid. The relation between codons and amino acids is given by the genetic code.

Concept note-3: -There are four nucleotides, or bases, in DNA: adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G), and thymine (T).

Concept note-4: -The purine bases adenine and guanine and pyrimidine base cytosine occur in both DNA and RNA, while the pyrimidine bases thymine (in DNA) and uracil (in RNA) occur in just one. Adenine forms a base pair with thymine with two hydrogen bonds, while guanine pairs with cytosine with three hydrogen bonds.
