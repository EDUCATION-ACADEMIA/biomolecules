When coiled together, DNA looks like a:

(A) Single Helix

(B) ** Double Helix

(C) Triple Helix

(D) Broken Ladder

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Double helix, as related to genomics, is a term used to describe the physical structure of DNA. A DNA molecule is made up of two linked strands that wind around each other to resemble a twisted ladder in a helix-like shape. Each strand has a backbone made of alternating sugar (deoxyribose) and phosphate groups.

Concept note-2: -To avoid bumping into each other, the staircase has to twist a little bit. This turns our staircase into a spiral staircase. This extra twist at the end is the reason for the helical shape. For the “steps” of the DNA “staircase” to fit together, they have to twist a little bit, making the final spiral shape of DNA.

Concept note-3: -As per the Watson-Crick double helical model, DNA is composed of two complementary polynucleotide chains which are anti-parallel to each other. Both the strands of DNA are held together by Hydrogen bonds and coiled around a common axis.

Concept note-4: -(The Double Helix) The model is called a double helix because two long strands twist around each other like a twisted ladder. The rails of the ladder are made of alternating sugar and phosphate molecules. The steps of the ladder are made of two bases joined together with either two or three weak hydrogen bonds.
