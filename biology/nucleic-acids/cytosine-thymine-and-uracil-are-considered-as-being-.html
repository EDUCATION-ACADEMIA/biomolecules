[SOURCES]
Cytosine, Thymine, and Uracil are considered as being ____

(A) Purines

(B) ** Pyramidines

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pyrimidine: A nitrogenous base similar to benzene (a six-membered ring) and includes cytosine, thymine, and uracil as bases used for DNA or RNA.

Concept note-2: -One of two chemical compounds that cells use to make the building blocks of DNA and RNA. Examples of pyrimidines are cytosine, thymine, and uracil.

Concept note-3: -The purine nitrogenous bases are characterized by their single amino group (−NH 2), at the C6 carbon in adenine and C2 in guanine. Similarly, the simple-ring structure of cytosine, uracil, and thymine is derived of pyrimidine, so those three bases are called the pyrimidine bases.

Concept note-4: -Cytosine and thymine are two types of nitrogenous bases in nucleotides, which build nucleic acids. The other nitrogenous bases found in nucleic acids are adenine, guanine, and uracil. Uracil is only found in RNA and is involved in protein synthesis.
