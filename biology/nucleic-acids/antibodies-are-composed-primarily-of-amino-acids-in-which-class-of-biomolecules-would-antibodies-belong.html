Antibodies are composed primarily of amino acids. In which class of biomolecules would antibodies belong?

(A) Nucleic acids

(B) Carbohydrates

(C) ** Proteins

(D) Lipids

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1 Answer. Antibodies are proteins. An antibody (Ab), also known as an immunoglobulin (Ig), is a large Y-shape protein produced by plasma cells that is used by the immune system to identify and neutralize foreign objects such as bacteria and viruses.

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: Antibodies are b. protein macromolecules. Antibodies, also referred to as immunoglobulins, are released by immune cells to identify and neutralize pathogens in the body.

Concept note-3: -What are antibodies made of? Antibodies are proteins. Each antibody has four polypeptides (peptides that consist of two or more amino acids), including two heavy chains and two light chains.
