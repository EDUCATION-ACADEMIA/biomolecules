NUCLEIC ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Unsaturated fats:

(A) ** Are liquid at room temperature

(B) ** Originate from plant sources

(C) ** Do not lead to the production of cholesterol

(D) Originate from animal sources

(E) Lead to the production of cholesterol, which may lead to plaque buildup in the arteries

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Studies show that eating foods rich in unsaturated fat instead of saturated fat improves blood cholesterol levels, which can decrease your risk of heart attack and stroke.

Concept note-2: -Replacement of saturated by polyunsaturated fatty acids in the diet may lower serum very low-density and low-density lipoprotein concentrations because the liver preferentially converts polyunsaturated fatty acids into ketone bodies instead of into very low-density lipoprotein triglycerides.

Concept note-3: -Trans fats are created through hydrogenation, a process that takes oil that is liquid at room temperature (unsaturated) and turns it into solid fat (saturated).

Concept note-4: -Dietary unsaturated fat increases HDL metabolic pathways involving apoE favorable to reverse cholesterol transport.
