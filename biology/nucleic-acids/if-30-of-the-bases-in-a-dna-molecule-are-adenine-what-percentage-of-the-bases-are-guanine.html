If 30% of the bases in a DNA molecule are adenine, what percentage of the bases are guanine?

(A) ** 20%

(B) 30%

(C) 35%

(D) 70%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The percentage of guanine is 20%.

Concept note-2: -Adenine (30%) = Thymine (30%) Guanine (20%) = Cytosine (20%) Therefore, 60% A-T and 40% C-G.

Concept note-3: -Option A is correct ans Thymine −30%, Guanine −20%, Cytosine −20% (Chargaffs nitrogen base pairing rule which states that amount of Adenine is equal to Thymine and Guanine is equal to Cytosine.

Concept note-4: -Thymine – 30%, Guanine – 20%, Cytosine – 20%. Chargaff’s nitrogen base pairing rule which states that amount of adenine is equal to thymine and guanine is equal to cytosine.

Concept note-5: -Hence, the sum of the purine residues equals the sum of the pyrimidine residues; i.e. A + G = T + C. Here, [A] = 30%, therefore, % of [T] is also 30%. Thus, [G]+[C] = 100-60 = 40%. [G] = 20% and [C] = 20%.
