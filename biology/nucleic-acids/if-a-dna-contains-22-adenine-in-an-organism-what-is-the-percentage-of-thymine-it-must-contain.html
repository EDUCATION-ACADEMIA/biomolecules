If a DNA contains 22% adenine in an organism, what is the percentage of thymine it must contain?

(A) ** 22%

(B) 28%

(C) 44%

(D) 56%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The sum of all four percentages must equal 100%. We know that the sample is 22% adenine; this tells us it is also 22% thymine. Since cytosine and guanine are present in equal amounts, we can simply divide their sum by 2. The final composition is 22% adenine, 22% thymine, 28% cytosine, and 28% guanine.

Concept note-2: -In a DNA sample, the percentage of adenine is 40% and percentage of thymine is 60%.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: A double-stranded DNA molecule with 20% thymine would contain 30% guanine.

Concept note-4: -Thymine (T) makes up 28% of the nucleotides in a sample of DNA from an organism.
