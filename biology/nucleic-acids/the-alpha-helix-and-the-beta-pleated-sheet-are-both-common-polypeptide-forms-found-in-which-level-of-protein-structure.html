The α helix and the β pleated sheet are both common polypeptide forms found in which level of protein structure?

(A) Primary

(B) ** Secondary

(C) Tertiary

(D) Quaternary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, the correct answer is ‘Secondary structure’.

Concept note-2: -Secondary structure The most common types of secondary structures are the helix and the pleated sheet. Both structures are held in shape by hydrogen bonds, which form between the carbonyl O of one amino acid and the amino H of another.

Concept note-3: -In the alpha helix, the bonds form between every fourth amino acid and cause a twist in the amino acid chain. In the -pleated sheet, the “pleats” are formed by hydrogen bonding between atoms on the backbone of the polypeptide chain.

Concept note-4: -The secondary structure consists of local packing of polypeptide chain into -helices and -sheets due to hydrogen bonds between peptide bond – central carbon backbone.

Concept note-5: -An -helix secondary structure is stabilized by hydrogen bonds between carbonyl oxygen and the amino group of every third residue in the helical turn with each helical turn consisting of 3.6 amino acid residues (Fig. 10.1A). The side chain of amino acids is projected outward from the outer helical surface.
