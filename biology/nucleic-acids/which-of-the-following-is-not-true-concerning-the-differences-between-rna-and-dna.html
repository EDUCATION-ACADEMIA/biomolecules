Which of the following is not true concerning the differences between RNA and DNA?

(A) ** RNA contains pyrimidines while DNA contains purines.

(B) The sugar in DNA contains one less oxygen than the sugar in RNA.

(C) DNA forms a double helix while RNA is able to fold in on itself.

(D) RNA is thought to have evolved before DNA.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer is: (c) DNA contains alternating sugar-phosphate molecules whereas RNA does not contain sugars.

Concept note-2: -The purines in DNA are adenine and guanine, the same as in RNA. The pyrimidines in DNA are cytosine and thymine; in RNA, they are cytosine and uracil. Purines are larger than pyrimidines because they have a two-ring structure while pyrimidines only have a single ring.

Concept note-3: -Thus, the major difference between DNA and RNA is that DNA is double-stranded and RNA is single-stranded. DNA is responsible for genetic information transmission, whereas RNA transmits genetic codes that are necessary for protein creation.

Concept note-4: -Purines. Adenine and guanine are found in both DNA and RNA.
