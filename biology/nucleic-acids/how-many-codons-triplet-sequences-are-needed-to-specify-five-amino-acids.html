How many codons (triplet sequences) are needed to specify five amino acids?

(A) ** 5

(B) 10

(C) 3

(D) 12

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -One amino acid codes triplet three nucleotide. So, after addition of adenosine residue the total number of nucleotide is 16. So five amino acids will be coded by the mRNA sequence.

Concept note-2: -Amino acids of a polypeptide A polypeptide of 5 amino acids is analogous to a chain with five links. It has two end amino acids, and three internal links. Breaking our chain into smaller pieces is similar to cutting links of a chain and producing smaller chains.

Concept note-3: -Hence, the correct answer is ‘Two amino acids’.

Concept note-4: -Figure 1: In mRNA, three-nucleotide units called codons dictate a particular amino acid.

Concept note-5: -Multiple codons can code for the same amino acid. The codons are written 5’ to 3’, as they appear in the mRNA.
