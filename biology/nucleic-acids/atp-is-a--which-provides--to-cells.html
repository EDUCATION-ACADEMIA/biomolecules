ATP is a ____ which provides ____ to cells

(A) ** Nucleic Acid / Energy

(B) Protein / Energy

(C) Nucleic Acid / Support

(D) Protein / Support

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adenosine triphosphate (ATP) is the source of energy for use and storage at the cellular level. The structure of ATP is a nucleoside triphosphate, consisting of a nitrogenous base (adenine), a ribose sugar, and three serially bonded phosphate groups.

Concept note-2: -The nucleic acid adenosine triphosphate (ATP), made up of an adenine nitrogenous base, a 5-carbon ribose sugar, and three phosphate groups, is involved in generating energy for cellular processes.

Concept note-3: -ATP synthesized in mitochondria is the primary energy source for important biological functions, such as muscle contraction, nerve impulse transmission, and protein synthesis.

Concept note-4: -ATP or Adenosine triphosphate is a nucleotide. Nucleotides are phosphoric acid esters of nucleosides. Adenosine is a nucleoside made up of ribose sugar and adenine base whereas adenosine triphosphate is a nucleotide with three phosphoric acid molecules attached to one molecule of adenosine.
