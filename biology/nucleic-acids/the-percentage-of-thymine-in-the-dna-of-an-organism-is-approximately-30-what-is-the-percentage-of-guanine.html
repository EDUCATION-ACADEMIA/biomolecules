The percentage of thymine in the DNA of an organism is approximately 30%. What is the percentage of guanine?

(A) 70%

(B) 30%

(C) 40%

(D) ** 20%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Thus, in the DNA of an organism, if guanine is 20% cytosine also will be 20%. So, both adenine and thymine together will be 60%, i.e. 30% adenine and 30% thymine. Q. In DNA of certain organisms, guanine constitutes 20% of the bases.

Concept note-2: -So, for this DNA sequence, C + G = 60%. The remaining 40% of the sequence must consist of A-T pairs (adenine-thymine). Therefore, 20% of the sequence is made up of adenine, and 20% of the sequence is made up of thymine.

Concept note-3: -Thymine – 30%, Guanine – 20%, Cytosine – 20%. Chargaff’s nitrogen base pairing rule which states that amount of adenine is equal to thymine and guanine is equal to cytosine.

Concept note-4: -If a double stranded DNA has 20% Thymine, the percentage of Guanine in the DNA. No worries!
