In the double helix structure of nucleic acids, cytosine hydrogen bonds to:

(A) Deoxyribose

(B) Adenine

(C) Thymine

(D) ** Guanine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adenine and thymine are connected by two hydrogen bonds, and cytosine and guanine are connected by three hydrogen bonds.

Concept note-2: -Note: Cytosine can form three hydrogen bonds with guanine, and adenine can form two hydrogen bonds with thymine. In simple words, C bonds with G and A bonds with T. This is called complementary base pairing because each base can only bond with a specific base partner.

Concept note-3: -It is a truth universally acknowledged that a guanine–cytosine (GC) base pair has three hydrogen bonds whereas adenine–thymine (AT) has two.

Concept note-4: -hydrogen bonds. Q. STATEMENT-1 : Guanine unites with cytosine with three hydrogen bonds but it forms only one hydrogen bond with thymine. STATEMENT-2 : Guanine and cytosine are purines while that of thymine is a pyrimidine.
