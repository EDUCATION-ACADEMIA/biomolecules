Which of the following is NOT one of the three groups comprising the nucleotide?

(A) ** R group

(B) phosphate

(C) nitrogenous base

(D) pentose sugar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nucleotides that make the DNA strands contain deoxyribose sugar, phospate molecule and one nitrogenous base out of adenine, guanine, thymine and cytosine. Uracil is never a part of nucleotide of DNA. It is present in RNA.

Concept note-2: -How Nucleotides Are Constructed. A nucleotide consists of base, sugar, and phosphate group.

Concept note-3: -A molecule consisting of a nitrogen-containing base (adenine, guanine, thymine, or cytosine in DNA; adenine, guanine, uracil, or cytosine in RNA), a phosphate group, and a sugar (deoxyribose in DNA; ribose in RNA).

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer is C. Amino acid. An amino acid is not a component of nucleotide.

Concept note-5: -The three components of a nucleotide are nitrogenous base, sugar and phosphate.
