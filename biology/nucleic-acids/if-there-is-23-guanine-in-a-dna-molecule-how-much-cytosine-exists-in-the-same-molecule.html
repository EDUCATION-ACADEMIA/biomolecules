If there is 23% Guanine in a DNA molecule, how much Cytosine exists in the same molecule?

(A) ** 23%

(B) 46%

(C) 54%

(D) 27%

(E) 0%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer choice is b. If the DNA of a species has 23% cytosine, there also will be 23% of the complementary base, guanine. Thymine and its complementary base, adenine, will each make up 27%.

Concept note-2: -Therefore the base percentage of GC is 54% in the whole DNA sequence.

Concept note-3: -So, the correct option is ‘27%’.

Concept note-4: -DNA bases bond in pairs with Thymine to Adenine and Guanine to Cytosine. For every one Thymine there will be one Adenine. So, 23% Thymine would mean that there is also 23% Guanine. Guanine would equal 54%/2 or 27%.
