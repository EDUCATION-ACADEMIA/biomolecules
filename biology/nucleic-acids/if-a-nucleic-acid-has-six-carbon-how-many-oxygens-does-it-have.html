If a nucleic acid has six carbon, how many oxygen’s does it have?

(A) 6

(B) 9

(C) 12

(D) ** cannot be determined

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 5-carbon sugar (known as a pentose) includes ribose and deoxyribose, which are present in nucleic acid. Both ribose and deoxyribose have five carbon atoms and one oxygen atom.

Concept note-2: -A nucleotide is composed of a nitrogenous base, and a phosphate group, but is not composed of a six carbon sugar.

Concept note-3: -The term “deoxy” refers to the fact that there is no oxygen attached to the 2’ carbon atom. There is a molecule that is similar to DNA but has an oxygen atom at this position. Its sugar is therefore called ribose and its name is ribonucleic acid or RNA. The base group is attached to the 1’ carbon.

Concept note-4: -A nucleotide is composed of three distinctive chemical sub-units: a five-carbon sugar molecule, a nucleobase (the two of which together are called a nucleoside), and one phosphate group.
