Which nucleic acid is being described:double stranded, compared to as a stair case, antiparallel, and complementary base pairs.

(A) RNA

(B) ** DNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The DNA Double Helix. The DNA double helix looks like a twisted staircase, with the sugar and phosphate backbone surrounding complementary nitrogen bases.

Concept note-2: -Deoxyribonucleic acid (abbreviated DNA) is the molecule that carries genetic information for the development and functioning of an organism. DNA is made of two linked strands that wind around each other to resemble a twisted ladder-a shape known as a double helix.

Concept note-3: -1: Antiparallel Strands: In a double stranded DNA molecule, the two strands run antiparallel to one another so one is upside down compared to the other. The phosphate backbone is located on the outside, and the bases are in the middle.

Concept note-4: -Double-stranded DNA is an antiparallel molecule, meaning that it’s composed of two strands that run alongside each other but point in opposite directions. In a double-stranded DNA molecule, the 5’ end (phosphate-bearing end) of one strand aligns with the 3’ end (hydroxyl-bearing end) of its partner, and vice versa.

Concept note-5: -One strand of DNA run from 5’ to 3’ end while other run from 3’ to 5’ end, thus antiparallel in nature. The DNA strands are complementary to each other because of the nitrogenous bases as A only binds to T and C only binds to G.
