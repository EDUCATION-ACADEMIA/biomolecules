Which nitrogen bases are considered purines and which are considered pyrimidines?

(A) A and T are pyrimidines, G, U, and C are purines

(B) ** A and G are purines, T, C, and U are pyrimidines

(C) A, U, and C are purines, T and G are pyrimidines

(D) A and G are pyrimidines, T, U, and C are purines

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Purines (adenine and guanine) are two-carbon nitrogen ring bases while pyrimidines (cytosine and thymine) are one-carbon nitrogen ring bases.

Concept note-2: -The purines in DNA are adenine and guanine, the same as in RNA. The pyrimidines in DNA are cytosine and thymine; in RNA, they are cytosine and uracil.

Concept note-3: -Adenine (A) and guanine (G) are purines, and cytosine (C), thymine (T), and uracil (U) are pyrimidines.

Concept note-4: -The purine bases are guanine (G) and adenine (A) which form corresponding nucleosides-deoxyribonucleosides (deoxyguanosine and deoxyadenosine) with deoxyribose moiety and ribonucleosides (guanosine, adenosine) with ribose moiety.
