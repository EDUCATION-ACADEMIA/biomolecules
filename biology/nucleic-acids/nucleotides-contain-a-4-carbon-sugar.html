NUCLEIC ACIDS

[SOURCES]
Nucleotides contain a 4 carbon sugar.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A nucleotide consists of 3 subunits namely sugar, nitrogenous base and phosphate group. Sugar in the nucleotide can be either ribose or deoxyribose. Both these are five carbon sugars.

Concept note-2: -A nucleotide is composed of three distinctive chemical sub-units: a five-carbon sugar molecule, a nucleobase (the two of which together are called a nucleoside), and one phosphate group.

Concept note-3: -Each nucleotide is composed of a nitrogenous base, a five-carbon sugar (deoxyribose), and a phosphate group.

Concept note-4: -Each nucleotide is made up of three parts: a nitrogen-containing ring structure called a nitrogenous base, a five-carbon sugar, and at least one phosphate group.
