Which of the following is the correct base paring rule for RNA?

(A) ** A pairs with U, and C pairs with G

(B) A pairs with G, and T pairs with C

(C) A pairs with T, and C pairs with G

(D) A pairs with C, and G pairs with T

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The rules of base pairing (or nucleotide pairing) are: A with T: the purine adenine (A) always pairs with the pyrimidine thymine (T) C with G: the pyrimidine cytosine (C) always pairs with the purine guanine (G)

Concept note-2: -Definition. A unit of two bases in a molecule of DNA or RNA. In DNA, adenine always pairs with thymine (A-T), and guanine always pairs with cytosine (G-C). RNA is the same, except that adenine always pairs with uracil (A-U).

Concept note-3: -G·U pairs have now been found in virtually every class of functional RNA, and have been shown to play many essential roles that are based upon the unique chemical and structural properties of the wobble pair.

Concept note-4: -Uracil (U) is one of the four nucleotide bases in RNA, with the other three being adenine (A), cytosine (C) and guanine (G). In RNA, uracil pairs with adenine. In a DNA molecule, the nucleotide thymine (T) is used in place of uracil.

Concept note-5: -The base pairing of guanine (G) and cytosine (C) is just the same in DNA and RNA. So in RNA the important base pairs are: adenine (A) pairs with uracil (U); guanine (G) pairs with cytosine (C).
