The amount of adenine is always equal to the amount of ____

(A) amino acid

(B) ** thymine

(C) cytosine

(D) guanine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chargaff’s rules states that DNA from any cell of all organisms should have a 1:1 ratio of pyrimidine and purine bases and, more specifically, that the amount of guanine is equal to cytosine and the amount of adenine is equal to thymine.

Concept note-2: -This is because adenine will always pair with thymine, so there will be just as many thymine bases as adenine bases. Together, adenine and thymine compose 70% of the segment. This means that 30% of the section is composed of guanine-cytosine pairs.

Concept note-3: -Chargaff’s rules state that in the DNA of any species and any organism, the amount of guanine should be equal to the amount of cytosine and the amount of adenine should be equal to the amount of thymine. Further a 1:1 stoichiometric ratio of purine and pyrimidine bases (i.e., A+G=T+C ) should exist.

Concept note-4: -Erwin Chargaff found that in DNA, the ratios of adenine (A) to thymine (T) and guanine (G) to cytosine (C) are equal.
