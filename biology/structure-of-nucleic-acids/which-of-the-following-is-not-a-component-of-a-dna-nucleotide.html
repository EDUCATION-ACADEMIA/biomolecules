Which of the following is NOT a component of a DNA nucleotide?

(A) phosphate

(B) nitrogen base

(C) deoxyribose sugar

(D) ** ribose sugar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ribose sugar is not a component of DNA. RNA constitutes ribose sugar which is a carbon sugar and is a monosaccharide. DNA consists of deoxyribose sugar which is linked to a nitrogenous base at the first carbon through a glycosidic bond. It is a pentose sugar.

Concept note-2: -Nucleotides that make the DNA strands contain deoxyribose sugar, phospate molecule and one nitrogenous base out of adenine, guanine, thymine and cytosine. Uracil is never a part of nucleotide of DNA.

Concept note-3: -Sugar. Both DNA and RNA are built with a sugar backbone, but whereas the sugar in DNA is called deoxyribose (left in image), the sugar in RNA is called simply ribose (right in image).

Concept note-4: -Nucleotide A nucleotide consists of a sugar molecule (either ribose in RNA or deoxyribose in DNA) attached to a phosphate group and a nitrogen-containing base. The bases used in DNA are adenine (A), cytosine (C), guanine (G) and thymine (T).

Concept note-5: -DNA is made up of four building blocks called nucleotides: adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), and cytosine (C). The nucleotides attach to each other (A with T, and G with C) to form chemical bonds called base pairs, which connect the two DNA strands.
