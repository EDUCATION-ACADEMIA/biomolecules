IMPORTANCE OF CARBOHYDRATES

[SOURCES]
What do organic chemists study

(A) water

(B) ** carbon compounds

(C) optics

(D) anatomy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Organic chemistry is the study of the structure, properties, composition, reactions, and preparation of carbon-containing compounds. Most organic compounds contain carbon and hydrogen, but they may also include any number of other elements (e.g., nitrogen, oxygen, halogens, phosphorus, silicon, sulfur).

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: The main reason is that almost all life forms that are found on earth are carbon-based, meaning that the majority of their body is made of carbon compounds, therefore the study of organic chemistry is also considered the study of carbon compounds.

Concept note-3: -The range of chemicals studied in organic chemistry includes hydrocarbons (compounds containing only carbon and hydrogen) as well as compounds based on carbon, but also containing other elements, especially oxygen, nitrogen, sulfur, phosphorus (included in many biochemicals) and the halogens.

Concept note-4: -The answer is organic chemistry. Organic chemistry is the branch of chemistry that studies the different possible reactions, properties, analysis, and laboratory techniques involved in organic compounds.
