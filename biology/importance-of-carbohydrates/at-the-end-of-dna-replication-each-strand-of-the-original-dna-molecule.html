IMPORTANCE OF CARBOHYDRATES

[SOURCES]
At the end of DNA replication, each strand of the original DNA molecule:

(A) has been altered

(B) ** remains intact

(C) has been tripled

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a round of replication, each of the two strands of DNA is used as a template for the formation of a complementary DNA strand. The original strands therefore remain intact through many cell generations.

Concept note-2: -DNA replication finishes when converging replication forks meet. During this process, called replication termination, DNA synthesis is completed, the replication machinery is disassembled and daughter molecules are resolved.

Concept note-3: -Following replication the new DNA automatically winds up into a double helix.

Concept note-4: -When DNA replicates, each strand of the original DNA molecule is used as a template for the synthesis of a second, complementary strand.

Concept note-5: -Repetitive regions at the very ends of chromosomes are called telomeres, and they’re found in a wide range of eukaryotic species, from human beings to unicellular protists. Telomeres act as caps that protect the internal regions of the chromosomes, and they’re worn down a small amount in each round of DNA replication.
