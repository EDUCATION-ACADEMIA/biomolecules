What are lipids mostly made of (monomers)?

(A) Sugar and Honey

(B) ** Glycerol and Fatty Acids

(C) Water and Salt

(D) Oxygen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fats are a class of lipids containing two kinds of monomers, fatty acids, and glycerol. Glycerol is a three-carbon biomolecule containing three hydroxyl groups, one bonded to each carbon atom. Dehydration synthesis creates an ester linkage between the carboxyl group of fatty acids and a hydroxyl group in glycerol.

Concept note-2: -Glycerol and fatty acids are the monomers that make up lipids.

Concept note-3: -Complete answer: A lipid is a polymer composed of three fatty acid molecules and one glycerol molecule.

Concept note-4: -One major class of lipids is called glycerolipids. Glycerolipids are composed of glycerol and fatty acids.

Concept note-5: -Lipids are an essential component of the cell membrane. The structure is typically made of a glycerol backbone, 2 fatty acid tails (hydrophobic), and a phosphate group (hydrophilic). As such, phospholipids are amphipathic.
