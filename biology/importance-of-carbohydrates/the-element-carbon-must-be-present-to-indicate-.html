The element carbon must be present to indicate ____

(A) an inorganic molecule

(B) it is a rock

(C) ** an organic molecule

(D) air

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Organic molecules are molecules that are made of carbon and hydrogen, and can include other elements. Organic molecules must contain carbon atoms covalently bonded to hydrogen atoms (C-H bonds). They usually involve oxygen and can also contain nitrogen, sulfur, phosphorous, and others.

Concept note-2: -The reason is carbonâ€™s ability to form stable bonds with many elements, including itself. This property allows carbon to form a huge variety of very large and complex molecules. In fact, there are nearly 10 million carbon-based compounds in living things!

Concept note-3: -This means that all organic compounds have in common the presence of carbon atoms and hydrogen atoms. In addition, different organic compounds may contain oxygen, nitrogen, phosphorous, and other elements. Carbon dioxide (CO2) does not have hydrogen; then, it is not an organic compound.

Concept note-4: -Hydrocarbons are organic molecules consisting entirely of carbon and hydrogen.

Concept note-5: -Scientists generally define a molecule as organic when it contains not only carbon, but also at least one other element. Typically, that element is hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen or sulfur.
