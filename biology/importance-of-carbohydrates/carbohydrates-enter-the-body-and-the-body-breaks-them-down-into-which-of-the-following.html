IMPORTANCE OF CARBOHYDRATES

[SOURCES]
Carbohydrates enter the body, and the body breaks them down into which of the following?

(A) ** A:Simple sugars

(B) B:Complex sugars

(C) C:Simple starches

(D) D:Complex starches

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your digestive system breaks down carbs into glucose or blood sugar. Your bloodstream absorbs glucose and uses it as energy to fuel your body. The amount of carbs you consume affects blood sugar. Taking in a lot of carbs can raise blood sugar levels.

Concept note-2: -When you eat carbs, your body breaks them down into simple sugars, which are absorbed into the bloodstream. As the sugar level rises in your body, the pancreas releases a hormone called insulin. Insulin is needed to move sugar from the blood into the cells, where the sugar can be used as a source of energy.

Concept note-3: -Digestion of Carbohydrates Digestion of starches into glucose molecules starts in the mouth, but primarily takes place in the small intestine by the action of specific enzymes secreted from the pancreas (e.g. -amylase and -glucosidase).

Concept note-4: -The small intestine, pancreas, and liver From there, the wall of the small intestine begins to make lactase, sucrase, and maltase. These enzymes break down the sugars even further into monosaccharides or single sugars. These sugars are the ones that are finally absorbed into the small intestine.

Concept note-5: -The digestive tract begins to break down carbohydrates into glucose, which is used for energy, upon consumption. Any extra glucose in the bloodstream is stored in the liver and muscle tissue until further energy is needed.
