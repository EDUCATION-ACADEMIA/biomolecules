The complete set of reactions carried out by an organism is called ____

(A) ** metabolism

(B) catabolism

(C) anabolism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -metabolism, the sum of the chemical reactions that take place within each cell of a living organism and that provide energy for vital processes and for synthesizing new organic material.

Concept note-2: -What Is Metabolism? Metabolism (pronounced: meh-TAB-uh-liz-um) is the chemical reactions in the body’s cells that change food into energy. Our bodies need this energy to do everything from moving to thinking to growing. Specific proteins in the body control the chemical reactions of metabolism.

Concept note-3: -(meh-TA-buh-lih-zum) The chemical changes that take place in a cell or an organism. These changes make energy and the materials cells and organisms need to grow, reproduce, and stay healthy. Metabolism also helps get rid of toxic substances.

Concept note-4: -Anabolism and catabolism Two types of metabolic reactions take place in the cell: ‘building up’ (anabolism) and ‘breaking down’ (catabolism).

Concept note-5: -Catabolism. Catabolism is the set of metabolic processes that break down large molecules. These include breaking down and oxidizing food molecules.
