Which is insoluble in cold water

(A) Glucose

(B) ** Starch

(C) Both of the above

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amylopectin from normal potato starch has a double-helix conformation, which does not readily dissolve in cold water [7].

Concept note-2: -Starches are insoluble in neutral water at room temperature. However, if they are heated beyond the boiling point of water in a closed container, they eventually dissolve.

Concept note-3: -Water insoluble component of starch is amylopectin. Water soluble component of starch is amylose.

Concept note-4: -The amylose is readily soluble in cold water in a matter of seconds.

Concept note-5: -When ethanol is added, which is less polar than water, it lowers the solubility and potential hydrogen bonds of the starch, because the water is more attracted to the alcohol than the starch, the starch is dehydrated by the alcohol and precipitates.
